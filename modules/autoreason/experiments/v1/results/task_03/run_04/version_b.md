# HYBRID WORK POLICY (REVISED)
## A Practical Framework for Distributed Teams

**Effective Date:** [Date]  
**Policy Owner:** CEO  
**Review Cycle:** Quarterly for first year, then annually

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a simplified hybrid work model with clear, enforceable standards. Rather than trying to optimize for all scenarios, it creates predictable patterns that support our core business functions while reducing administrative complexity.

---

## 1. WORK LOCATION REQUIREMENTS

### Simplified Structure
**All employees:** 2 days per week in office, 3 days remote
- **Fixed office days:** Tuesday and Wednesday for all employees
- **Remote days:** Monday, Thursday, Friday

**Department Modifications:**
- **EU Team (3 employees):** Fully remote with quarterly US office visits
- **New hires:** First 60 days require Monday-Wednesday in office

*FIXES: Eliminates anchor day scheduling conflicts, ensures meaningful in-person collaboration time, removes complex department-specific rules that created administrative chaos*

### Quarterly Exceptions Process
- Employees can request modified schedules for specific quarters
- Requires manager approval with business justification
- Maximum 25% of department can have exceptions simultaneously
- Medical accommodations processed separately through HR

*FIXES: Provides flexibility without daily scheduling chaos, creates clear approval process for conflicts*

---

## 2. MEETING AND COLLABORATION STANDARDS

### Default Meeting Approach
**Tuesday-Wednesday (In-Office Days):**
- High-bandwidth collaboration: architecture reviews, strategic planning, complex problem-solving
- All-hands meetings scheduled only on these days
- Client meetings requiring multiple team members

**Monday/Thursday/Friday (Remote Days):**
- Routine check-ins, individual work sessions, client calls with single representatives
- Written updates replace meetings when possible

*FIXES: Eliminates impossible "remote-first facilitation" requirements, aligns meeting types with attendee locations*

### Technology Standards
**Conference Rooms:**
- Standard video conferencing setup (single camera, directional microphone)
- Large displays for screen sharing
- No complex 360° or omnidirectional equipment

**Individual Equipment:**
- Laptop, single external monitor, standard headset
- Access to cloud-based collaboration tools
- No specialized whiteboarding or real-time collaboration requirements

*FIXES: Removes technology requirements that don't work in practice, focuses on proven solutions*

### EU Team Integration
**Communication Protocol:**
- EU team overlap hours: 9 AM - 12 PM Eastern (3 PM - 6 PM CET)
- Key decisions communicated via written updates within 24 hours
- Weekly EU-specific standup at 9 AM Eastern

**Quarterly Integration:**
- One EU team member visits US office each quarter (rotating)
- US leadership visits EU annually for team building
- Major strategic decisions scheduled during overlap hours

*FIXES: Eliminates unsustainable EU working hour expectations, reduces travel costs, creates realistic overlap expectations*

---

## 3. PERFORMANCE AND CAREER MANAGEMENT

### Evaluation Approach
**Department-Specific Metrics:**

*Engineering:*
- Sprint completion rates and code quality (60%)
- Participation in Tuesday-Wednesday architecture discussions (20%)
- Peer collaboration feedback (20%)

*Sales:*
- Pipeline and conversion metrics (70%)
- Client relationship scores (20%)
- Team collaboration effectiveness (10%)

*FIXES: Acknowledges that collaboration IS part of performance, creates measurable standards that account for work location*

### Career Development
- All promotion discussions happen during in-office days
- Mentorship programs pair people with overlapping office schedules
- Leadership development includes both remote and in-person skill building

*FIXES: Addresses proximity bias by making key career conversations happen when everyone is present*

### Conflict Resolution
**Clear escalation path:**
1. Direct manager discussion
2. Department head review
3. HR mediation for policy interpretation
4. CEO final decision on exceptions

*FIXES: Provides missing conflict resolution mechanism with clear authority levels*

---

## 4. EQUIPMENT AND WORKSPACE

### Standard Package (All Employees)
- Laptop, monitor, basic accessories
- $300 annual home office stipend (furniture, lighting, supplies)
- $50/month internet contribution

### Office Resources
- Assigned desks for all employees (used 2 days/week)
- Shared collaboration spaces
- Private call rooms

*FIXES: Simplifies equipment policy, eliminates tracking multiple stipends, accepts office space costs as fixed*

### Security Requirements
- VPN for all remote work
- Two-factor authentication
- Monthly security check-ins (can be remote)

*FIXES: Streamlines security requirements to essential elements only*

---

## 5. FINANCIAL AND OPERATIONAL FRAMEWORK

### Cost Structure
**Annual per-employee costs:**
- Home office stipend: $300
- Internet contribution: $600
- EU quarterly visits: $5,000 per person (3 people total)
- Office space: Maintained at current levels

**Break-even analysis:** Policy reduces commuting costs and some office resources while adding home office support. Net neutral expected.

*FIXES: Addresses missing financial impact analysis, provides realistic cost expectations*

### Success Metrics
**Primary indicators:**
- Retention rates (quarterly measurement)
- Project delivery timelines (monthly tracking)
- Employee satisfaction with work arrangement (quarterly survey)

**Secondary indicators:**
- Office space utilization on Tuesday-Wednesday
- Meeting effectiveness scores
- Client satisfaction (sales team specific)

*FIXES: Creates actionable metrics with clear measurement timelines and baseline data*

## IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE

**Month 1:** Policy communication, desk assignment, equipment orders
**Month 2:** Office reconfiguration, technology setup
**Month 3:** Full implementation with weekly check-ins
**Month 6:** First comprehensive review

## POLICY BOUNDARIES

### What This Policy Does NOT Cover
- Fully remote work options (available through separate accommodation process)
- Flexible daily schedules (core hours remain 9 AM - 5 PM in local timezone)
- International remote work (limited to current EU team)

*FIXES: Sets clear boundaries on policy scope to prevent scope creep*

### Future Considerations
- Policy will be evaluated against competitive market conditions annually
- Expansion to additional locations requires separate business case
- Technology improvements may enable more flexibility over time

*FIXES: Acknowledges competitive talent market reality while maintaining current focus*

4. **Added missing conflict resolution process** - Clear escalation path for policy disputes
