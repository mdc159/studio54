# HYBRID WORK POLICY
**Effective Date: [Date]**  
**Version 2.0**

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid framework that respects departmental needs while maintaining company cohesion. Teams operate with clear minimum requirements but self-organize additional collaboration based on business needs. The policy balances structure with operational simplicity, focusing on business outcomes and client service.

---

## 1. REQUIRED IN-OFFICE DAYS

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Minimum requirement:** 1 day per month per person
- **Team coordination days:** One full-team day per quarter (scheduled 60 days in advance)
- **Self-organization:** Teams determine additional days based on project phases, code reviews, and architecture decisions
- **Core collaboration window:** 11 AM - 3 PM PT when in office

### Sales Team (15 people)
- **Minimum requirement:** 3 days per week per person
- **Required days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (core collaboration days)
- **Client priority:** Schedule adjusts for client needs and commitments; Monday/Friday remote unless client meetings scheduled
- **Team meetings:** All hands on Wednesdays, 2-4 PM local time

### All Other Departments (15 people)
- **Standard requirement:** 2 days per week per person
- **Recommended days:** Wednesday (company-wide anchor day) + one flexible day coordinated with dependent teams
- **Department coordination:** Each department designates one additional team day monthly

### Special Circumstances
- **New hires:** First 30 days include sufficient in-person/video collaboration for effective onboarding (defined by manager and new hire)
- **Client-facing roles:** Schedule accommodates client needs and commitments during client visit weeks
- **Cross-team projects:** Participants coordinate schedules for project duration

*Justification for changes: Version A's rigid new hire requirement (3 days/week) doesn't account for role differences. Version B's flexibility better serves business needs while maintaining onboarding effectiveness. Performance improvement plan requirements removed as unnecessarily punitive.*

## 2. MEETING NORMS

### Scheduling Standards
- **Core meeting hours:** 11 AM - 3 PM PT / 2-6 PM ET (accommodates EU morning overlap)
- **Core communication hours:** 11 AM - 3 PM PT for urgent matters requiring same-day response
- **EU inclusion rule:** Important decisions require 24-hour minimum notice before finalizing to allow EU input
- **Practical scheduling:** When scheduling across timezones, prioritize including people critical to decisions rather than forcing attendance when async participation works better

### Meeting Types and Protocols

**All-Hands Meetings**
- Monthly, rotating time: 10 AM PT one month, 8 AM PT the next
- Recorded with key decisions summarized in writing
- EU team joins alternating months live, watches recording otherwise

**Department Meetings**
- Engineering: Async-first standups, weekly video sync at 12 PM PT
- Sales: Daily 9 AM ET huddles, weekly pipeline reviews Wednesday 2 PM ET
- Other departments: Flexible within core hours, coordinated with dependent teams

**Hybrid Meeting Basics**
- Test technology 5 minutes before important meetings
- Share documents in advance for async review
- Assign someone to ensure remote participants can contribute
- All decisions recorded in [shared workspace]

### EU Team Integration (3 people)
- **Decision inclusion:** 24-hour minimum for decisions affecting EU team
- **Handoff protocols:** End-of-day summaries for US team pickup
- **Meeting participation:** EU team joins 50% of recurring meetings live, 50% async
- **Coverage coordination:** EU team lead coordinates with US teams on handoff communication

*Justification for changes: Version A's complex rotation tracking and meeting time restrictions are operationally unworkable. Version B's approach better serves actual business coordination needs while maintaining EU inclusion.*

## 3. EQUIPMENT POLICY

### Standard Remote Setup (All Employees)
**Provided by Company:**
- Laptop suitable for role requirements
- External monitor (24" minimum)
- Wireless keyboard and mouse
- Headset with microphone for video calls

**Home Office Support:**
- $300 one-time setup reimbursement for home office essentials
- $40/month internet reimbursement (flat rate)

### Office Equipment
**Available in All Offices:**
- Hot-desking stations with monitors and peripherals (no reservations required)
- Conference rooms with hybrid meeting technology
- Printing, scanning, and secure storage for daily use

**Reserved Equipment (For Regular Office Users):**
- Sales team: Dedicated desks with dual monitors
- Engineering: Bookable quiet zones with specialized development setups

### Equipment Management
- **IT support:** Standard business hours support, remote troubleshooting available
- **Replacement:** Equipment replaced within 3-5 business days
- **Return policy:** All equipment returned within 5 days of employment termination

*Justification for changes: Version A's equipment costs ($1,350+ per employee) are excessive for a 50-person company. Version B's streamlined approach ($740 per employee) provides necessary functionality without over-engineering. Removed complex SLA requirements inappropriate for company size.*

## 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION APPROACH

### Universal Performance Standards
Performance measured by **outcomes and impact**, not location or hours worked.

### Evaluation Framework

**Regular Check-ins:**
Managers and direct reports meet regularly (frequency determined by role and project needs) to discuss:
- Progress toward goals and project milestones  
- Support needed for success
- Collaboration effectiveness (including cross-functional partnership)

**Location-Neutral Metrics:**
- **Engineering:** Code quality, sprint completion, technical leadership, knowledge sharing
- **Sales:** Pipeline generation, close rates, client satisfaction, team collaboration  
- **All roles:** Project delivery, business outcome achievement, company value demonstration

### Performance Documentation
- Clear goal setting with measurable outcomes
- Regular progress updates in [performance management system]
- Annual review process consistent with existing company practices
- Quarterly self-assessment with specific examples

### Management Support
- Managers receive guidance on remote team management best practices
- Department heads coordinate to ensure consistent performance standards across locations
- Focus on training managers to set clear expectations and provide regular feedback
- Quarterly calibration sessions to ensure consistent standards

*Justification for changes: Version A's complex 360-degree feedback and certification requirements exceed what a 50-person company can operationally support. Version B's approach maintains performance rigor while using existing management structures.*

## 5. TIMEZONE COORDINATION STRATEGY

### Core Business Hours
- **Primary overlap:** 11 AM - 3 PM PT (2-6 PM GMT) for urgent same-day response
- **All other communication:** Asynchronous with next-business-day response expected

### Communication Protocols

**Synchronous Communication:**
- **Slack/Teams:** During overlap hours for urgent matters
- **Phone/video calls:** Scheduled in advance with timezone consideration
- **Emergency contact:** Defined escalation path for true urgencies

**Asynchronous Communication:**
- **Email:** All non-urgent business communication  
- **Project updates:** Posted in shared workspaces with @mentions
- **Documentation:** Comprehensive notes for handoffs between timezones

### EU Team Integration
- **Decision delays:** 24-hour minimum for decisions affecting EU team
- **Handoff protocols:** Detailed end-of-day summaries for US team pickup
- **Meeting rotation:** EU team joins 50% of recurring meetings live, 50% async
- **Time zone awareness:** All meetings show multiple timezones in calendar system

*Justification for changes: Version A's secondary overlap window and complex communication rules create operational confusion. Version B's simplified approach maintains coordination while being practically implementable.*

## 6. BUSINESS CONTINUITY

### Client Service Standards  
- Client-facing employees coordinate schedules to ensure coverage during client business hours
- Client meetings and commitments take priority over internal collaboration preferences
- Backup coverage arrangements for key client relationships and support functions

### Essential Functions Coverage
- Each department identifies essential daily functions and ensures coverage
- Cross-training and documentation support business continuity regardless of employee location
- Emergency contact procedures for urgent business needs

*Justification for addition: Version A lacked explicit business continuity planning. This addresses real operational risks that Version B correctly identified.*

## 7. IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE

### Phase 1 (Month 1): Setup and Communication
- Communicate policy to all employees with Q&A sessions
- Process equipment requests and begin fulfillment  
- Department heads work with teams to establish collaboration schedules
- IT support processes established

### Phase 2 (Month 2): Adjustment Period
- Teams refine collaboration approaches based on real experience
- Address technology and workspace issues as they arise
- Collect feedback on effectiveness through existing management meetings

### Phase 3 (Month 3+): Steady State
- Policy operates as standard practice
- Quarterly effectiveness assessment in management team meetings
- Adjustments made based on business needs and employee feedback

*Justification for changes: Version A's 6-week timeline assuming simultaneous equipment delivery and office reconfiguration is unrealistic. Version B's approach allows for real-world implementation constraints.*
