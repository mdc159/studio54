# Hybrid Work Policy: Practical Framework for 50-Person Startup

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a structured hybrid framework that accommodates different team needs while maintaining operational simplicity. The approach prioritizes clear expectations and sustainable implementation over complex flexibility.

---

## 1. Required In-Office Days

### Simplified "Fixed Schedule" Model

**Engineering Team (20 people):**
- **Required days:** Wednesdays (team sync day)
- **Optional days:** Tuesdays and Thursdays available for booking
- **Capacity limit:** Max 12 engineers on optional days (first-come, first-served booking)

**Sales Team (15 people):**
- **Required days:** Tuesdays and Wednesdays
- **Remote days:** Mondays, Thursdays, Fridays

**Leadership/Support Functions (15 people):**
- **Required days:** Wednesdays (company alignment day)
- **Optional days:** Choose one additional fixed day per quarter (no weekly changes)

### Office Capacity Management
- Wednesday: 50 people (full capacity - all hands day)
- Tuesday: 15 people (sales only)
- Monday/Thursday/Friday: 15 people maximum
- Desk booking required 24 hours in advance for optional days
- No day-banking or weekly variations allowed

**Fixes Problems:** Eliminates capacity planning chaos (#1) and enforcement impossibility (#1) by removing flexible banking and creating predictable attendance patterns.

---

## 2. Meeting Norms

### Meeting Types and Formats

**All-Hands Meetings:**
- Monthly, first Wednesday, 2 PM ET
- In-person only (everyone required in office on Wednesdays)
- Remote EU team joins via single conference room setup

**Cross-Functional Meetings:**
- Tuesday/Wednesday: Default in-person for attendees in office
- Other days: Video-first
- Mixed meetings: In-office participants use conference room as group, remote participants join as individuals

**Engineering Team Meetings:**
- Wednesday sync: In-person
- Daily standups: Async via Slack
- Sprint planning: Wednesday in-person sessions

**Sales Team Meetings:**
- Tuesday/Wednesday: In-person
- Other days: Video calls

### Meeting Standards
- Core meetings recorded, stored 90 days
- Meeting notes required for all cross-team meetings
- No meetings 9-10 AM (focus time) or 4-6 PM ET (EU handoff time)

**Fixes Problems:** Eliminates "one remote, all remote" rule that creates bad experiences (#2) and removes over-engineered technology requirements (#2).

---

## 3. Equipment Policy

### Streamlined Equipment Approach
- **One-time remote setup:** $1,200 per employee (monitor, keyboard, mouse, webcam)
- **Office equipment:** Shared monitors and peripherals at hot-desks
- **No duplicate setups:** Choose primary work location for equipment optimization

### Provided Equipment
- Company laptop (standard spec for all roles)
- One external monitor (home OR office setup, not both)
- Basic peripherals package
- Standard headphones

### IT Support
- **Response target:** Next business day for non-urgent issues
- **Urgent issues:** 4-hour response during overlap hours (12-3 PM ET) only
- **Office days:** In-person IT support available Wednesdays

**Fixes Problems:** Eliminates duplicate equipment costs and unrealistic SLA commitments (#5) while maintaining adequate support.

---

## 4. Performance Evaluation Approach

### Simplified Outcome-Based Framework

**Engineering Performance:**
- Sprint commitments met (binary: yes/no)
- Code review participation
- Project delivery dates
- Annual peer feedback (no quarterly scoring)

**Sales Performance:**
- Revenue targets
- Pipeline metrics
- Client retention rates

**Universal Metrics:**
- Quarterly OKR achievement
- Annual 360 feedback
- Project contribution assessment

### Review Process
- **Quarterly:** Manager check-ins (video or in-person)
- **Annual:** Performance reviews on Wednesday office days
- **Bias prevention:** Structured evaluation rubrics, no location-based criteria

**Fixes Problems:** Removes subjective "collaboration scores" (#4) and eliminates frequent peer evaluation overhead that creates bias (#4).

---

## 5. Timezone Management Strategy

### EU Team Integration (3 employees)

**Overlap Hours:**
- **Primary overlap:** 1-3 PM ET (7-9 PM CET) - limit to 2 meetings per week maximum
- **EU accommodation:** 9 AM ET (3 PM CET) standup twice weekly maximum
- **Decision documentation:** All decisions posted in Slack within 2 hours of meetings

**EU Team Schedule:**
- **Flexible start:** 10 AM CET to accommodate occasional evening calls
- **Bi-annual visits:** EU team visits US office twice yearly (not quarterly)
- **US visits EU:** One US leadership visit to EU annually

### Communication Protocols
- **Synchronous:** Only during 1-3 PM ET overlap, max 6 hours/week
- **Asynchronous:** Default for all non-urgent communication
- **Response times:** 24 hours within same timezone, 48 hours cross-timezone

**Fixes Problems:** Eliminates unrealistic EU meeting times (#6), reduces travel costs from quarterly to bi-annual (#3), and creates sustainable overlap expectations (#6).

---

## 6. Implementation Timeline

### Phase 1: Setup (Weeks 1-2)
- Order equipment based on employee location choice
- Install basic conference room technology (single camera/mic per room)
- Communicate fixed schedules to all employees

### Phase 2: Trial Period (Weeks 3-6)
- Begin fixed office schedules
- Weekly feedback sessions with team leads
- Adjust booking system based on actual usage

### Phase 3: Full Implementation (Week 7+)
- Policy fully in effect
- Monthly metrics review
- Quarterly adjustment meetings

**Fixes Problems:** Removes over-engineered manager training requirements (#7) and focuses on essential implementation steps.

---

## 7. Success Metrics and Review

### Monthly Assessment
- **Office utilization:** Actual vs. planned attendance
- **Meeting effectiveness:** Completion of scheduled meetings
- **EU integration:** EU team participation in key decisions

### Quarterly Review
- **Employee satisfaction:** Simple 5-question survey
- **Productivity:** Team output metrics (existing KPIs)
- **Policy adherence:** Office day compliance rates

### Annual Assessment
- **Retention rates:** Overall and by team
- **Performance outcomes:** Individual and team goal achievement
- **Cost analysis:** Office space, equipment, and travel expenses

**Fixes Problems:** Focuses on measurable outcomes rather than subjective satisfaction targets and eliminates complex audit assumptions (#4).

---

## 8. Conflict Resolution and Accommodations

### Accommodation Process
- **Medical/family needs:** Work with HR to modify office day requirements
- **Performance issues:** Location-agnostic improvement plans
- **Team conflicts:** Mediation through department heads

### Non-Negotiable Elements
- Wednesday office attendance (except approved accommodations)
- 24-hour booking notice for optional days
- Participation in assigned team meetings

### Escalation Path
1. Direct manager discussion
2. Department head review
3. CEO decision (with HR input for accommodations)

**Fixes Problems:** Defines clear accommodation process (#7) and establishes firm boundaries for policy flexibility.

---

This policy prioritizes operational simplicity and sustainable implementation while addressing the core needs of different teams. The framework eliminates complex flexibility that creates enforcement problems while maintaining enough structure to ensure team cohesion and productivity.
