# Hybrid Work Policy: Balancing Innovation and Connection

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid framework that respects engineering's remote-first preference while addressing sales' collaboration needs. The approach prioritizes outcomes over presence while creating structured opportunities for in-person connection.

---

## 1. Required In-Office Days

### Core Framework: "2-1-Flex" Model

**Engineering Team (20 people):**
- **Minimum requirement:** 1 day per week in-office
- **Recommended:** 2 days per week during sprint planning weeks
- **Flexibility:** Can bank office days (e.g., 4 days one week, 0 the next)
- **Protected remote time:** Fridays designated as "deep work" days - no mandatory office presence

**Sales Team (15 people):**
- **Minimum requirement:** 3 days per week in-office
- **Mandatory days:** Tuesday and Wednesday (core collaboration days)
- **Flexible day:** One additional day chosen by team/individual
- **Remote options:** Monday and Friday available for client calls and administrative work

**Leadership/Support Functions (15 people):**
- **Requirement:** 2 days per week in-office
- **Mandatory:** Wednesday (all-hands alignment day)
- **Flexible:** One additional day based on team needs

### Office Capacity Management
- Maximum 60% capacity on any given day (30 people)
- Desk booking system required 48 hours in advance
- Priority booking for mandatory days

---

## 2. Meeting Norms

### Meeting Types and Formats

**All-Hands Meetings:**
- Monthly, first Wednesday, 2 PM ET / 11 AM PT / 8 PM CET
- Hybrid format with in-office broadcast location
- 45-minute maximum with 15-minute async Q&A follow-up

**Cross-Functional Meetings:**
- Default to video-first (even if some participants are in-office)
- "One remote, all remote" rule - if anyone dials in, everyone uses individual devices
- Meeting rooms equipped with 360-degree cameras and quality audio

**Engineering Team Meetings:**
- Daily standups: Async via Slack, optional video sync twice weekly
- Sprint planning: In-person encouraged but not required
- Retrospectives: Alternating in-person/virtual monthly

**Sales Team Meetings:**
- Weekly pipeline reviews: In-person on Tuesdays
- Client strategy sessions: In-person preferred
- Training and onboarding: In-person mandatory

### Meeting Standards
- All meetings recorded and stored for 30 days
- Shared notes template with action items
- 5-minute buffer between meetings for transitions
- No meetings during "focus time" blocks (9-11 AM daily for engineering)

## 3. Equipment Policy

### Remote Work Stipend
- **One-time setup allowance:** $2,000 per employee
- **Annual refresh:** $500 for existing employees
- **New hire bonus:** Additional $500 for desk setup

### Provided Equipment (All Employees)
- Company laptop (spec based on role requirements)
- External monitor (24" minimum, 27" for engineering)
- Wireless keyboard and mouse
- Noise-canceling headphones
- Webcam upgrade (4K for client-facing roles)

### Office Equipment
- Hot-desking stations with dual monitors
- Video conferencing rooms (4 rooms, 6-person capacity each)
- Phone booth spaces for private calls
- Collaboration spaces with digital whiteboards

### IT Support
- Remote support via ticketing system (4-hour response SLA)
- In-person support available on office days
- Equipment replacement: 2-day shipping for remote employees
- VPN and security software mandatory for all devices

## 4. Performance Evaluation Approach

### Outcome-Based Performance Framework

**Core Principle:** Performance measured by results and impact, not location or hours worked.

**Engineering Performance Metrics:**
- Sprint velocity and story point completion
- Code quality metrics (review feedback, bug rates)
- Technical debt reduction contributions
- Peer collaboration scores (quarterly 360 reviews)

**Sales Performance Metrics:**
- Revenue targets and pipeline development
- Client satisfaction scores
- Deal closure rates and cycle times
- Team collaboration and knowledge sharing

**Universal Performance Indicators:**
- Goal achievement (OKRs reviewed quarterly)
- Communication effectiveness scores
- Project delivery timeliness
- Cultural contribution assessment

### Review Process
- **Quarterly check-ins:** 30-minute video calls with managers
- **Annual reviews:** In-person preferred but not required
- **Peer feedback:** Anonymous 360 reviews collected digitally
- **Self-assessment:** Structured template focusing on achievements and growth areas

### Location Bias Prevention
- Manager training on remote performance evaluation
- Standardized evaluation criteria regardless of work location
- Promotion criteria explicitly exclude physical presence requirements
- Regular audit of promotion/raise patterns by work location

## 5. Timezone Management Strategy

### Core Hours and Communication

**Overlap Hours:**
- **Primary overlap:** 12 PM - 3 PM ET (9 AM - 12 PM PT, 6 PM - 9 PM CET)
- **Secondary overlap:** 9 AM - 12 PM ET (6 AM - 9 AM PT, 3 PM - 6 PM CET)
- All critical meetings scheduled within primary overlap

**EU Team Integration (3 employees):**
- **Morning sync:** 9 AM ET daily standup for EU participation
- **Flexible hours:** EU team can start later (10 AM CET) to accommodate evening calls
- **Quarterly in-person:** EU team visits US office once per quarter (company-funded)
- **Documentation first:** All decisions documented in shared spaces before implementation

### Communication Protocols

**Synchronous Communication:**
- Urgent matters only during overlap hours
- Video calls scheduled with 24-hour notice minimum
- Meeting recordings mandatory for timezone inclusivity

**Asynchronous Communication:**
- Slack for daily updates and non-urgent questions
- Email for formal communications and documentation
- Project management tools (Asana/Jira) for task tracking
- Loom videos for complex explanations

**Response Time Expectations:**
- **Urgent:** 2 hours during business hours
- **Normal:** 24 hours for cross-timezone communication
- **Weekend/holiday:** No response expected

## 6. Implementation Timeline

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)
- Equipment ordering and distribution
- Office space reconfiguration for hot-desking
- IT infrastructure setup (booking systems, meeting room tech)
- Manager training on hybrid leadership

### Phase 2: Soft Launch (Weeks 5-8)
- Policy rollout with 2-week adjustment period
- Daily feedback collection via brief surveys
- Weekly pulse checks with team leads
- Adjustment of office day requirements based on initial feedback

### Phase 3: Full Implementation (Weeks 9-12)
- Formal policy enforcement begins
- First monthly all-hands in hybrid format
- Performance review cycle adjustment
- Policy effectiveness assessment

## 7. Success Metrics and Review

### Quarterly Assessment Metrics
- **Employee satisfaction:** Hybrid work satisfaction survey (target: 75% positive)
- **Productivity:** Team velocity and output metrics (maintain or improve current levels)
- **Collaboration:** Cross-team project success rates
- **Retention:** Voluntary turnover rates by team and work preference
- **Office utilization:** Desk booking rates and space efficiency

### Policy Review Schedule
- **Monthly:** Leadership team review of metrics and feedback
- **Quarterly:** Employee survey and policy adjustment consideration
- **Annually:** Comprehensive policy review with potential major changes

### Escape Clauses
- Emergency full-remote capability maintained
- Individual accommodation process for exceptional circumstances
- Quarterly opt-out surveys to gauge policy satisfaction
- 6-month major revision window if success metrics aren't met

---

## 8. Conflict Resolution

### Escalation Path
1. **Team level:** Discussion with direct manager
2. **Department level:** Review with department head
3. **Executive level:** Final decision by CEO with HR consultation

### Common Scenarios
- **Engineering resistance to office days:** Focus on team-building value, allow day banking
- **Sales pressure for more office time:** Emphasize client-facing flexibility, protect deep work time
- **Timezone conflicts:** Prioritize documentation, rotate meeting times monthly

This policy balances the startup's need for innovation and culture while respecting individual work preferences and maintaining operational efficiency across multiple timezones.
