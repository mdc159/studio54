# Hybrid Work Policy: Structured Framework for 50-Person Startup

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a structured hybrid framework that accommodates different team needs while maintaining operational simplicity. The approach prioritizes clear expectations and outcomes over presence while creating predictable opportunities for in-person connection.

---

## 1. Required In-Office Days

### Structured "Core Day Plus" Model

**Engineering Team (20 people):**
- **Required day:** Wednesdays (team sync day)
- **Optional days:** Tuesdays and Thursdays available for booking
- **Capacity limit:** Max 12 engineers on optional days (first-come, first-served booking)
- **Protected remote time:** Fridays designated as "deep work" days - no mandatory office presence

**Sales Team (15 people):**
- **Required days:** Tuesdays and Wednesdays
- **Remote days:** Mondays, Thursdays, Fridays

**Leadership/Support Functions (15 people):**
- **Required day:** Wednesdays (company alignment day)
- **Optional days:** Choose one additional fixed day per quarter (no weekly changes)

### Office Capacity Management
- Wednesday: 50 people (full capacity - all hands day)
- Tuesday: 15 people (sales only)
- Monday/Thursday/Friday: 15 people maximum
- Desk booking required 24 hours in advance for optional days

**Rationale for departure from Version A:** Version A's banking system and weekly variations create enforcement nightmares and capacity planning chaos. Version B's fixed schedules provide predictability while Version A's protected Friday deep work time addresses legitimate engineering needs.

---

## 2. Meeting Norms

### Meeting Types and Formats

**All-Hands Meetings:**
- Monthly, first Wednesday, 2 PM ET / 11 AM PT / 8 PM CET
- In-person for office attendees, EU team joins via conference room setup
- 45-minute maximum with 15-minute async Q&A follow-up

**Cross-Functional Meetings:**
- Tuesday/Wednesday: Default in-person for attendees in office
- Other days: Video-first
- Mixed meetings: In-office participants use conference room as group, remote participants join individually

**Engineering Team Meetings:**
- Wednesday sync: In-person
- Daily standups: Async via Slack, optional video sync twice weekly
- Sprint planning: Wednesday in-person sessions

**Sales Team Meetings:**
- Tuesday/Wednesday: In-person
- Other days: Video calls

### Meeting Standards
- All meetings recorded and stored for 30 days
- Shared notes template with action items required for cross-team meetings
- No meetings during "focus time" blocks (9-10 AM daily)

**Rationale for departure from Version A:** Version A's "one remote, all remote" rule creates poor experiences for in-person participants and requires over-engineered technology. Version B's approach is more practical while Version A's recording retention and structured notes provide necessary documentation.

---

## 3. Equipment Policy

### Remote Work Setup
- **One-time setup allowance:** $1,500 per employee
- **Annual refresh:** $500 for existing employees

### Provided Equipment (All Employees)
- Company laptop (spec based on role requirements)
- External monitor (home OR office optimization, not both)
- Wireless keyboard and mouse
- Noise-canceling headphones
- Webcam upgrade for client-facing roles

### Office Equipment
- Hot-desking stations with shared monitors
- Video conferencing rooms (4 rooms, 6-person capacity each)
- Phone booth spaces for private calls

### IT Support
- **Response target:** Next business day for standard issues
- **Urgent issues:** 4-hour response during overlap hours (12-3 PM ET)
- **Office support:** In-person IT available on Wednesdays
- **Equipment replacement:** 2-day shipping for remote employees

**Rationale for departure from Version A:** Version A's duplicate equipment setup ($2,000 + annual refresh) is wasteful. Version B's approach is more cost-effective while Version A's role-based specifications and rapid replacement ensure productivity isn't compromised.

---

## 4. Performance Evaluation Approach

### Outcome-Based Performance Framework

**Core Principle:** Performance measured by results and impact, not location or hours worked.

**Engineering Performance Metrics:**
- Sprint velocity and story point completion
- Code quality metrics (review feedback, bug rates)
- Project delivery timeliness
- Annual peer feedback (not quarterly)

**Sales Performance Metrics:**
- Revenue targets and pipeline development
- Client satisfaction scores
- Deal closure rates and cycle times

**Universal Performance Indicators:**
- Quarterly OKR achievement
- Annual 360 reviews
- Project delivery timeliness

### Review Process
- **Quarterly check-ins:** 30-minute calls with managers (video or in-person)
- **Annual reviews:** Wednesday office days preferred
- **Location bias prevention:** Standardized evaluation criteria, no presence requirements

**Rationale for departure from Version A:** Version A's quarterly peer reviews create evaluation fatigue and potential bias. Version B's annual approach is more sustainable while Version A's comprehensive metrics and bias prevention measures remain essential.

---

## 5. Timezone Management Strategy

### EU Team Integration (3 employees)

**Overlap Hours:**
- **Primary overlap:** 1-3 PM ET (7-9 PM CET) - maximum 6 hours/week of EU participation
- **Secondary overlap:** 9 AM ET (3 PM CET) for standups - twice weekly maximum
- **Documentation first:** All decisions documented in shared spaces within 2 hours

**EU Team Schedule:**
- **Flexible start:** 10 AM CET to accommodate evening calls
- **Bi-annual visits:** EU team visits US office twice yearly (company-funded)
- **US leadership visit:** One annual visit to EU team

### Communication Protocols
- **Synchronous:** Limited to overlap hours, max 6 hours/week for EU team
- **Asynchronous:** Default for all non-urgent communication
- **Response times:** 24 hours within timezone, 48 hours cross-timezone

**Rationale for departure from Version A:** Version A's quarterly EU visits ($12K+ annually) and extensive evening meeting expectations are unsustainable. Version B's bi-annual approach balances connection with costs, while Version A's documentation-first principle ensures inclusivity.

---

## 6. Implementation Timeline

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)
- Equipment ordering based on employee location choice
- Office space reconfiguration for hot-desking
- Basic conference room technology setup
- Fixed schedule communication

### Phase 2: Trial Period (Weeks 3-6)
- Policy rollout with adjustment feedback
- Weekly pulse checks with team leads
- Booking system optimization based on usage

### Phase 3: Full Implementation (Week 7+)
- Formal policy enforcement
- Monthly metrics review
- Quarterly policy assessment

**Rationale for departure from Version A:** Version A's manager training requirements add unnecessary complexity for a 50-person startup. Version B's streamlined approach focuses on essential implementation while Version A's feedback mechanisms ensure continuous improvement.

---

## 7. Success Metrics and Review

### Monthly Assessment
- **Office utilization:** Actual vs. planned attendance rates
- **Meeting effectiveness:** Completion rates and participant feedback
- **EU integration:** Participation in key decisions and projects

### Quarterly Review
- **Employee satisfaction:** Hybrid work satisfaction survey (target: 75% positive)
- **Productivity:** Team velocity and output metrics (maintain current levels)
- **Policy adherence:** Office day compliance rates

### Annual Assessment
- **Retention rates:** Overall and by team/work preference
- **Performance outcomes:** Individual and team goal achievement
- **Cost analysis:** Office, equipment, and travel expenses

**Rationale for departure from Version A:** Version A's complex monthly reviews with multiple satisfaction metrics create survey fatigue. Version B's focused approach captures essential data while Version A's specific targets provide clear success criteria.

---

## 8. Conflict Resolution and Accommodations

### Accommodation Process
- **Medical/family needs:** Work with HR to modify office requirements
- **Performance issues:** Location-agnostic improvement plans
- **Individual circumstances:** Case-by-case review with department heads

### Escalation Path
1. **Team level:** Discussion with direct manager
2. **Department level:** Review with department head
3. **Executive level:** CEO decision with HR consultation for accommodations

### Non-Negotiable Elements
- Wednesday office attendance (except approved accommodations)
- 24-hour booking notice for optional days
- Participation in assigned team meetings
- Adherence to timezone overlap limits for EU team

**Rationale for departure from Version A:** Version B's clear non-negotiables provide necessary structure while Version A's comprehensive escalation path ensures fair resolution of conflicts.

---

This policy balances operational simplicity with team needs by establishing clear, predictable schedules while maintaining outcome-focused performance evaluation and sustainable timezone management. The framework eliminates complex flexibility that creates enforcement problems while preserving essential elements for team cohesion and productivity.
