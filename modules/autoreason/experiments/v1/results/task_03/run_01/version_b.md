# Revised Hybrid Work Policy Proposal
**Simplified Flexibility Framework**

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a streamlined approach that balances engineering autonomy with sales collaboration needs through minimal-complexity structures designed for a 50-person company's operational capacity.

## 1. Required In-Office Days

### Core Framework: "Minimum Effective Collaboration"

**Company-Wide Requirements:**
- **Wednesdays** are designated "Collaboration Day"
- All US-based employees (35 people) encouraged but not mandated to be in office
- EU team members join key meetings virtually during overlap hours (see Section 4)

*Problem Fixed: Eliminates anchor day enforcement problems by removing mandate and reducing from two days to one, addressing office capacity constraints and hot-desking issues.*

**Function-Specific Guidelines:**

**Engineering (20 people):**
- Target: 1-2 days/week office presence
- Sprint planning occurs on Wednesdays when possible
- Teams self-organize around project collaboration needs
- No mandatory office days for individual contributors

*Problem Fixed: Removes VP approval bureaucracy for remote periods and eliminates rigid enforcement that could cause talent retention issues.*

**Sales (15 people):**
- Target: 2-3 days/week office presence
- Wednesday collaboration day strongly recommended
- Self-organize around client meeting schedules
- No rigid day requirements

*Problem Fixed: Eliminates sales team inequality by removing the 4-day mandate and aligns expectations with reality that most B2B sales happens remotely anyway.*

**Other Functions (15 people):**
- Target: 1-2 days/week office presence
- Department heads provide guidance based on role needs

### Flexibility Approach:
- **No Exception Processes Required:** Teams coordinate directly with managers
- **No Tracking Systems:** Trust-based approach with outcome focus
- **No Approval Requirements:** Manager discretion for temporary arrangements

*Problem Fixed: Eliminates bureaucratic exception processes that would consume manager time and require non-existent tracking systems.*

## 2. Meeting Norms

### Meeting Types and Approach:

**In-Person Preferred (when participants are in office):**
- Weekly team meetings (Wednesdays when possible)
- Sprint planning and retrospectives
- Quarterly planning sessions
- New employee onboarding sessions

**Default Remote:**
- Daily standups
- 1:1s between managers and reports
- Client presentations
- Training and development sessions
- All recurring meetings

*Problem Fixed: Eliminates hybrid meeting infrastructure overhead by defaulting to remote for recurring meetings, avoiding daily tech setup issues.*

### Meeting Standards:

**Simple Technology Requirements:**
- Use existing conference room setups
- Standard Zoom/Google Meet for remote participants
- Meeting notes in shared documents
- No special hybrid technology required

*Problem Fixed: Avoids 6-figure infrastructure investment in speaker-tracking cameras and wireless presentation systems that a 50-person company cannot justify.*

**Scheduling Guidelines:**
- Core collaboration hours: 9 AM - 3 PM PT / 12 PM - 6 PM ET
- EU inclusion: 8 AM - 11 AM PT / 4 PM - 7 PM EU (limit to 3 meetings/week max per EU employee)
- Friday afternoons reserved for focused work (no meetings after 2 PM PT)

*Problem Fixed: Limits EU late-shift burden to 3 meetings/week maximum rather than daily 4-7 PM availability, addressing work-life balance and potential labor law issues.*

## 3. Equipment and Technology Policy

### Standard Remote Equipment (All Employees):
- Laptop with adequate specs for role
- External monitor
- Headphones with microphone
- $400 home office setup budget (one-time)
- $50/month internet stipend

*Problem Fixed: Eliminates complex equipment categories and reduces administrative overhead while providing essential tools.*

### Office Setup:
- Hot-desking with basic docking stations
- Shared printers and supplies
- Standard conference room booking
- No specialized hybrid technology

*Problem Fixed: Avoids complex technology rollout timeline and supply chain dependencies by using existing office infrastructure.*

### Security Requirements:
- VPN for remote access
- Two-factor authentication
- Company data encryption
- Quarterly security reminders

*Problem Fixed: Simplifies security to essential requirements without creating productivity barriers for home printing and mobile work.*

## 4. Performance Evaluation Approach

### Evaluation Philosophy: "Results and Communication Focus"

**Core Evaluation Areas:**
- Goal achievement (quarterly objectives)
- Quality of work output
- Communication responsiveness
- Team collaboration contribution

*Problem Fixed: Eliminates unmeasurable metrics like "company culture participation" and complex peer feedback rotation systems that require sophisticated HR infrastructure.*

### Simple Evaluation Process:

**Quarterly Reviews:**
- Manager-employee discussion of goal progress
- Identification of support needs
- Next quarter planning
- Work arrangement check-in

**Annual Reviews:**
- Comprehensive performance discussion
- Career development planning
- Compensation review

*Problem Fixed: Removes 360 feedback systems and peer feedback rotation that require software and HR bandwidth not available to smaller companies.*

### Performance Support:
- Regular 1:1s for feedback
- Clear goal setting
- Training resources as needed
- Equipment/arrangement adjustments when helpful

*Problem Fixed: Focuses on practical support rather than complex measurement systems.*

## 5. Timezone Management Strategy

### EU Team Integration (3 people):

**Sustainable Overlap:**
- **Limited Overlap Requirement:** 8 AM - 10 AM PT (4 PM - 6 PM EU)
- Maximum 3 overlap meetings per EU employee per week
- Async handoff documentation for critical items only

*Problem Fixed: Reduces EU late-shift burden from daily to 3x/week and shortens required overlap hours to respect work-life balance.*

**Communication Approach:**
- Slack for urgent items during overlap hours
- Email for non-urgent communication
- Loom recordings for complex updates
- Written summaries for key decisions

*Problem Fixed: Eliminates complex "follow-the-sun" development handoffs that don't work with limited staffing.*

### Cross-Coast US Coordination:

**Practical Scheduling:**
- Default meeting time: 10 AM PT / 1 PM ET
- Accommodate East Coast until 5 PM ET
- Accommodate West Coast from 8 AM PT
- Async alternatives when scheduling doesn't work

*Problem Fixed: Simplifies cross-coast coordination without complex handoff protocols.*

## Implementation Timeline

### Phase 1 (Week 1): Simple Communication
- All-hands announcement
- Manager Q&A session
- Equipment budget communication

### Phase 2 (Weeks 2-4): Gradual Adoption
- Teams begin Wednesday collaboration focus
- Equipment ordering as needed
- Feedback collection

### Phase 3 (Week 5+): Steady State
- Policy in full effect
- Monthly check-ins with managers
- Quarterly policy review

*Problem Fixed: Eliminates complex pilot testing and technology implementation that assumes equipment can be delivered quickly.*

## Success Measurement

**Simple Metrics:**
- Employee satisfaction (quarterly pulse survey)
- Project delivery consistency
- Employee retention
- Manager feedback on collaboration effectiveness

*Problem Fixed: Focuses on measurable outcomes rather than subjective metrics like "innovation indicators" that lack measurement methodology.*

**Review Process:**
- Quarterly manager feedback collection
- Semi-annual employee pulse survey
- Annual policy effectiveness review

*Problem Fixed: Reduces review frequency to sustainable levels and focuses on feedback sources that actually exist.*

## Budget Impact

**One-Time Costs:**
- Home office setup: $400 × 50 = $20,000
- No technology infrastructure upgrades required

**Ongoing Costs:**
- Internet stipends: $50 × 50 × 12 = $30,000/year
- No additional software or hardware maintenance

*Problem Fixed: Provides realistic budget requirements that a 50-person company can manage rather than 6-figure infrastructure investments.*

This revised policy maintains the core benefits of hybrid work while eliminating operational complexity that would overwhelm a 50-person company's capacity to execute effectively.
