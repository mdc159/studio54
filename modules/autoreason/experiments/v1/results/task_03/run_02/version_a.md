# Hybrid Work Policy
## [Company Name] - Effective [Date]

---

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid work framework that balances Engineering's remote-first preference with Sales' collaboration needs while maintaining operational excellence across three time zones. The policy emphasizes outcomes over location while providing structured touchpoints for team cohesion.

---

## 1. In-Office Requirements by Function

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 1 day per week in-office (Tuesday or Wednesday preferred)
- **Team Sync Days**: Entire engineering team in-office on the first Wednesday of each month
- **Flexibility**: Individual contributors may work fully remote with manager approval if they maintain performance standards and participate in monthly team sync
- **Project-Based Exceptions**: Critical project phases may require additional in-office collaboration (max 2 weeks per quarter)

### Sales Team (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 3 days per week in-office (Monday, Tuesday, Thursday)
- **Flexibility**: Friday remote work permitted after achieving monthly targets
- **Client-Facing Roles**: Account Executives must be in-office during core client hours (9 AM - 4 PM local time) on designated days
- **Remote Exceptions**: Approved travel for client meetings counts toward in-office requirement

### Leadership & Support Functions (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 days per week in-office (Tuesday and one additional day of choice)
- **All-Hands Requirement**: Full leadership team in-office every Tuesday for strategic alignment

---

## 2. Meeting Norms & Collaboration Standards

### Core Meeting Hours
- **Primary Window**: 11 AM - 2 PM ET (8 AM - 11 AM PT, 4 PM - 7 PM GMT)
- **Secondary Window**: 9 AM - 11 AM ET (6 AM - 8 AM PT, 2 PM - 4 PM GMT) for urgent cross-functional needs

### Meeting Guidelines
- **Default to Hybrid**: All meetings must accommodate both remote and in-office participants
- **Required Equipment**: Conference rooms equipped with 360-degree cameras, quality microphones, and large displays
- **Meeting Etiquette**:
  - Camera on for meetings under 10 people
  - Designated facilitator ensures remote voices are heard
  - All materials shared digitally, even for in-office participants
  - 5-minute buffer between meetings for timezone transitions

### Asynchronous Communication
- **Documentation Standard**: All decisions documented in shared systems within 24 hours
- **Response Time SLAs**:
  - Urgent (Slack): 2 hours during business hours
  - Standard (Email): 24 hours
  - Non-urgent: 48 hours
- **Handoff Protocols**: End-of-day updates required when work passes between time zones

## 3. Equipment & Technology Policy

### Company-Provided Equipment (All Employees)
- Laptop (MacBook Pro or equivalent Windows machine)
- External monitor (minimum 24")
- Ergonomic keyboard and mouse
- Noise-canceling headphones with microphone
- Webcam (if laptop camera insufficient)

### Home Office Stipend
- **Amount**: $1,500 one-time setup allowance + $100/month ongoing
- **Eligible Expenses**: Desk, chair, lighting, internet upgrades, co-working space memberships
- **Approval Process**: Receipts submitted through expense system with manager approval

### Security Requirements
- VPN mandatory for all remote work
- Company-managed device encryption and monitoring
- Two-factor authentication for all systems
- Home office security assessment (self-certification checklist)

### Office Resources
- **Hot-desking System**: Reservation-based desk booking (24-hour advance notice)
- **Private Spaces**: Bookable phone booths and small meeting rooms
- **Collaboration Zones**: Dedicated spaces for hybrid team meetings
- **Storage**: Personal lockers available for regular office users

## 4. Performance Evaluation Framework

### Outcome-Based Metrics (70% of evaluation)
- **Engineering**: Code quality, sprint completion rates, system reliability metrics
- **Sales**: Revenue targets, pipeline generation, client satisfaction scores
- **All Roles**: Goal achievement, project delivery, stakeholder feedback

### Collaboration & Culture Metrics (20% of evaluation)
- Participation in team meetings and company events
- Mentorship and knowledge sharing activities
- Cross-functional project contributions
- Peer feedback scores (360-degree reviews)

### Professional Development (10% of evaluation)
- Skill advancement and learning objectives
- Industry engagement and thought leadership
- Internal training completion and knowledge sharing

### Location-Agnostic Standards
- **Documentation**: All employees must maintain up-to-date project documentation
- **Availability**: Clear communication of working hours and response times
- **Proactive Communication**: Regular updates on project status and blockers
- **Team Integration**: Active participation in team-building and company culture initiatives

### Performance Support
- **Monthly Check-ins**: Manager-employee discussions on work location effectiveness
- **Quarterly Reviews**: Formal assessment including remote work effectiveness
- **Performance Improvement**: Location-specific support plans when needed (e.g., co-working space allowance, additional in-office time)

## 5. Timezone Management Strategy

### EU Team Integration (3 people)
- **Core Overlap**: 9 AM - 11 AM ET daily for EU team participation
- **Rotating Schedule**: EU team members alternate early/late meetings weekly
- **Documentation Priority**: All EU handoffs require detailed written updates
- **Quarterly Visits**: EU team members visit US offices once per quarter (company-funded)

### Cross-Coast Coordination (US East/West)
- **Extended Core Hours**: 11 AM - 2 PM ET for all-team meetings
- **Follow-the-Sun Handoffs**: Structured protocols for passing work between coasts
- **Regional Autonomy**: West Coast team empowered to make decisions outside East Coast hours

### Asynchronous-First Protocols
- **Decision Documentation**: All decisions recorded in central system with timestamps
- **Video Updates**: Weekly async video updates for complex projects spanning time zones
- **Overlap Optimization**: Critical discussions scheduled during maximum overlap hours
- **Emergency Escalation**: Clear protocols for urgent cross-timezone issues

### Escalation & Exceptions
- **Manager Level**: Day-to-day flexibility and minor adjustments
- **Department Head**: Temporary policy modifications for projects
- **CEO Approval**: Permanent individual exceptions or policy changes
- **Emergency Protocol**: Temporary full-remote authorization for business continuity

## 7. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if my role requires more/less office time than specified?**
A: Managers can approve temporary adjustments. Permanent changes require department head approval and documentation of business justification.

**Q: How are promotions affected by work location choice?**
A: Promotions are based solely on performance metrics outlined in Section 4. Location preference cannot be a factor in advancement decisions.

**Q: What happens if office space becomes limited?**
A: Desk booking system prioritizes based on scheduled in-office requirements. Additional space will be secured if utilization exceeds 80% capacity.

**Q: Can I work from other locations while remote?**
A: Yes, with manager approval and adherence to security protocols. International work requires legal and tax review.
