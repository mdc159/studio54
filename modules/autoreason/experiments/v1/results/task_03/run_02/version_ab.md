# Hybrid Work Policy (Final Version)
## [Company Name] - Effective [Date]

---

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid work framework that balances Engineering's remote-first preference with Sales' collaboration needs while maintaining operational excellence across three time zones. The policy emphasizes outcomes over location while providing structured touchpoints for team cohesion and realistic resource allocation.

---

## 1. In-Office Requirements by Function

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 days per week in-office (Tuesday-Thursday window)
- **Team Sync Days**: Entire engineering team in-office on the second Tuesday of each month
- **Sales Collaboration**: Available in-office within 48 hours notice for Sales demos/technical discussions
- **Project-Based Exceptions**: Critical project phases may require additional in-office collaboration (max 3-4 days per week for 1 month per quarter)
- **Individual Flexibility**: High performers may work fully remote with manager approval if they maintain performance standards and meet collaboration requirements

*Justification: Version B's 2-day requirement better addresses Sales coordination needs while Version A's individual flexibility maintains Engineering autonomy. Tuesday alignment ensures Sales-Engineering coordination.*

### Sales Team (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 3 days per week in-office (Tuesday-Thursday required, Monday or Friday flexible)
- **Flexibility**: Friday remote work permitted after achieving monthly targets
- **Client-Facing Roles**: Account Executives must be in-office during core client hours (9 AM - 4 PM local time) on designated days
- **Remote Exceptions**: Approved travel for client meetings counts toward in-office requirement

*Justification: Version B's Tuesday-Thursday core with flexible third day optimizes Sales-Engineering overlap while maintaining Version A's performance-based Friday flexibility.*

### Leadership & Support Functions (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 days per week in-office (Tuesday required, one additional day Tuesday-Thursday)
- **All-Hands Requirement**: Full leadership team in-office every Tuesday for strategic alignment

*Justification: Combines both versions' Tuesday requirement with Version B's realistic Tuesday-Thursday window.*

## 2. Office Capacity & Meeting Infrastructure

### Capacity Management
- **Desk Allocation**: 25 reserved desks (Sales leadership, frequent collaborators) + 30 bookable flex desks
- **Booking System**: 72-hour advance reservation with 8-hour minimum blocks
- **Capacity Monitoring**: Weekly utilization reports with 85% trigger for expansion planning
- **Overflow Protocol**: Agreements with 2 nearby co-working facilities for peak demand

*Justification: Version B's capacity math is essential - Version A's hot-desking was mathematically impossible with actual headcount.*

### Meeting Infrastructure  
- **Conference Rooms**: 6 hybrid-ready rooms equipped with 360-degree cameras, quality microphones, and large displays
- **Investment Required**: $60,000 for complete room technology upgrade
- **Meeting Types**:
  - All-remote: Default for cross-timezone collaboration
  - All-in-office: Strategic sessions requiring full engagement
  - Hybrid: Only when business critical and unavoidable

*Justification: Version A's meeting standards are superior, but Version B's realistic investment number and meeting type clarity prevent over-reliance on difficult hybrid formats.*

## 3. Timezone Management & Communication

### Core Meeting Hours
- **Primary Window**: 10 AM - 1 PM ET (7 AM - 10 AM PT, 3 PM - 6 PM GMT)
- **Secondary Window**: 1 PM - 3 PM ET for West Coast focus

*Justification: Version B's shortened window is operationally sustainable while Version A's extended coverage was unrealistic for EU team.*

### EU Team Integration (3 people)
- **Sustainable Schedule**: 2-hour daily overlap maximum (10 AM - 12 PM ET)
- **Schedule Premium**: 15% compensation adjustment for flexibility requirements
- **Rotation Protocol**: Monthly rotation of early/late meeting responsibilities among EU team
- **Quarterly Visits**: EU team members visit US offices once per quarter (company-funded)
- **Legal Compliance**: Policy reviewed by EU employment counsel

*Justification: Version B's sustainability focus and compensation are legally necessary. Version A's longer hours would violate EU work-time directives.*

### Asynchronous Communication Standards
- **Documentation Standard**: All decisions documented in shared systems within 4 hours
- **Response Time SLAs**:
  - Urgent (Slack): 2 hours during business hours
  - Standard (Email): 24 hours  
  - Non-urgent: 48 hours
- **Handoff Protocols**: End-of-day updates required when work passes between time zones
- **Async-First Rule**: Meetings over 30 minutes require pre-work and documented outcomes

*Justification: Version A's detailed communication standards combined with Version B's 4-hour documentation window for operational efficiency.*

- **Compliance Monitoring**: Monthly automated security scans
- **Audit Process**: Quarterly random virtual home office assessments
- **Enforcement**: Progressive response (warning → training → equipment retrieval)

*Justification: Version A's security requirements with Version B's actual enforcement mechanisms.*

*Justification: Version A's comprehensive standards with Version B's specific accountability triggers.*

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if my role requires more/less office time than specified?**
A: Managers can approve temporary adjustments up to 30 days. Extended changes require department head approval with documented business justification.

**Q: What happens if I can't get a desk on my required office day?**
A: Overflow agreements with nearby co-working spaces provide guaranteed backup workspace. Persistent capacity issues trigger immediate expansion planning.

**Q: How are EU team members compensated for schedule flexibility?**
A: 15% schedule premium plus annual working visit to US offices, with strict 2-hour daily overlap limits to ensure sustainable work-life balance.

**Q: How are promotions affected by work location choice?**
A: Promotions are based solely on performance metrics outlined in Section 5. Location preference cannot be a factor in advancement decisions.
