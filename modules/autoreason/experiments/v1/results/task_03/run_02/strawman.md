## Critical Problems with This Hybrid Work Policy

### **Unworkable Meeting Windows**

The "core meeting hours" of 11 AM - 2 PM ET creates a 3-hour daily window for a 50-person company across multiple time zones. This is mathematically insufficient - you'd need roughly 15-20 hours of meeting time daily just for basic coordination. The EU team's 9-11 AM ET window means they're working until 7 PM local time regularly, which will cause immediate attrition.

### **Contradictory Office Requirements**

Engineering gets 1 day minimum but Sales gets 3 days, yet they need to collaborate. The policy doesn't address what happens when Engineering's remote-first preference collides with Sales needing them present for client demos, technical discussions, or deal support. The "first Wednesday monthly" engineering sync will conflict with Sales' Monday/Tuesday/Thursday requirement about 25% of the time.

### **Hot-Desking Math Doesn't Work**

With Sales requiring 3 days/week (45 person-days) plus Leadership requiring 2 days/week (30 person-days) plus Engineering's 1 day/week (20 person-days), you need 95 person-days of desk space weekly. For a 50-person company, this likely exceeds actual office capacity, but the policy provides no capacity management beyond "24-hour advance notice."

### **Technology Costs Are Underestimated**

$1,500 + $100/month per person for 50 employees = $75,000 upfront + $60,000 annually just for home office stipends. Add company equipment ($3,000+ per person = $150,000) plus office technology upgrades for hybrid meetings (easily $50,000+). Total cost approaches $300,000+ with no budget allocation or cost-benefit analysis.

### **Performance Metrics Create Perverse Incentives**

"Participation in team meetings and company events" as 20% of evaluation directly contradicts "outcomes over location." Remote workers will game this by attending unnecessary meetings. The policy simultaneously claims location agnostic evaluation while making physical presence a scored metric.

### **EU Team Setup Is Unsustainable**

Three EU employees working until 7 PM daily for "core overlap" plus quarterly US visits creates an untenable work-life balance. The policy assumes EU workers will accept this without addressing compensation, local labor laws, or realistic retention challenges.

### **Security Requirements Lack Enforcement Mechanism**

"Home office security assessment (self-certification checklist)" is security theater. VPN requirements, device encryption, and 2FA are mentioned but there's no compliance monitoring, audit process, or consequences for violations. This creates liability exposure.

### **Meeting Equipment Assumptions Are Wrong**

"All meetings must accommodate both remote and in-office participants" with "360-degree cameras" and "designated facilitators" assumes every meeting room is a high-tech conference facility. Most companies have regular meeting rooms that can't support this without major renovation.

### **Handoff Protocols Are Undefined**

"End-of-day updates required when work passes between time zones" sounds reasonable but doesn't specify format, systems, approval workflows, or what constitutes adequate handoff documentation. This will create bottlenecks and blame-shifting.

### **Exception Processes Create Administrative Burden**

Multiple approval layers (manager, department head, CEO) for various exceptions will bog down operations. "Emergency Protocol" for business continuity isn't defined - who declares emergencies? What triggers them? How long do they last?

### **Quarterly EU Visits Are Operationally Complex**

Company-funded quarterly visits for 3 EU employees means 12 international trips annually with visa requirements, tax implications, and coordination complexity. No budget allocated and no consideration of tax residency issues for frequent travelers.

### **Missing Conflict Resolution**

No process for handling conflicts between department requirements (e.g., when Sales needs Engineering in-office but Engineering is at minimum requirement). No tie-breaker mechanism when business needs conflict with policy flexibility.

### **Capacity Planning Is Absent**

Policy assumes office space, parking, catering, and IT support can flex up and down based on daily attendance without addressing lead times, minimum service levels, or cost implications of variable capacity.
