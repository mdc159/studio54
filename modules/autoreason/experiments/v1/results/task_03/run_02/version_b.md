# Hybrid Work Policy (Revised)
## [Company Name] - Effective [Date]

---

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid work framework that balances operational needs across functions while maintaining productivity across three time zones. The policy prioritizes business outcomes while providing structured coordination mechanisms and realistic resource allocation.

---

## 1. Office Presence Framework

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Core Requirement**: 2 days per week in-office (Tuesday-Thursday window)
- **Team Alignment**: Second Tuesday of each month - full engineering team in-office
- **Client Support**: Available in-office within 48 hours notice for Sales demos/technical discussions
- **Project Flexibility**: Critical phases may require 3-4 office days per week (max 1 month per quarter)

*Fixes contradictory office requirements by ensuring Engineering availability for Sales collaboration and aligning sync days with Sales presence*

### Sales Team (15 people)  
- **Core Requirement**: 3 days per week in-office (Tuesday-Thursday required, Monday or Friday flexible)
- **Client Hours**: In-office during 9 AM - 4 PM ET on designated days
- **Engineering Coordination**: Tuesday overlap guaranteed for technical support needs

*Fixes Engineering-Sales coordination by mandating Tuesday overlap*

### Leadership & Support Functions (15 people)
- **Core Requirement**: 2 days per week in-office (Tuesday required, one additional Tuesday-Thursday)
- **Strategic Alignment**: Leadership team in-office every Tuesday

---

## 2. Office Capacity Management

### Desk Allocation System
- **Reserved Desks**: 25 permanent assignments (Sales leadership, frequent collaborators)
- **Flex Pool**: 30 bookable desks via scheduling system
- **Booking Rules**: 72-hour advance notice required, 8-hour minimum blocks
- **Capacity Monitoring**: Weekly utilization reports with 85% capacity trigger for expansion planning
- **Overflow Protocol**: Temporary workspace agreements with 2 nearby co-working facilities

*Fixes hot-desking math by calculating actual capacity needs (max 65 people on peak Tuesday) and providing overflow solutions*

### Office Hours & Access
- **Core Hours**: 8 AM - 6 PM ET for full services
- **Extended Access**: 6 AM - 8 PM with reduced support
- **Peak Day Management**: Tuesday capacity prioritized by business function (Sales > Engineering > Support)

---

## 3. Meeting & Communication Framework

### Time Zone Strategy
- **Primary Collaboration Window**: 10 AM - 1 PM ET (3 hours daily)
- **EU Team Schedule**: 10 AM - 12 PM ET participation (3 PM - 5 PM local) - 2 hours maximum daily
- **Cross-Coast Coverage**: 1 PM - 3 PM ET for West Coast focus
- **Async-First Rule**: Meetings over 30 minutes require pre-work and documented outcomes

*Fixes unworkable meeting windows by reducing EU evening hours to sustainable 2-hour maximum and providing clear time boundaries*

### Meeting Standards
- **Hybrid-Ready Rooms**: 6 conference rooms equipped for video (investment: $60,000)
- **Meeting Types**: 
  - All-remote: Default for cross-timezone collaboration
  - All-in-office: Strategic sessions, sensitive discussions
  - Hybrid: Only when business critical and unavoidable
- **Documentation**: All decisions logged in central system within 4 hours

*Fixes technology assumptions by limiting hybrid meetings and specifying actual room requirements*

### EU Team Integration (3 people)
- **Sustainable Schedule**: 2-hour daily overlap maximum (10 AM - 12 PM ET)
- **Compensation**: 15% premium for schedule flexibility
- **Rotation**: Monthly rotation of early/late meeting responsibilities
- **Annual Visits**: One 2-week working visit to US offices per person per year
- **Local Labor Compliance**: Policy reviewed by EU employment counsel

*Fixes EU sustainability by limiting hours, adding compensation, and addressing legal compliance*

## 5. Performance & Accountability

### Evaluation Framework (Location-Agnostic)
- **Outcome Metrics**: 80% of evaluation
  - Function-specific KPIs (revenue, code quality, project delivery)
  - Stakeholder satisfaction scores
  - Goal achievement rates
- **Collaboration Effectiveness**: 20% of evaluation
  - Project handoff quality scores
  - Cross-functional partnership ratings
  - Knowledge sharing contributions (measured by documentation, not meeting attendance)

*Fixes perverse incentives by removing meeting participation as a metric and focusing on collaboration outcomes*

### Accountability Mechanisms
- **Weekly Status Updates**: Standardized format for all remote workers
- **Monthly Performance Check**: Manager assessment of work location effectiveness
- **Escalation Triggers**: Two consecutive months of performance concerns = mandatory location adjustment

## 6. Operational Protocols

### Cross-Functional Coordination
- **Sales-Engineering Escalation**: 48-hour in-office response requirement for critical client needs
- **Project Handoffs**: Structured templates with approval workflows
- **Decision Authority**: Clear escalation matrix with timezone considerations
- **Conflict Resolution**: Department head mediation with 72-hour resolution SLA

*Fixes missing conflict resolution and undefined handoff protocols*

### Exception Management
- **Standard Exceptions**: Manager approval (temporary schedule adjustments <30 days)
- **Extended Exceptions**: Department head approval (30-90 days)
- **Policy Modifications**: CEO approval required (>90 days or permanent changes)
- **Emergency Remote Work**: Automatic approval for weather, health, or business continuity (manager notification within 24 hours)

*Fixes administrative burden by streamlining exception processes and defining emergency protocols*

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What happens if I can't get a desk on my required office day?**
A: Overflow agreements with nearby co-working spaces provide guaranteed backup. Persistent capacity issues trigger immediate expansion planning.

**Q: How are EU team members compensated for schedule flexibility?**
A: 15% schedule premium plus annual working visit to US offices, with strict 2-hour daily overlap limits.

**Q: What if Sales needs Engineering support but they're remote?**
A: 48-hour in-office response requirement ensures critical client support. Non-urgent requests use structured async handoff protocols.
