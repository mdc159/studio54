Here are the real problems I found:

**1. Word count likely exceeds 500 words.**
The policy body alone (excluding the preamble/changelog section) runs approximately 520–540 words. The task requires a maximum of 500 words for the memo. The preamble ("I'll address each identified problem...") adds further words, but even if excluded, the policy itself is over the limit.

**2. The preamble/changelog section should not exist.**
The task asks for a policy memo. The document opens with "I'll address each identified problem systematically" and a bulleted list of editorial changes. This is not part of a policy memo and violates the spirit and likely the letter of the deliverable requirement.

**3. Prohibited Use 6 introduces an assumption not derivable from base facts.**
The prohibition states "no AI tool may process company or customer data in cloud environments or configurations not covered by that authorization." The phrase "cloud environments or configurations" is not derivable from the base facts. The base facts state only that FedRAMP authorization is pending with a Q3 target. No specific restriction on cloud environments or configurations is stated or derivable.

**4. Enforcement item 2 partially duplicates Prohibited Use 3.**
Enforcement item 2 restates the pull request license confirmation requirement already established as a prohibition. Enforcement is supposed to enforce rules, not re-declare them. This creates redundancy and ambiguity about whether it is a rule or a consequence.

**5. Prohibited Use 4 motivation is partially circular.**
The motivating fact cited for the Slack prohibition includes "outside counsel finding that inputting data into non-approved third-party AI services likely violates existing DPA terms." However, Slack is an approved company tool — it is not a third-party AI service in the same sense. The DPA concern motivating the Slack AI prohibition is not directly derivable from the base facts; the base fact simply states Slack AI features are disabled, not why they were disabled.

**6. Permitted Uses item 3 references a budget figure without policy consequence.**
The $50,000 budget figure is mentioned as a constraint on approval of new tools, but the policy states no consequence or criterion tied to it. It reads as informational filler rather than an enforceable policy item, which conflicts with the constraint against aspirational or non-operative language.

**7. Scope item 3 ("Conflicting informal practices are superseded") is vague and adds no enforceable content.**
It does not identify what practices, what mechanism supersedes them, or what happens if someone claims ignorance. It functions as filler and does not meet the numbered-item standard of being a concrete, enforceable statement.

**8. No section explicitly addresses the 45% of sales staff already using AI for email drafting.**
The base facts identify this as a current-state reality requiring policy coverage. The policy prohibits unapproved tools but does not explicitly address the pre-existing behavior of sales staff using AI email drafting tools, leaving a gap in enforceability for an identified at-risk population.
