## Problems Found

### 1. Document Includes Non-Policy Preamble Content
The document opens with a multi-paragraph editorial block ("I'll address each problem in order…") that is not part of the policy memo. This content is not a section, contains no numbered items, and should not appear in a company policy document. It is not derivable from the base facts and violates the spirit of the task, which calls for a policy memo, not a revision log.

### 2. Scope Item 3 Is Prose Justification, Not a Scoping Rule
Scope item 3 ("The company handles customer PII and financial data and holds SOC2 Type II certification…") is background context, not a statement of who or what the policy covers. It does not define scope. The section constraint requires numbered items that function as scoping rules. This item fails that function.

### 3. Permitted Uses Item 2 Contains Unenforceable Conditions Without Reference to Existing Controls
The constraint requires the policy be "enforceable without new tooling (use existing access controls and review processes)." Permitted Uses item 2 requires that AI-generated sales email content be "reviewed and confirmed accurate and original by the sender before transmission" and that this be "recorded." No existing review or recording process for sales email drafting is identified in the base facts. This invents a new procedural requirement not derivable from the base facts.

### 4. Prohibited Uses Item 1 Cites SOC2, GDPR, and FedRAMP as Motivating Facts
The constraint states "every prohibition must reference which base fact motivates it." SOC2 Type II certification, GDPR obligations, and pending FedRAMP authorization are compliance statuses, not the motivating facts for the prohibition. The motivating facts are Incident 1 and the outside counsel DPA finding. Citing compliance statuses as motivators conflates what the company is with why the prohibition exists.

### 5. Enforcement Item 3 Invents a Procedural Requirement
Enforcement item 3 requires that violations of Prohibition 2 be "reported by the employee's manager to Legal before any external remediation action is taken." No base fact establishes a manager-to-Legal reporting chain or a pre-remediation Legal consultation requirement. This is an invented procedural step not derivable from the base facts.

### 6. Word Count Is Not Verifiable as Compliant
The document includes the editorial preamble block, which adds substantial word count. If the 500-word limit applies to the full submitted document (the reasonable interpretation), the document very likely exceeds 500 words. Even if the preamble is excluded, the policy itself is close to the limit and the margin is not confirmed. The preamble's presence creates an unresolved compliance question.

### 7. Permitted Uses Item 3 References Budget as an Approval Criterion Without Establishing an Approval Process
Item 3 states tools "may be requested through Engineering or department leadership for evaluation against the $50K/year AI tooling budget." The base fact is that $50K/year is allocated; it does not establish Engineering or department leadership as the approval authority or define an evaluation process. The specific routing of approval requests is not derivable from the base facts.
