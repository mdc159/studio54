## Problems Found

### 1. Document Contains Extraneous Preamble (Constraint: Output should be the policy memo)

The document opens with a lengthy "Changes made and problems addressed" section that is not part of the policy. The task asked for a policy memo. This editorial commentary should not appear in the final deliverable. It inflates the document and is not a policy element.

### 2. Word Count Likely Exceeds 500 Words (Constraint: Maximum 500 words)

The policy body alone—excluding the preamble—is well over 500 words. Sections Scope (3 items), Permitted Uses (5 items), Prohibited Uses (5 items with lengthy basis notes), and Enforcement (9 items) collectively run approximately 600–650 words of policy text. The constraint is almost certainly violated even if the editorial preamble is excluded from the count.

### 3. Enforcement Has 9 Numbered Items, Not a Clean Structure (Constraint: Each section must have numbered items)

The constraint requires numbered items. Having 9 enforcement items—4 consequence rules followed by 5 audit-trail sub-items labeled "Prohibited Use #X audit trail"—creates an implicit two-tier structure within a single section. The audit trail items are functionally sub-items of the prohibition items, not independent enforcement rules. This violates the flat numbered-item requirement in spirit and creates structural ambiguity.

### 4. Termination as a Consequence Is Not Derivable from Base Facts (Constraint: Add nothing not derivable from base facts)

Enforcement item 3 states that customer data exposure results in termination. No base fact establishes or implies termination as a consequence. Outside counsel flagged DPA violations; no HR or disciplinary framework is mentioned in the base facts. This is invented content.

### 5. "Permanent Revocation" for a Second Violation Is Also Invented

Enforcement item 2 specifies permanent revocation on a second violation. The base facts contain no disciplinary ladder or escalation framework. This is not derivable from any stated fact.

### 6. Written Warning in Enforcement Item 1 Is Invented

A "written warning" as a formal HR mechanism is not derivable from any base fact. The base facts do not describe an HR system, warning framework, or disciplinary process.

### 7. Permitted Uses Item 5 Requires Finance Sign-Off — Finance Is Not a Gatekeeper in the Base Facts

The base facts establish a $50K budget but do not assign Finance any approval authority over AI tool selection. Requiring Finance head sign-off is an invention not derivable from the stated facts.

### 8. Slack Prohibition Basis Is Partially Circular

Prohibited Use #4 justifies the Slack prohibition partly by saying "Slack AI features are currently disabled"—i.e., the current state is used as its own justification. The real basis (DPA violation per outside counsel) is present but buried after the circular logic.

### 9. Enforcement Item 9 Gives Legal Interpretive/Gatekeeping Authority Not Established in Base Facts

Item 9 states Legal "reviews all customer contracts and deliverables before execution." The base facts say outside counsel flagged a risk; they do not establish Legal as a mandatory pre-execution reviewer of all contracts. This is an invented process.

### 10. Sales AI Tool Approval Path Is Vague and Potentially Unenforceable

Permitted Uses item 4 permits sales AI writing tools "through tools approved under item 5," but item 5 describes a future approval process. No currently approved sales AI tool exists in the base facts. This means sales staff have no currently permitted AI writing tool, yet the policy implies they do—a logical gap.
