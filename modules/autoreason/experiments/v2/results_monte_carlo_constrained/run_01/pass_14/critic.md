## Problems Found

### Constraint Violations

**1. Word count likely exceeds 500 words.**
The policy body alone (excluding the preamble/changelog section) runs well over 500 words. Counting the memo sections: Scope (~75 words), Permitted Uses (~105 words), Prohibited Uses (~190 words), Enforcement (~105 words) totals approximately 475 words for the numbered items alone. Adding headers, the title block, and section labels pushes it over 500. The constraint applies to the document produced, and the document includes the lengthy preamble/changelog as part of the submitted document — if that counts, it far exceeds 500 words. If it doesn't count, the margin is extremely tight and likely still violated.

**2. The document includes content outside the four required sections.**
The constraint states the memo must contain exactly 4 sections. The submitted document contains a substantial preamble ("Changes made and why") with 8 labeled sub-items. Even if framed as a cover note, this is part of the submitted document and violates the "exactly 4 sections" constraint as stated.

### Factual / Derivability Issues

**3. Scope item 2 adds no policy content.**
The constraint requires each section to have numbered items. Scope item 2 restates a survey statistic as a numbered item but conveys no rule, obligation, or scope boundary. It is filler that consumes word budget without governing anything.

**4. Permitted Uses item 4 introduces an "approved seat inventory" process.**
The base facts do not mention an approved seat inventory or any such tracking mechanism. This is an invented enforcement mechanism. The constraint prohibits adding anything not derivable from the base facts, and the constraint requiring enforceability without new tooling does not authorize inventing new administrative processes either.

**5. Enforcement item 1 places reporting duty on "the discovering employee or reviewer."**
The base facts provide no basis for assigning this duty to discovering employees generally. This is an invented obligation not derivable from the base facts.

### Missing Required Elements

**6. No prohibition references the outside counsel finding about AI-generated code copyrightability.**
The base facts explicitly state outside counsel flagged that AI-generated code may not be copyrightable. No prohibited use or any section references this fact, meaning not all base facts are used as required ("use ALL of these").

### Enforcement Weakness

**7. Enforcement item 2 scopes Legal escalation only to violations "involving customer data."**
Incident 2 (copyright violation) and Incident 3 (GPL license header) both carry legal liability but would not trigger Legal escalation under this language. This creates an unenforceable gap given the known incident types.

**8. "Within one business day" in Enforcement item 2 has no stated consequence for non-compliance.**
The policy must be enforceable. A deadline with no attached consequence or escalation path is not enforceable under the existing access controls and review processes cited.
