## Problems Found

### 1. The Document Contains Non-Policy Content
The document opens with a meta-commentary block ("I'll address each problem, then present the revised policy memo only." followed by a "Changes made" changelog). This is not part of the policy memo. The task requires a policy memo — not a revision history or editor's notes. This content violates the constraint that the output should be the policy document.

### 2. Word Count Likely Exceeds 500 Words
The policy body alone (excluding the meta-commentary preamble) is approximately 520–540 words. The constraint is a maximum of 500 words. Even generously excluding headers, the numbered items and section text exceed the limit. The changelog/preamble section adds further words if counted as part of the submitted document.

### 3. Enforcement Item 4 Is Not an Enforcement Item
Enforcement item 4 ("Manager and pull request reviewer approval of all AI-generated code and external content is mandatory before merge or delivery") restates a rule from Permitted Uses item 2. Enforcement sections should contain consequences and accountability mechanisms, not restatements of operational rules. This item does not describe any consequence, escalation, or enforcement mechanism — it is misplaced policy content.

### 4. Prohibited Uses Item 2 Motivation Is Incomplete or Misdirected
The motivating facts cited for "No unapproved AI tools" are Incidents #2 and #3. However, Incident #2 (copyrighted marketing copy) and Incident #3 (GPL header) are more directly about IP and copyright risks than about the use of unapproved tools per se. The actual base fact most directly motivating a ban on unapproved tools — outside counsel's finding that inputting customer data into third-party AI services likely violates DPA terms — is not cited here, even though it applies broadly to unapproved third-party services.

### 5. Scope Item 3 Adds No Enforceable Content
"Company Slack AI features are disabled and unavailable under this policy" is a factual status statement, not a policy rule. If the features are already disabled by configuration, no employee action is governed. This item does not create any obligation, prohibition, or scope boundary — it is filler.

### 6. Permitted Uses Item 3 Contains Aspirational-Adjacent Conditional Language
The phrase "Remaining budget *may* fund additional tools *only after* Legal confirms in writing" introduces a future conditional process. The constraint prohibits aspirational language and requires enforceability without new tooling. A budget allocation and approval process described here is not enforceable through existing access controls or review processes — it describes a future decision workflow that does not currently exist.

### 7. "IP Review" in Prohibited Uses Item 3 Header Is Undefined
The item header says "No AI-generated content merged or delivered without IP review," but no such formal IP review process is defined anywhere in the base facts or elsewhere in the policy. The actual mechanism described in the item body is standard pull request rejection and manager rejection — not a distinct "IP review." The label implies a process that doesn't exist.
