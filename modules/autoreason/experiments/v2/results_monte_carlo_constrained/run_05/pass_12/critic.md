## Problems Found

### Constraint Violations

**1. Word count likely exceeds 500 words.**
The body of the memo (excluding the "Synthesis rationale" block) runs approximately 430–450 words, which may be within limit, but the "Synthesis rationale" section at the bottom adds roughly 100 words. If the document is treated as a single submission, it violates the 500-word maximum. The rationale block has no basis in the task instructions and constitutes unexplained filler that should not exist in a policy memo.

**2. Prohibited Use #5 does not reference a base fact that motivates a prohibition.**
The motivating citation for the Slack item reads: "Slack AI features are currently disabled by company configuration; this policy codifies that state as a requirement." This is circular — it says the fact motivating the prohibition is that the thing is already prohibited. No base fact explains *why* Slack AI must remain disabled (e.g., no DPA flag, no incident, no compliance hook is cited). The constraint requires every prohibition to reference which base fact motivates it; this one does not satisfy that requirement.

**3. Permitted Uses item 2 is a prohibition, not a permitted use.**
Item 2 under Permitted Uses ("prompts and accepted suggestions must not include customer PII...") is a restriction/prohibition. Placing it under "Permitted Uses" violates the structural logic of the four required sections and blurs the boundary between Permitted and Prohibited Uses, creating an enforcement ambiguity.

**4. The 45% of sales using AI for email drafting has no corresponding permitted outlet.**
The base facts establish that 45% of sales already use AI for email drafting. The policy bans all unapproved tools and does not provide any approved tool for sales use cases. This is a policy gap, not a fixer — but it means the policy as written silently bans a widespread existing behavior without acknowledging the $50K budget or whether any tool could cover that use case, leaving the policy factually incomplete relative to the base facts it was required to use.

**5. The $50K budget is never referenced.**
The base facts state the budget as a given fact, and the constraint says to use all base facts. The budget figure does not appear anywhere in the document.

### Other Problems

**6. Enforcement section collapses items 1–2 and 3–5 into two tiers without explaining the distinction.**
Items 1 and 2 of Enforcement both end with "up to and including termination," making the two-tier framing meaningless — there is no actual differentiation in consequence between the tiers.

**7. "Condition of employment" in Enforcement item 4 is unenforceable without HR/legal process reference.**
The policy claims to be enforceable without new tooling using existing processes, but "condition of employment" is a legal term that implies contractual integration. No existing mechanism (offer letters, employment agreements, annual acknowledgment) is referenced, leaving it unanchored.
