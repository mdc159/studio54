## Problems Found

### 1. The document is a meta-commentary, not a policy memo
The task asks for a policy memo. The document begins with "I'll address each problem in turn, then present the revised document" followed by a changelog. This preamble is not part of a policy memo. A policy memo does not explain its own revision history. The preamble constitutes a significant portion of the document and is entirely outside the required format.

### 2. Word count almost certainly exceeds 500 words
The 500-word constraint applies to the policy itself. Even setting aside the preamble (which arguably should not count), the policy body alone — four sections with dense numbered items and lengthy parenthetical citations — appears to exceed 500 words. The Prohibited Uses section alone contains four items with multi-clause motivating-fact citations that are verbose. No word count is provided and the document gives no indication this constraint was verified.

### 3. Prohibited Uses item 4 introduces an approval process not derivable from base facts
Item 4 states "No new AI tool approved or used without Legal confirming in writing." The base facts do not establish a written Legal clearance process for tool approval. Outside counsel flagged risks, but no approval workflow involving Legal sign-off is derivable from the base facts. The constraint explicitly states "add nothing that isn't derivable from them." This prohibition invents a new process.

### 4. Enforcement item 1 treats violations of items 1 and 2 identically without factual basis
Lumping Prohibited Uses items 1 and 2 into a single identical enforcement consequence (immediate termination) treats a data privacy violation and an unapproved tool violation as equivalent. The base facts do not support this equivalence, and it creates an enforcement problem: terminating someone for using an unapproved writing tool on the same automatic basis as a PII breach is not credibly enforceable and is not supported by anything in the base facts.

### 5. Scope item 4 is redundant with Scope item 2 and Permitted Uses item 1
Scope item 4 ("Employees without an approved tool assignment have no permitted AI use for any work-related task") repeats the substance already stated in Scope item 2. Redundancy is not itself prohibited, but it pads word count in a 500-word-constrained document and weakens the claim that the document is under the limit.

### 6. The 45% sales AI usage statistic from the base facts is entirely unaddressed
The base facts state 45% of sales staff are already using AI for email drafting. This fact is not referenced anywhere in the policy — not in scope, not in prohibited uses, not in enforcement. The constraint says to use ALL base facts. Omitting this fact violates that constraint.

### 7. The $50K budget base fact is not used anywhere
The base facts include a $50K/year AI tooling budget. This fact appears nowhere in the policy. The constraint requires ALL base facts be used.
