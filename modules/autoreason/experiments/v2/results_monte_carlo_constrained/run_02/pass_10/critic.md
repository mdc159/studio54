## Problems Found

### 1. Document is not a policy memo
The deliverable opens with "I'll address each problem, then present the revised memo only—no preamble in the deliverable." followed by a bulleted change-log. This meta-commentary is not part of a policy memo and violates the task's implicit requirement that the output *is* the document, not a document plus editorial notes. The change-log appears in the deliverable itself, contradicting the sentence that promises it won't.

### 2. Word count likely exceeds 500 words
The change-log preamble adds roughly 150 words before the memo even begins. Even if only the memo body is counted, Permitted Use #3 is verbose ("The $50K annual AI tooling budget is allocated for AI tooling; no tool is approved for use without CTO written authorization" restates itself redundantly), and the full document is close to or over the 500-word limit. No word count is provided to verify compliance.

### 3. Enforcement section does not address sales/non-engineering violations
Prohibited Use #2 targets sales behavior (AI-generated outbound communications). The Enforcement section has no mechanism for detecting or escalating violations of Prohibited Use #2. Engineering Leads cannot enforce a prohibition on sales email drafting. The policy is therefore not enforceable for one of its three named incidents.

### 4. Prohibited Use #4 enforcement is absent
There is no Enforcement item corresponding to Prohibited Use #4 (unapproved AI tools). The Enforcement section covers only Prohibited Uses #1 and #3 explicitly. An unenforced prohibition undermines the CEO's requirement for an enforceable policy.

### 5. "Assessed by Legal" in Permitted Use #2 is not enforceable without new process
The constraint requires enforceability using *existing* access controls and review processes. Requiring Legal to assess copyright status on every pull request containing AI-generated code is not an existing review process — it implies a new legal review workflow that does not exist in the base facts. This violates the "no new tooling" / existing processes constraint.

### 6. Permitted Use #3 contains tautological language
"The $50K annual AI tooling budget is allocated for AI tooling" is a circular statement that adds no policy content. It does not constitute a numbered item with enforceable meaning, weakening the section's coherence.

### 7. Slack AI features are unaddressed
The base facts explicitly state company Slack has AI features disabled. This is a current access-control decision with policy relevance (employees are already using Slack; its AI features are a known vector). The policy neither acknowledges this as a prohibited use nor explains it as an existing control, leaving a gap between the base facts and the policy's coverage.

### 8. No effective date is populated
The memo lists `[DATE]` as the effective date. A policy memo submitted for review with a placeholder date is incomplete as a deliverable.
