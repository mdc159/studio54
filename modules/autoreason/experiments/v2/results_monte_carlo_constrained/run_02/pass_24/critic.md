## Problems Found

### 1. The Document Is Not a Policy Memo — It Is a Revision Commentary Attached to a Policy

The task asks for a policy memo. The document opens with a multi-paragraph editorial preamble ("I'll address each problem in turn…") listing eight "changes made" and "problems they fix." This preamble is not part of any policy memo; it references a prior draft that does not exist in this submission. The deliverable contains extraneous material that is not the requested document. A reader receiving this as a company policy would be confused by the opening section entirely.

---

### 2. Word Count Violation Is Probable

The constraint is a maximum of 500 words. The policy section alone (from "AI Tool Usage Policy" onward) is approximately 480–500 words depending on counting method, but the preamble adds several hundred more words to the document. If the word count applies to the entire submitted document — which is the natural reading when the task says "maximum 500 words" — the submission is substantially over limit. Even if only the policy text counts, the count is borderline and the preamble itself is unjustified filler.

---

### 3. Permitted Use #3 Is Prose, Not a Numbered Item

The constraint requires each section to have **numbered items, not prose paragraphs**. Permitted Use #3 contains three distinct rules compressed into a single unnumbered-style paragraph block: CTO approval process, FedRAMP restriction, and budget cap. These are separate obligations packed into one item in a way that violates the spirit and likely the letter of the "numbered items" constraint, which exists precisely to make a policy scannable and enforceable item by item.

---

### 4. Enforcement #1 Assigns Reporting Duty to "Managers" Without Definition

The constraint requires enforceability without new tooling, using existing processes. Enforcement #1 says "Managers report violations of Prohibited Use #1 to Legal immediately." The Scope section defines no managerial chain for non-engineering, non-sales staff ("50 other" employees). For that population, there is no identified manager role, making this item unenforceable as written for a portion of the 200-person company the policy explicitly covers.

---

### 5. Prohibited Use #3 Duplicates Permitted Use #2 Without Adding Prohibition Content

Prohibited Use #3 ("No AI-generated code committed without Engineering Lead pull-request approval") restates a condition already established in Permitted Use #2. A prohibition section should state what is forbidden; this item merely cross-references an affirmative requirement stated elsewhere. It adds no independent prohibition and creates potential interpretive conflict between the two sections.

---

### 6. The Preamble Cites "Problems" in a Prior Draft That Is Not Provided

The opening section references eight specific problems (e.g., "Problem 1 (word count)," "Problem 3 (DPA citation mismatch)") in a document that is not included in this submission. This implies the submitted policy is a revision of something, but no original is present. The self-referential commentary is therefore orphaned and meaningless to any actual reader of the policy.
