# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes Config CLI Tool

## Target Customer Segments (Priority Order)

### Primary: Platform Engineering Teams (SMB/Mid-Market)
- **Profile**: 50-500 employee companies with dedicated DevOps/Platform teams
- **Pain Point**: Manual, error-prone Kubernetes configuration management across environments
- **Buying Power**: $50K-200K annual tooling budgets
- **Decision Makers**: VP Engineering, DevOps Lead, Platform Engineering Manager

### Secondary: Individual DevOps Engineers/Consultants
- **Profile**: Senior engineers at smaller companies or independent consultants
- **Pain Point**: Need professional-grade tooling for client projects
- **Buying Power**: $500-5K annual personal/project budgets
- **Decision Makers**: Individual practitioners

### Tertiary: Enterprise Platform Teams (Future)
- **Profile**: 1000+ employee companies with mature Kubernetes adoption
- **Pain Point**: Standardization and governance at scale
- **Note**: Requires enterprise features not yet built

## Pricing Model

### Freemium with Commercial Add-ons
**Free Tier (Open Source Core)**

- Basic config validation
- Community support
- Unlimited personal use

**Professional Tier: $49/user/month**
- Advanced validation rules
- Team collaboration features
- Audit logging

**Team Tier: $199/month (up to 10 users)**
- Everything in Professional

- Custom integrations

## Distribution Channels

### Phase 1: Developer-Led Growth
1. **GitHub/Open Source Community**

   - Active issue response (<24 hours)

2. **Content Marketing**
   - Technical blog posts on Kubernetes best practices
   - Conference talks at KubeCon, DevOps Days
   - YouTube tutorials and demos

3. **Developer Communities**
   - Active participation in r/kubernetes, CNCF Slack
   - Kubernetes meetup presentations
   - Partnership with CNCF ecosystem

### Phase 2: Direct Sales (Month 6+)
- Inside sales for inbound leads >$10K ARR potential
- Product-led growth with in-app upgrade prompts

## What NOT to Do Yet

### Avoid These Premature Moves:

1. **Enterprise Sales Motion**
   - No dedicated enterprise sales team
   - No custom enterprise features
   - No 6-figure deal pursuit

2. **Platform Expansion**
   - Don't build for other orchestrators (Docker Swarm, etc.)
   - No GUI/web interface development
   - Avoid feature creep beyond core config management

3. **Geographic Expansion**
   - No international sales/marketing
   - No localization efforts
   - Focus solely on English-speaking markets

4. **Partnership Complexity**
   - No OEM/white-label partnerships
   - Avoid complex technology integrations
   - No reseller channel development

5. **Organizational Overhead**
   - No formal sales engineering team
   - No dedicated customer success managers
   - Keep legal/compliance minimal (SOC2 can wait)
