**MEMORANDUM**

**TO**: All Employees
**FROM**: [CEO Name], CEO
**DATE**: October 26, 2023
**SUBJECT**: Policy on the Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tools

This policy outlines the acceptable and prohibited uses of AI tools within the company. Its purpose is to enable productivity gains while mitigating security, legal, and compliance risks associated with AI technology, particularly concerning customer Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and Intellectual Property (IP). Outside counsel has flagged that code generated by AI tools may not be copyrightable (Base Fact: Legal review).

**1. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and other individuals working on behalf of the company when used in connection with company business. For the purposes of this policy, an "AI tool" is defined as any software or service that uses artificial intelligence or machine learning to generate code, text, images, or other content, or to automate tasks *specifically for use in software development or sales/marketing activities*. This includes, but is not limited to:

1.  AI coding assistants (e.g., tools that suggest code completions or generate code snippets).
2.  AI writing and communication tools used for generating drafts of written content.

**2. Permitted Uses**

The following uses of AI tools are permitted, subject to the restrictions outlined in Section 3:

1.  Use of GitHub Copilot Business subject to license availability (Base Fact: Already licensed). Employees can check license availability via the #ai-tool-access Slack channel. Engineering leadership will decide who gets access based on need.
2. Those in an engineering role that requires access to Github Copilot Business, but lacks it, should request access through the #ai-tool-access Slack channel.
3.  Sales and marketing staff may use AI tools to generate drafts of emails, provided that generated content is reviewed by a supervisor prior to sending (Base Fact: 45% of sales using AI for email drafting).

**3. Prohibited Uses**

The following uses of AI tools are strictly prohibited:

1.  Inputting any customer PII, financial data, or database schemas into any AI tool (Base Fact: Handles customer PII and financial data, GDPR obligations, Incident #1). This includes both real and synthetic PII.
2.  Using AI tools to generate content that infringes upon the intellectual property rights of others, including copyrighted text or verbatim use of a competitor's marketing copy (Base Fact: Incident #2, Legal review). "Verbatim" is defined as any phrase or sentence that is substantially similar to existing copyrighted material, as determined by the Legal Department.
3.  Using AI tools to generate code licensed under a copyleft license that would require the company to release the source code of its proprietary software (Base Fact: Incident #3, Legal Review).
4.  Using AI features within company Slack (Base Fact: Slack AI Features Disabled).
5.  Using any AI tool other than GitHub Copilot Business for code generation without express written permission from the Director of Engineering due to the potential for unvetted security risks (Base Fact: Security).
6.  Incorporating AI-generated code into the codebase without a review by an engineer with at least two years of experience at the company. The review must confirm that the code does not include verbatim content from another source (defined as in Section 3.2) and that the license is compatible with our existing codebase (Base Fact: Incident #3).

**4. Enforcement**

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract.

1.  Engineering managers will ensure that code review processes include a check to confirm that the code does not include verbatim content from another source (defined as in Section 3.2) and that the license is compatible with our existing codebase during the pull request review process, and before code is committed. Concerns regarding potential IP or licensing issues should be escalated to the Legal Department.
2.  Sales leadership will train sales representatives to compare AI-generated drafts against competitor marketing materials to identify potential copyright infringements. Sales leadership will review at least 10% of AI-assisted sales emails per representative monthly to ensure compliance. Training materials will cover examples of problematic content and steps for remediation.
3.  The Security Team will investigate alerts related to unusual data flows to external AI services, using existing network monitoring tools and data loss prevention (DLP) systems to detect unexpected data egress patterns.
4.  Employees are required to report any suspected violations of this policy to their manager or the Security Team.
5.  Non-compliance will result in a warning for the first offense unless the violation involves the input of customer PII or financial data into an unapproved AI tool, which will result in immediate suspension of access to relevant systems pending investigation. Repeat offenses will also result in suspension. Human Resources, in conjunction with the Legal Department and the relevant department head, will determine further disciplinary action based on the severity and frequency of the violation, as well as the employee's history with the company.
6.  The Security Team will conduct quarterly audits of a sample of code commits and sales emails to ensure compliance with this policy, reporting findings to the executive team. Audits will use automated tools to detect potential policy violations. The specific code repositories and types of sales emails audited will be rotated each quarter.
