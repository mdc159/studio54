MEMO: Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tool Usage Policy
Effective Date: [Current Date]

SCOPE
1. This policy applies to all employees of [Company Name], regardless of role or department.
2. The policy covers all AI tools, including but not limited to generative AI, coding assistants, and language models.
3. The policy applies to both company-provided and personal AI tools used for work purposes.
4. This policy is designed to balance productivity gains with security and intellectual property protection.

PERMITTED USES
1. GitHub Copilot Business is the only approved AI coding assistant, limited to 80 licensed seats.
2. AI tools may be used for:
   a. Code generation and completion
   b. Email drafting
   c. Process documentation
   d. General productivity enhancement
3. Users must manually review and validate all AI-generated content before use.
4. AI tool usage must comply with existing confidentiality and data protection agreements.
5. Employees must maintain a record of significant AI-assisted work in project documentation.

PROHIBITED USES
1. Do not input any customer personally identifiable information (PII) into AI tools (Ref: Base Fact #2, Incident #1).
2. Do not paste internal source code, database schemas, or proprietary technical documentation into third-party AI services.
3. Do not use AI-generated text without verifying originality and avoiding potential copyright infringement (Ref: Base Fact #2, Incident #2).
4. Do not commit AI-generated code without:
   a. Thorough manual review
   b. Verification of licensing compliance
   c. Removing any third-party license headers (Ref: Base Fact #2, Incident #3)
5. Do not use AI tools that are not explicitly approved by the IT Security team.
6. Do not use personal AI accounts for work-related tasks.
7. Do not bypass company data protection agreements by using unauthorized AI services.

ENFORCEMENT
1. Violations will be addressed through existing performance management processes.
2. First offense: Written warning and mandatory AI tool usage training.
3. Subsequent offenses may result in:
   a. Revocation of AI tool access
   b. Disciplinary action up to termination
4. Managers are responsible for monitoring and enforcing this policy within their teams.
5. IT Security will conduct quarterly audits of AI tool usage logs.
6. Employees must report potential policy violations to their manager or IT Security immediately.
