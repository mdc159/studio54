**Revised Company Policy Memo: AI Tool Usage**

**Scope**

1. This policy applies to all employees of [Company Name] and, where applicable, customers who interact with our systems, including those handling customer PII and financial data.
2. The policy governs the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools and coding assistants within the workplace.
3. Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all employees, subject to the company's Code of Conduct and other applicable policies.

**Permitted Uses**

1. Employees may use AI coding assistants, such as GitHub Copilot Business, for productivity purposes, provided they are licensed and properly configured.
2. Employees may use company-approved AI-powered communication tools, such as Slack, without AI features enabled.
3. Employees may use approved AI tools for research and development purposes, subject to approval from their manager, the CTO, and the following criteria:
	* The project has a clear business justification and has been approved by management.
	* The manager has reviewed and approved the technical requirements and risk assessment.
	* The CTO has reviewed and approved the technical feasibility and security implications.

**Prohibited Uses**

1. **Use of AI tools that handle customer PII or financial data** without explicit approval from management and the CTO (motivated by compliance obligations, e.g., GDPR).
2. **Inputting customer data into third-party AI services**, as this likely violates existing Data Processing Agreement (DPA) terms (motivated by outside counsel review).
3. **Using AI-generated code that includes copyrighted or licensed materials** without proper attribution and licensing. This includes, but is not limited to, GPL headers, which require explicit permission from the copyright holder (motivated by incident 3).
4. **Using AI-generated text that includes competitor's copyrighted marketing copy verbatim** without proper permission or licensure (motivated by incident 2).
5. **Sharing customer databases or sensitive information with unauthorized third-party AI services**, including but not limited to ChatGPT (motivated by incident 1).
6. **Using AI tools in a way that compromises customer data or system security**, including but not limited to, introducing malware or using tools with known security vulnerabilities.

**Enforcement**

1. Employees must comply with this policy, and failure to do so may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, which may include:
	* Verbal warning
	* Written warning
	* Mandatory training or coaching
	* Suspension
	* Termination
2. Managers and employees are responsible for reporting any non-compliance or incidents related to AI tool usage.
3. The CTO and management will monitor AI tool usage and update the policy as necessary to ensure compliance with company obligations and security requirements. This will be done in conjunction with regular compliance audits.
4. The company will provide regular training and awareness programs, at least quarterly, to educate employees on AI tool usage, security best practices, and compliance obligations.
