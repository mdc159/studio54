**Revised Company Policy Memo: AI Tool Usage**

**Scope**

1. This policy applies to all employees of [Company Name], including but not limited to 120 engineers, 30 sales representatives, and 50 other employees, in all departments and locations.
2. The policy governs the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools, including coding assistants, chatbots, and other software that leverages AI functionality, within the workplace.
3. Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all employees, subject to the company's Code of Conduct and other applicable policies.

**Permitted Uses**

1. Employees may use GitHub Copilot Business and other AI coding assistants licensed by the company for productivity purposes, provided they are properly configured and used in accordance with the company's approved usage guidelines.
2. Employees may use company-approved AI-powered communication tools, such as Slack, without AI features enabled.
3. Employees may use approved AI tools for research and development purposes, subject to approval from their manager and the CTO.
4. The company will maintain a list of approved AI tools and services, which will be made available to employees on the company's intranet.

**Prohibited Uses**

1. **Use of AI tools that handle customer PII or financial data** without explicit approval from management and the CTO (motivated by compliance obligations, e.g., GDPR).
2. **Inputting customer data into third-party AI services**, as this likely violates existing Data Processing Agreement (DPA) terms (motivated by outside counsel review).
3. **Using AI-generated code that includes copyrighted or licensed materials**, such as GPL headers, without proper attribution and licensing (motivated by incident 3).
4. **Using AI-generated text that includes competitor's copyrighted marketing copy verbatim**, without proper permission or licensure (motivated by incident 2).
5. **Sharing customer databases or sensitive information with unauthorized third-party AI services**, including but not limited to ChatGPT (motivated by incident 1).
6. **Using AI tools that have not been approved by the company**, including but not limited to any AI tools that have not been added to the company's approved list (motivated by lack of specificity).

**Enforcement**

1. employees must comply with this policy, and failure to do so may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
2. Managers and employees are responsible for reporting any non-compliance or incidents related to AI tool usage to the CTO and management.
3. The CTO and management will monitor AI tool usage and update the policy as necessary to ensure compliance with company obligations and security requirements.
4. The company will provide regular training and awareness programs to educate employees on AI tool usage, security best practices, and compliance obligations.

**Budget and Allocation**

The company has allocated $50K/year for AI tooling, which will be used to license and purchase approved AI tools and services. The CTO is responsible for managing the AI tool budget and ensuring that only approved AI tools are used.

**Reporting Mechanisms**

Employees are required to report any incidents or non-compliance related to AI tool usage to the CTO and management via email or the company's incident reporting system.

**Consequences for Managers**

Managers who fail to enforce this policy or report incidents may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

**Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Agreements**

Employees using AI-generated code or text must ensure that it does not violate confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements. Any AI-generated code or text that may violate such agreements must be reviewed and approved by the relevant stakeholders before use.

**Training for AI Tools**

The company will provide regular training and awareness programs to educate employees on AI tool usage, security best practices, and compliance obligations.

3. **Insufficient specificity**: Added specific guidance on approved AI tools, usage guidelines, and approval processes.
4. **Missing elements**: Added the budget constraints and allocation of the $50K/year budget for AI tooling.
5. **Inconsistent tone**: Removed aspirational language and used consistent, mandatory language throughout the policy.
