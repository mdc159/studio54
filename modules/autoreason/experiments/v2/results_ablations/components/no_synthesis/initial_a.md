# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes Configuration Management CLI

## Executive Summary

This strategy focuses on converting your existing 5K GitHub community into a sustainable revenue stream through a freemium model targeting DevOps engineers and platform teams at mid-market companies. The approach emphasizes product-led growth while building enterprise features for monetization.

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary Segment: Mid-Market DevOps Teams (50-500 employees)
- **Profile**: Companies with 5-20 developers, 2-5 Kubernetes clusters
- **Pain Points**: Manual config management, environment drift, compliance auditing
- **Budget**: $2K-10K annual tooling budget per team
- **Decision Makers**: DevOps leads, Engineering managers
- **Buying Process**: Bottom-up adoption, 30-90 day evaluation cycles

### Secondary Segment: Platform Engineering Teams at Growth Companies
- **Profile**: 100-1000 employees, multiple product teams
- **Pain Points**: Standardization across teams, governance, security policies
- **Budget**: $10K-50K annual platform tooling budget
- **Decision Makers**: Platform Engineering leads, VPE, CTO
- **Buying Process**: Formal procurement, 60-180 day cycles

### Tertiary Segment: Individual Contributors & Small Teams
- **Profile**: Startups, consultants, side projects
- **Value**: Free tier users who provide feedback and word-of-mouth marketing
- **Conversion Path**: Grow into primary segment or influence purchasing decisions

## Pricing Model

### Freemium Structure

**Community Edition (Free)**
- Core CLI functionality
- Basic config validation
- Local development features
- Community support
- Up to 3 environments per workspace

**Professional ($49/user/month)**
- Advanced validation rules
- Environment promotion workflows
- Audit logging
- Team collaboration features
- Slack/Teams integrations
- Email support
- Up to 10 environments

**Enterprise ($149/user/month)**
- SSO/SAML integration
- Custom policy enforcement
- Advanced RBAC
- API access
- Priority support
- Professional services credits
- Unlimited environments
- Air-gapped deployment options

### Pricing Rationale
- Aligns with existing DevOps tool pricing (Terraform Cloud, GitLab)
- Per-user model scales with team growth
- Clear value differentiation between tiers
- Enterprise pricing allows for negotiation and custom deals

## What We Explicitly Won't Do (Year 1)

### Product Limitations
- **No multi-cloud support**: Focus exclusively on Kubernetes, avoid AWS/Azure/GCP native tools
- **No enterprise sales team**: Keep to founder-led sales until $100K ARR
- **No custom development**: Resist customer requests for bespoke features
- **No acquisition strategy**: Don't pursue competitors or complementary tools

### Market Constraints
- **No SMB focus**: Avoid customers with <$1K annual budget potential
- **No international expansion**: Stay North America focused for support/sales
- **No channel partnerships**: Avoid reseller agreements until proven direct model
- **No venture capital**: Bootstrap to maintain control and validate model

### Operational Boundaries
- **No remote team expansion**: Keep team co-located for tight collaboration
- **No trade shows beyond 3**: Limit conference spend to proven ROI events
- **No PR agency**: Handle all communications in-house
- **No office space**: Maintain current cost structure
