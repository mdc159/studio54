# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes Config CLI Tool

## Executive Summary

This GTM strategy focuses on converting your existing developer mindshare into sustainable revenue through a freemium model targeting DevOps engineers at mid-market companies. With 5k GitHub stars as social proof, we'll build revenue through managed services and enterprise features while maintaining the open-source core.

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary: Mid-Market DevOps Teams (50-500 employees)
**Characteristics:**
- 2-10 person DevOps/Platform teams
- Managing 10-100 Kubernetes clusters
- Annual revenue $10M-$100M
- Currently using mix of kubectl, Helm, and custom scripts
- Pain points: Config drift, environment consistency, compliance auditing

**Decision makers:** DevOps Lead, Engineering Director
**Budget authority:** $10K-$50K annually for tooling
**Buying process:** Bottom-up adoption, 30-90 day evaluation cycles

### Secondary: Platform Engineering Teams at Scale-ups (100-1000 employees)
**Characteristics:**
- 5-20 person platform teams
- Managing 100+ clusters across multiple environments
- Series B-D companies with established engineering practices
- Need governance, standardization, and developer self-service

**Decision makers:** VP Engineering, Platform Engineering Lead
**Budget authority:** $50K-$200K annually
**Buying process:** RFP-driven, 90-180 day cycles

### Tertiary: Kubernetes Consultancies
**Characteristics:**
- 10-100 person consulting firms
- Managing client Kubernetes environments
- Need white-label or multi-tenant capabilities
- Value proposition: Faster client delivery, reduced operational overhead

## Pricing Model

### Tier 1: Open Source (Free)
- Core CLI functionality
- Basic config management
- Community support via GitHub/Discord
- **Goal:** Maintain adoption funnel, gather usage data

### Tier 2: Professional ($49/month per cluster)
- Advanced validation rules
- Config drift detection and alerts
- Slack/Teams integrations
- Email support with 48-hour SLA
- Usage analytics dashboard
- **Target:** Mid-market teams managing 5-20 clusters

### Tier 3: Enterprise ($149/month per cluster, minimum 10 clusters)
- RBAC and audit logging
- SSO/SAML integration
- GitOps workflow automation
- Custom validation policies
- Dedicated support with 4-hour SLA
- Professional services credits
- **Target:** Large teams with compliance requirements

### Tier 4: Managed Service ($299/month per cluster)
- Fully managed config management
- 24/7 monitoring and alerting
- Incident response
- Monthly optimization reviews
- **Target:** Teams wanting to outsource operations

## What We Will NOT Do Yet

### ❌ Enterprise Sales Team
**Why not:** With only 3 people, focus on product-led growth and technical sales engineers. Traditional enterprise sales requires 6-12 month cycles that don't match our current resources.

### ❌ Multiple Product Lines
**Why not:** Resist expanding into adjacent tools (monitoring, security, etc.). Master Kubernetes config management first before horizontal expansion.

### ❌ On-Premise Deployment Options
**Why not:** Cloud-first approach reduces operational complexity. On-premise requests indicate enterprise readiness but require significant support infrastructure.

### ❌ White-Label/OEM Partnerships
**Why not:** Complex to implement and support. Focus on building direct brand recognition first.

### ❌ International Expansion
**Why not:** English-speaking markets provide sufficient TAM. International expansion requires localization, compliance, and support infrastructure.

### ❌ Freemium Feature Restrictions
**Why not:** Don't cripple the open-source version. Success depends on genuine adoption, not artificial limitations driving upgrades.

### ❌ Professional Services Team
**Why not:** Partner with existing consultancies rather than building internal services capacity. Maintain focus on product development.
