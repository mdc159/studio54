# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes Config CLI Tool

## Executive Summary

This strategy focuses on converting existing community momentum into sustainable revenue through a freemium enterprise model, targeting DevOps teams at mid-to-large companies while maintaining open-source community growth.

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary Segment: Mid-Market DevOps Teams (50-500 employees)
**Profile:**
- Companies with 5-50 Kubernetes clusters
- DevOps teams of 3-15 engineers
- Annual infrastructure spend: $100K-$2M
- Pain points: Config drift, compliance auditing, multi-environment management
- Budget authority: Engineering managers with $50K+ tool budgets

**Why this segment:**
- Large enough to pay for tools but small enough to move quickly
- Complex enough to need advanced features but not requiring enterprise sales cycles
- Often have dedicated DevOps budget lines

### Secondary Segment: Platform Engineering Teams at Large Enterprises (500+ employees)
**Profile:**
- Companies with 50+ clusters across multiple business units
- Platform teams serving 50+ internal developers
- Strict compliance requirements (SOC2, HIPAA, PCI)
- Budget authority: Director/VP level with $500K+ budgets

### Tertiary Segment: Kubernetes Consultancies
**Profile:**
- 10-100 person consulting firms
- Managing configs for 5-20 clients simultaneously
- Need white-labeling and multi-tenant capabilities
- Reseller potential for enterprise accounts

## Pricing Model

### Freemium Structure

**Open Source (Free Forever):**
- Core CLI functionality
- Basic config validation
- Single cluster management
- Community support
- GitHub integration

**Professional ($49/user/month):**
- Multi-cluster management
- Config drift detection
- RBAC and audit logs
- Slack/Teams integrations
- Email support
- Advanced templating

**Enterprise ($149/user/month):**
- SAML/SSO integration
- Advanced compliance reporting
- Custom policy enforcement
- Priority support + SLA
- On-premise deployment option
- Professional services credits

### Rationale:
- Low Professional tier captures mid-market quickly
- Clear feature differentiation drives upgrades
- Per-user pricing scales with team growth
- Enterprise tier captures large accounts without complex negotiations

## What We Will Explicitly NOT Do Yet

### No Direct Sales Team
- Avoid hiring expensive enterprise sales reps
- Focus on product-led growth and inbound leads
- Use founders for enterprise conversations until $500K ARR

### No Custom Development/Professional Services
- Resist requests for custom feature development
- Avoid becoming a consulting company
- Focus on product scalability over one-off solutions

### No Broad Market Expansion
- Don't target small startups (<10 engineers) - insufficient budget
- Avoid non-Kubernetes container orchestration tools
- Skip adjacent markets (monitoring, logging) until core is established

### No Complex Enterprise Features Initially
- Skip multi-tenancy until clear enterprise demand
- Avoid complex approval workflows
- Don't build custom reporting until Q3

### No Geographic Expansion
- Focus only on English-speaking markets initially
- Avoid GDPR complexity with EU expansion
- Skip APAC until product-market fit is proven

### No Alternative Pricing Models
- Don't experiment with usage-based pricing initially
- Avoid freemium limits (seat caps, time limits)
- Skip annual discount programs until Q3
