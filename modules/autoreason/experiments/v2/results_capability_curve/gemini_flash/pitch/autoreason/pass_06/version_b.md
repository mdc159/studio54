*   **Paid Tier:** "Team Pro" at $99/month. Includes:
    *   Unlimited configurations managed.
    *   Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) for up to 5 team members.
    *   Priority support (email, 24-hour response time).
    *   Audit logging of configuration changes.
    *   Integration with CI/CD pipelines (e.g., GitLab CI, CircleCI).
    *   Configuration Policy Engine: Define and enforce custom configuration policies as code, automatically preventing deployments that violate compliance requirements (e.g., preventing deployments without proper annotations for cost tracking, enforcing specific security settings, validating resource limits). *The Policy Engine includes pre-built policies specifically designed for SOC2 and PCI compliance*, addressing a key pain point for FinTech companies facing audits. *Our implementation offers a UI for non-programmers to configure OPA policies, unlike raw OPA which requires writing Rego code. The UI will include templates for common compliance requirements such as preventing the use of privileged containers and enforcing network policies*.
    *   Integration with Sentry and PagerDuty: Automatically log and alert on configuration violations, ensuring immediate awareness of potential issues. *Alerts will be grouped and prioritized based on the severity of the compliance violation to reduce alert fatigue*. *Each alert will include a link to relevant documentation and remediation steps*.
    *   *Audit Report Generation: Automatically generate reports detailing Kubernetes configuration compliance status for SOC2/PCI audits. These reports can be directly submitted to auditors*.

The alert prioritization and remediation guidance reduce alert fatigue and ensure engineers can quickly address critical configuration issues. The audit report generation feature *directly saves time and effort in preparing for audits*, a key value proposition for companies facing imminent deadlines. *We will provide a sample audit report on the pricing page to demonstrate the value of this feature*.

**5. What You Won't Do:**

*   **Enterprise Sales:** Avoid proactive enterprise sales efforts. *Large enterprises have complex, highly customized Kubernetes environments and often require extensive integration with existing security and compliance tools. Supporting these environments would require significant engineering resources and divert focus from the target customer segment*. Will respond to inbound, but qualify heavily on team size, audit timeline, and willingness to self-serve.
*   **Extensive Feature Development:** Don't chase every feature request. Prioritize features that directly *improve audit compliance*, *enhance security*, and *integrate with existing CI/CD pipelines and compliance tools*. *Avoid features that don't directly contribute to simplifying SOC2/PCI audits or preventing compliance violations*. We will ruthlessly prioritize features based on their impact on audit readiness.
*   **Broad Paid Advertising:** Avoid broad paid advertising campaigns (e.g., Google Ads targeting generic Kubernetes keywords). The cost per acquisition (CPA) would likely be too high compared to our targeted content marketing and partner-driven approach. *We WILL consider hyper-targeted Google Ads campaigns focused on *very specific* long-tail keywords related to configuration errors and SOC2/PCI compliance, but with a strict budget of $500/month*. *We will bid on keywords such as "Kubernetes SOC2 configuration checklist" and "PCI compliance Kubernetes best practices." We will track the CPA and ROI of these campaigns closely and adjust our bidding strategy accordingly*.

**6. Biggest Risk:**

*   **Risk:** Inability to demonstrate clear and measurable *audit compliance* value, leading to low conversion rates and churn. Platform engineers may not perceive a significant improvement in their ability to *pass their SOC2/PCI audits* after using the tool.
*   **Mitigation:** Focus relentlessly on gathering data from early adopters to quantify the *audit compliance* benefits of the tool. Develop a robust reporting dashboard that allows users to track configuration violations, *compliance gaps identified*, and overall improvement in audit readiness. *Proactively engage with customers to understand their audit requirements and tailor the tool to meet their specific needs*.
*   **Metric to Watch:** Conversion rate from free to paid users, churn rate, *audit success rate among paying customers*, *customer satisfaction with the audit report generation feature*, AND leading indicators such as the *number of SOC2/PCI policy violations detected per week* by the policy engine. Low conversion rates and high churn indicate that the perceived value is not high enough. *We will proactively solicit feedback from customers after their audits to understand how the tool helped them and identify areas for improvement*. *If the number of policy violations detected decreases dramatically after initial setup, it may indicate that our pre-built policies are too strict or that users are simply disabling them*. *We need to track instances of blocked deployments due to validation failures*.
