**MEMORANDUM**

**TO**: All Employees
**FROM**: [CEO Name], CEO
**DATE**: October 26, 2023
**SUBJECT**: Policy on the Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tools

This policy outlines the approved and prohibited uses of AI tools at [Company Name]. Its purpose is to balance the potential productivity benefits of AI with the critical need to protect company and customer data, intellectual property, and maintain regulatory compliance.

**1. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and other individuals working on behalf of [Company Name]. It covers all AI tools, including but not limited to: coding assistants, natural language processing models, and generative AI applications. All employees are responsible for understanding and adhering to this policy, regardless of their department.

**2. Permitted Uses**

The following AI tools are approved for use, subject to the restrictions outlined in the "Prohibited Uses" section:

1.  GitHub Copilot Business (licensed seats only). This is the only approved AI coding assistant. (Base Fact: Approved tools)
2.  AI features within company-approved platforms that do *not* involve submitting prompts or data covered in Prohibited Uses. This includes, but is not limited to, grammar/spell checking in approved word processors or spreadsheet formula suggestions. (Base Fact: Current state)
3.  Use of AI for internal documentation and summarization that does not involve disclosing confidential information.

**3. Prohibited Uses**

The following actions are strictly prohibited when using AI tools:

1.  Inputting any customer Personally Identifiable Information (PII) or financial data into any AI tool. This includes, but is not limited to, names, addresses, email addresses, credit card numbers, database schemas, or any data subject to GDPR or SOC2 compliance. This prohibition includes paraphrasing or rephrasing sensitive data in an attempt to circumvent this rule. (Base Fact: Handles customer PII and financial data, Compliance, Legal review, Incident 1)
2.  Using AI tools to generate code or content that infringes on the intellectual property rights of others, including competitors. (Base Fact: Incident 2, Legal review)
3.  Using AI tools that generate code with incompatible open-source licenses (e.g., GPL) without explicit approval from the Engineering leadership and Legal. All AI-generated code must be reviewed for license compliance. "Explicit approval" requires a documented exception approved by the VP of Engineering and the General Counsel. (Base Fact: Incident 3, Legal review)
4.  Using any AI tool for which the company does not have a business agreement (e.g., ChatGPT, Bard) without explicit approval from the Security and Legal teams. To request approval, submit a detailed justification to the Security team (security@example.com) outlining the tool, its intended use, and data security measures. (Base Fact: Approved tools)
5.  Circumventing existing data security protocols or access controls to use AI tools. (Base Fact: Enforceable without new tooling)
6.  Sharing internal company data, including code, documentation, or business strategies, with any public or third-party AI service. (Base Fact: Legal review)

**4. Enforcement**

1.  Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract.
2.  Engineering managers are responsible for ensuring their teams adhere to this policy during code reviews and development processes, including verifying license compliance of AI-generated code. (Base Fact: Enforceable without new tooling)
3.  Sales managers are responsible for reviewing AI-generated content for compliance and accuracy, including verifying that content does not violate copyright. (Base Fact: Enforceable without new tooling)
4.  The Security team will periodically audit AI tool usage through analysis of network traffic logs, code repository activity, and conducting employee surveys. (Base Fact: Enforceable without new tooling)
5.  This policy will be reviewed and updated at least annually, or more frequently as needed due to the evolving nature of AI technology. The Security team will manage the review process.
