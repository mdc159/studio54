**MEMORANDUM**

**TO**: All Employees, Contractors, and Temporary Staff
**FROM**: [CEO Name], Chief Executive Officer
**DATE**: October 26, 2023
**SUBJECT**: **Mandatory Policy: Responsible and Ethical Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tools**

This policy establishes mandatory guidelines for the responsible, ethical, secure, legally compliant, and business-aligned use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools within [Company Name]. **This policy is effective immediately. Strict adherence is required and will be actively monitored to protect company assets, intellectual property, confidential data (including customer and employee data), maintain customer trust, and ensure compliance with all applicable legal, regulatory, ethical, contractual, and industry-specific obligations.** Violation of this policy will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract, and potential legal penalties, as outlined in the [Company Name] Employee Handbook and Contractor Agreement terms. This policy will be reviewed and updated no less than quarterly, or more frequently as needed, to address evolving AI technologies, risks, and regulatory landscapes. The AI Governance Committee, in collaboration with the Legal and Compliance departments, is responsible for managing these reviews and updates, with input from relevant stakeholders. All updates will be communicated via company-wide email, posted on the company intranet, and require mandatory acknowledgment of receipt and understanding from all Personnel via [Designated Training Platform/Method, e.g., the company's Learning Management System (LMS)]. Acknowledgment must be completed within *5* business days of the policy update announcement. Failure to acknowledge receipt will result in a temporary suspension of access to company systems until acknowledgment is received.

**1. Purpose and Scope**

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, temporary staff, consultants, and other individuals or entities (collectively, "Personnel") accessing or using AI tools in *any* way related to [Company Name], regardless of location or device. This includes, but is not limited to, using AI tools for company business, accessing company resources (including cloud-based services), on company property, or using AI tools that process or interact with [Company Name]'s data, systems, or intellectual property. This policy applies to *any* AI tool used, accessed, developed, or deployed by Personnel, whether the tool is company-provided, personally owned, or accessed via personal accounts. The policy covers *all* stages of the AI lifecycle, from initial exploration and development through training, deployment, monitoring, and eventual decommissioning.

**No exceptions to this policy are permitted without *prior written approval* from the AI Governance Committee, granted only under *specifically defined* and documented exceptional circumstances and subject to stringent, auditable controls.** All exception requests must be submitted via the AI Policy Exception Request Form (available at [Link to Form, e.g., a SharePoint form on the company intranet] – *[Company Intranet Address]/AI_Policy_Exception_Request_Form*) and must include *all* of the following: a detailed justification, a comprehensive risk assessment (using the AI Risk Assessment Framework), a proposed mitigation plan with specific, measurable controls, a detailed data flow diagram, and a sign-off from the relevant business unit leader, the IT Security team, the Data Governance team, and the Legal department. The AI Governance Committee will maintain a centralized, auditable log of *all* exception requests, approvals, and denials, including the rationale for each decision, the conditions of approval, and the expiration date (if any). This log will be stored in [Designated System, e.g., a secure database accessible only to authorized personnel] – *[Secure Database Name]/AI_Exception_Log, with access restricted to AI Governance Committee members, IT Security, Legal, and Internal Audit personnel*. Approvals will be granted on a case-by-case basis, will be time-bound (no longer than *60 days*), and will be subject to regular review and audit (no less than monthly) by the Internal Audit department. Any approved exception must be documented in the Approved AI Tool Registry with clear conditions and limitations. The AI Governance Committee will notify IT Security of all approved exceptions for implementation of appropriate monitoring and controls. Notification will occur via an automated alert generated by the AI Exception Log system and routed directly to the IT Security team's ticketing system ([Designated Ticketing System, e.g., ServiceNow]). The IT Security team will acknowledge receipt of the alert within *4* business hours and implement the required controls within *2* business days, documenting the implementation in the ticketing system and providing confirmation to the AI Governance Committee.

This policy encompasses, but is *not* limited to, the following AI tool categories *and any similar technologies regardless of specific name or marketing label*:

*   AI coding assistants (e.g., GitHub Copilot, AWS CodeWhisperer)
*   AI writing and content generation tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Grammarly, Jasper)
*   AI-powered search engines and knowledge retrieval systems (e.g., Perplexity AI)
*   AI-driven data analysis and business intelligence tools (e.g., Tableau CRM, Power BI with AI capabilities)
*   AI-enabled communication platforms (e.g., chatbots, virtual assistants)
*   AI tools integrated into other software applications (e.g., AI features in CRM, ERP systems)
*   AI-powered image, audio, or video generation and manipulation tools (e.g., DALL-E 2, Stable Diffusion, RunwayML)
*   AI-powered tools for automated decision-making (e.g., credit scoring, risk assessment, fraud detection)
*   AI tools used in research and development (e.g., drug discovery, materials science)
*   Robotic Process Automation (RPA) tools with AI capabilities
*   Any other tool leveraging artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, or natural language processing.
*   AI models, whether pre-trained, fine-tuned, or custom-built, including foundation models and generative AI models.
*   AI-powered cybersecurity tools (e.g., threat detection, vulnerability assessment).
*   **Specifically excluded:** Basic calculator functions or spreadsheet software (e.g., Excel's AVERAGE function) used *solely* for simple calculations and reporting that do *not* involve predictive modeling, complex data analysis, or automated decision-making with significant business impact, *and that do not access or process any Sensitive Data*. *Any use of spreadsheet software beyond these explicitly defined parameters is subject to this policy*. The IT department will maintain a locked-down, pre-approved version of spreadsheet software on company devices to ensure users do not inadvertently use unauthorized AI add-ins. This version will be deployed and managed via [Designated Software Deployment Tool, e.g., Microsoft Endpoint Manager] with a configuration policy that restricts the installation of unauthorized add-ins and monitors for attempts to circumvent these restrictions. The IT Help Desk will provide support for this approved version and address any user issues within *8* business hours.

**2. Definitions**

*   **AI Tool:** *For the purposes of this policy*, any software, platform, service, application, model, or algorithm (including pre-trained, fine-tuned, or custom-built models) that uses artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, or natural language processing to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence *and* that could potentially impact [Company Name]'s data, systems, reputation, legal compliance, financial performance, strategic objectives, customer relationships, or employee well-being. This includes, but is not limited to, tools used for text generation (e.g., creating marketing copy, drafting legal documents), code completion (e.g., suggesting code snippets, automated debugging), image/video/audio creation/manipulation (e.g., generating stock photos/music/voiceovers, deepfakes), data analysis (e.g., predicting customer churn, identifying market trends), decision-making (e.g., credit scoring, automated recruitment), process automation (e.g., automating customer support responses, RPA), and cybersecurity (e.g., threat detection, fraud prevention). *Note:* Basic statistical functions or spreadsheet software (e.g., Excel's AVERAGE function), used *solely* for simple calculations and reporting that do *not* involve predictive modeling, complex data analysis, or automated decision-making with significant business impact, generally do *not* fall under this definition *unless they access or process any Sensitive Data*. *If you are unsure if a technology falls under this definition, you are required to consult with the AI Governance Committee prior to its use.* Consultation is initiated by submitting a detailed inquiry via the AI Tool Clarification Request Form (available at [Link to Form, e.g., a ServiceNow request form] – *[ServiceNow Instance Address]/AI_Tool_Clarification_Request*), including a comprehensive description of the tool, its intended use case *with a detailed process flow diagram created using [Designated Diagramming Tool, e.g., Lucidchart]*, data inputs and outputs *including examples of data to be used (e.g., sample customer records, anonymized data sets)*, and potential risks *assessed using the AI Risk Assessment Framework*. A determination will be made within *4* business days *by the AI Governance Committee, in consultation with the IT Security and Legal departments*. *The AI Governance Committee's decision is binding and will be documented and communicated to the requestor via email, stored in the AI Tool Clarification Request Log (accessible to Internal Audit), and added to a knowledge base for future reference accessible via [Designated Knowledge Base, e.g., Confluence page] -- *[Confluence Instance Address]/AI_Tool_Knowledge_Base*. If the AI Governance Committee requires additional information, the timeframe for determination will be extended by *2* business days, and the requestor will be notified accordingly via automated email generated by the ServiceNow request system.
*   **Approved AI Tool:** An AI Tool that has been explicitly authorized for use by [Company Name] for *specific, documented purposes* and under *specific, documented conditions*, and is listed in the Approved AI Tool Registry (accessible via [Link to Registry, e.g., a secure SharePoint list] – *[SharePoint Instance Address]/Approved_AI_Tool_Registry, access restricted to AI Governance Committee members, IT Security, Legal, Data Governance, and Internal Audit*). *The Approved AI Tool Registry is the only source of truth for approved AI tools.* Approval is tool-specific and use-case specific. *Approval for one purpose does not imply approval for any other purpose. Each new use case requires a separate approval*. Approval is contingent on a thorough risk assessment (using the AI Risk Assessment Framework), documented data handling safeguards (including data encryption, access controls, and data loss prevention measures implemented using [Designated Security Tools, e.g., Microsoft Purview, CrowdStrike] – *specifically, Microsoft Purview Information Protection for data classification and DLP policies, and CrowdStrike Falcon for endpoint detection and response*), compliance with *all* applicable laws and regulations, adherence to this entire policy, and the tool-specific requirements detailed in the Approved AI Tool Registry. The Registry will include *at a minimum*: the date of approval, the expiration date (if any), the approved use cases *with detailed process descriptions in a standardized format using [Designated Documentation Template] – *[SharePoint Instance Address]/AI_Process_Documentation_Template.docx*), the approved data types *with specific examples and classification levels referencing the Data Classification Policy*, any restrictions on data usage, required security controls *including specific configuration settings and documented procedures for implementation*, monitoring requirements *including key performance indicators and thresholds monitored via [Designated Monitoring Tool, e.g., Splunk] – *Splunk Enterprise Security with pre-defined dashboards and alerts for AI tool usage, data access, and policy violations*), the designated point of contact for support and issue reporting (including escalation procedures), and a link to the documented risk assessment and mitigation plan. The Approved AI Tool Registry will be reviewed and updated no less than monthly *by the AI Governance Committee in conjunction with IT Security and Data Governance, and changes will be communicated to all personnel via the designated communication channel (company-wide email and intranet posting) and highlighted in the monthly AI Policy Update Newsletter*.
*   **Sensitive Data:** Data that, if compromised (e.g., through unauthorized access, disclosure, loss, alteration, or misuse), could cause significant harm to [Company Name], its employees, customers, partners, or reputation, *or* that is protected by law, regulation, or contract. This includes, but is not limited to, Personally Identifiable Information (PII) *as defined by GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable privacy laws*, financial data (e.g., credit card numbers, bank account details), Protected Health Information (PHI) *as defined by HIPAA*, proprietary data, trade secrets, confidential business information, internal credentials, legal documents subject to attorney-client privilege, employee HR data, security information, customer data, source code, algorithms, AI model weights, training data, product roadmaps, pricing information, supplier agreements, and any data classified as "Confidential" or "Restricted" according to the [Company Name] Data Classification Policy. Failure to do so will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment*. A consultation request must be submitted via the Data Governance Help Desk ([Link to Help Desk, e.g., the Data Governance team's ServiceNow queue] – *[ServiceNow Instance Address]/Data_Governance_Help_Desk*) and include a detailed description of the data in question, its source, its intended use, and any potential risks *including a preliminary assessment using the AI Risk Assessment Framework*. The Data Governance team will respond to the request within *24* business hours.

Even for Internal Use, Personnel must adhere to all data privacy and security protocols.
