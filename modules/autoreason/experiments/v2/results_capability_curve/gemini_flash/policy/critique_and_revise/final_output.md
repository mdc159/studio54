**2. Definitions**

For the purposes of this policy, the following definitions apply:

*   **AI Tool:** Any software, platform, or service that uses artificial intelligence, machine learning, or natural language processing to perform tasks, generate content, or provide insights. *This includes both cloud-based (SaaS) and locally installed applications, browser extensions, and APIs. It also includes AI features embedded within other software applications. This definition also applies to AI models, datasets, and algorithms, regardless of where they are hosted or deployed. This includes any tool that claims or appears to leverage AI capabilities, even if those capabilities are not readily apparent. This definition also encompasses AI tools used for testing, development, and deployment of AI models, as well as tools used for monitoring AI model performance and detecting bias. Specifically, this includes tools used for: data augmentation, feature engineering, model training, model validation, hyperparameter tuning, model deployment, model monitoring, explainable AI (XAI), and adversarial robustness testing. This also includes any libraries or frameworks used in the development of AI models, such as TensorFlow, PyTorch, scikit-learn, and similar. Note that this definition includes tools that are "AI-enabled" even if the AI functionality is not the primary function of the tool.*
