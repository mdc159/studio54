**Policy Memo: AI Tool Usage**

**Scope**

1. This policy applies to all employees of [Company Name] who use AI tools for work-related purposes.
2. This policy applies to all AI tools, whether managed by the company or brought in by individual employees.
3. This policy does not apply to personal use of AI tools on personal devices outside of work hours.

**Permitted Uses**

1. Employees may use approved AI tools, as listed below, for work-related purposes with prior approval from their manager or technology coordinator.
	* GitHub Copilot Business (up to 80 seats, already licensed).
	* Company Slack (limited to approved features).
2. Employees may use approved AI tools for testing and evaluation purposes, with prior approval from the technology coordinator.
3. Employees may use AI tools for research and development purposes, with prior approval from a manager and the technology coordinator.

**Prohibited Uses**

1. Employees may not paste customer PII or financial data into third-party AI services (base fact: inputting customer data into third-party AI services likely violates existing DPA terms).
2. Employees may not use AI-generated code with GPL license headers from training data (base fact: AI-generated code may not be copyrightable, and the GPL would apply to the training data).
3. Employees may not use AI-generated text that includes copyrighted material from competitors (base fact: incident involving competitor's copyrighted marketing copy verbatim).
4. Employees may not use AI tools to generate code, text, or other materials that could compromise the company's SOC2 Type II certification or GDPR obligations (base fact: GDPR compliance for EU customers).

**Enforcement**

1. Employees must obtain prior approval from their manager or technology coordinator before using AI tools for work-related purposes.
2. Employees must comply with all applicable company policies, including data protection and intellectual property policies.
3. Employees who violate this policy will face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
4. Managers and technology coordinators are responsible for ensuring that their teams comply with this policy.
5. The company will review and update this policy annually, or as necessary.
