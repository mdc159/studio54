**Revised Policy Memo: AI Tool Usage**

**Scope**

1. This policy applies to all employees of [Company Name] who use AI tools for work-related purposes, including remote and hybrid workers, contractors, third-party vendors, and any individuals who access company systems and data.

2. This policy applies to all AI tools, whether managed by the company or brought in by individual employees, including personal devices used for work purposes during work hours, and any software or services that utilize AI technology.

3. This policy also applies to company-wide initiatives that involve the use of AI tools for work-related purposes, including research and development projects, customer-facing applications, and operational processes.

**Purpose**

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all employees use AI tools in a manner that is consistent with the company's data protection, intellectual property, and conflict of interest policies, and to mitigate the risks associated with AI tool usage.

**Permitted Uses**

1. Employees may use approved AI tools, as listed below, for work-related purposes with prior approval from their manager or technology coordinator:

	* GitHub Copilot Business (up to 80 seats, already licensed).
	* Company Slack (limited to approved features).
	* Other AI tools approved by the technology coordinator after a thorough review of their features, functionality, and compliance with the company's data protection, intellectual property, and conflict of interest policies.
	* AI tools specifically designated for testing and evaluation purposes, with prior approval from the technology coordinator.
	* AI tools specifically approved for research and development purposes, with prior approval from a manager and the technology coordinator.
	* Additional AI tools approved by the technology coordinator for specific projects or initiatives, subject to prior approval from a manager.
	* AI tools specifically approved for use in company-wide initiatives, with prior approval from the relevant manager and the technology coordinator.

2. Employees may use approved AI tools for testing and evaluation purposes, with prior approval from the technology coordinator, to:

	* Assess AI tool performance.
	* Identify potential security risks.
	* Evaluate AI-generated content.

3. Employees may use AI tools for research and development purposes, with prior approval from a manager and the technology coordinator, to:

	* Explore new AI technologies.
	* Develop AI-based applications.
	* Improve AI-generated content.

**Restrictions and Guidelines**

1. Employees must not paste customer Personally Identifiable Information (PII) or financial data into third-party AI services without prior approval from the technology coordinator and compliance with existing Data Protection Agreement (DPA) terms. Employees must ensure that customer data is properly anonymized or de-identified before using AI tools.

2. Employees must not use AI-generated code with GPL license headers from training data, as this may compromise the company's intellectual property and copyright rights. Instead, employees must use AI-generated code that is specifically designed to be proprietary and does not include GPL license headers.

3. Employees must not use AI-generated text that includes copyrighted material from competitors, as this may infringe on their intellectual property rights. Employees must instead use AI-generated text that is original and does not include copyrighted material from competitors.

4. Employees must not use AI tools to generate code, text, or other materials that could compromise the company's SOC2 Type II certification or GDPR obligations, including:

	* Generating code that may be subject to GPL licensing requirements.
	* Using AI-generated text that includes copyrighted material from competitors.
	* Sharing or storing sensitive customer data in non-compliant AI services.

5. Employees must comply with all applicable company policies, including data protection, intellectual property, conflict of interest, and compliance policies. Employees who fail to comply with these policies may face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

**Additional Requirements**

1. The technology coordinator will maintain a list of approved AI tools and ensure that they are regularly reviewed and updated.

2. Employees will be required to report any suspected security breaches or data protection incidents related to AI tool usage to the technology coordinator within 24 hours of discovery.

3. Employees will be required to complete regular training and education on AI usage and compliance, as provided by the company, and acknowledge their understanding of this policy before using AI tools.

4. Employees will be required to sign a usage agreement and report any changes to their AI tool usage or list of approved AI tools to the technology coordinator within 30 days of the change.

**Reporting Requirements**

1. Employees will be required to report any suspected security breaches or data protection incidents related to AI tool usage to the technology coordinator within 24 hours of discovery.

2. The technology coordinator will investigate these incidents and take appropriate action to mitigate any risks.

3. The company will provide regular updates on the status of reported incidents and the actions taken to address them.

**Enforcement**

1. Employees must obtain prior approval from their manager or technology coordinator before using AI tools for work-related purposes, and provide documentation of their usage and compliance with company policies.

2. Managers and technology coordinators are responsible for ensuring that their teams comply with this policy, and will be held accountable for any violations.

3. The company will review and update this policy annually, or as necessary, to ensure compliance with changing regulations and industry best practices.

4. The company will provide regular training and education on AI usage and compliance, and employees will be required to complete this training before using AI tools for work-related purposes.
