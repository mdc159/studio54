Here are the real problems with this proposal:

**The table is cut off.** The rework cost table in §0.1 starts with a header row and then stops. The revision notes explicitly claim problem 10 was resolved by adding a schedule impact column and parallelizability explanations. That resolution is unverifiable because the table is truncated. The document's own preamble says a truncated section is a defect regardless of what the revision table claims.

**The plain-language summary obligation has no verification mechanism.** Alex is required to deliver a plain-language summary to Jordan at distribution. There is no logging requirement, no acknowledgment requirement from Jordan, no definition of what "at the time of distribution" means precisely, and no consequence if Alex delivers a summary that is incomplete, misleading, or technically inaccurate. Alex authored the §0.3 analysis and has a disclosed conflict of interest. The summary is the primary tool Jordan has to evaluate that analysis, but there is no check on its quality or even its delivery.

**The 10-day tiebreaker objection window and the plain-language summary are sequenced in a way that creates a real problem.** Jordan's 10-day window to object to Alex as tiebreaker starts from the issue date. The plain-language summary is delivered at distribution, which can occur up to one business day after the issue date, and Morgan's backup distribution adds another business day. Jordan could receive the summary on day 3 or later, losing meaningful evaluation time from the window the document claims was extended specifically to allow that evaluation.

**Morgan Singh's daily check obligation is underspecified.** Morgan is required to check the checklist item "once per business day during the one-business-day window." A one-business-day window contains at most one business day. The instruction to check once per business day during a single business day is either redundant or incoherent depending on whether the window spans parts of two calendar days. It does not specify when during that day Morgan must check, which matters if Morgan checks early and the window closes later the same day.

**The alternative tiebreaker clock interaction is unresolved in a specific case.** The document states the 3-business-day tiebreak clock starts when disagreement is logged, not when the alternative tiebreaker is designated. If Jordan logs a disagreement on day 9, then spends day 10 requesting and designating an alternative tiebreaker, the alternative tiebreaker may have fewer than 2 business days remaining. The document acknowledges this and says the alternative tiebreaker must act within remaining time or notify parties and trigger Option C. But if the alternative tiebreaker is designated on day 10 and the original 14-day default window closes on day 14, there is no analysis of what happens when the tiebreak clock expires and the default would simultaneously fire — the default suspension rule says the default doesn't fire while tiebreak is pending, but the tiebreak clock expiring without action is itself supposed to fire the default. The interaction between a failed alternative tiebreaker, the Option C fallback for that failure, and the simultaneously pending default is not reconciled.

**Morgan Singh adjudicates disputes about log entry validity but has a conflict of interest that is never disclosed.** Morgan is the backup distributor, has checklist access control, receives escalations, and is also the sole adjudicator of whether a disagreement log entry is valid. A dispute about log entry validity determines whether the default is suspended. Morgan's adjudication is described as final with no appeal. Morgan's multiple roles in the same process are never flagged as a structural problem.

**The project calendar is a single point of failure with no fallback.** All business day calculations depend on the project calendar in Confluence maintained by the project coordinator. The project coordinator is unnamed. There is no version control requirement, no audit trail requirement, and no procedure for what happens if the calendar is modified after deadlines are set, is unavailable, or is disputed. A retroactive addition of a holiday to the project calendar could materially shift deadlines that have already passed or are in progress.

**The "process defect" classification for missing automation test records has no operational consequence.** If Alex marks the document final without a linked test record or Morgan's acknowledgment, the document says this is a process defect that must be logged but explicitly does not invalidate the final-form timestamp. There is no defined owner for logging the defect, no defined location for the log, no defined consequence, and no defined remediation. The defect classification creates the appearance of accountability without any mechanism for it.

**The steering committee escalation path appears three times but the steering committee is never defined.** Manager non-response during a pending tiebreak escalates to the steering committee. Morgan and Alex both failing to distribute escalates to the steering committee. A failed alternative tiebreaker triggers Option C and a logged failure, but earlier language implies steering committee involvement. The steering committee has no defined membership, meeting cadence, response obligation, or decision authority anywhere in the document.
