# REVISED HYBRID WORK POLICY
## Practical Framework for Distributed Teams

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes clear expectations for hybrid work while maintaining operational flexibility and equal treatment across all roles. The framework prioritizes business outcomes and team needs over arbitrary presence requirements.

---

## 1. WORK LOCATION REQUIREMENTS

### Unified Framework: Business-Driven Presence

**All Teams - Consistent Standards**
- **Core office days:** Tuesdays and Wednesdays (maximum team overlap)
- **Flexible location:** Monday, Thursday, Friday
- **Required presence:** Monthly all-hands meeting (1st Tuesday), quarterly planning sessions
- **Team-specific needs:** Additional days determined by role requirements, not team membership

*Fixes discriminatory framework by applying same base requirements to all employees*

### Role-Based Adjustments (Applied Equally Across Teams)

**Client-Facing Roles** (regardless of department)
- Must be available during client's preferred meeting times
- Office presence required when hosting in-person client meetings
- Remote work permitted when serving clients in different timezones

**Collaborative Project Roles**
- Additional office time required during active cross-team projects
- Advance notice required for extended remote periods during critical deadlines

**Individual Contributor Roles**
- Standard 2-day office requirement sufficient
- Focus time protected regardless of location

*Fixes arbitrary team-based requirements by basing decisions on actual job functions*

---

## 2. MEETING STANDARDS

### Location-Appropriate Meeting Formats

**In-Person Meetings** (when all attendees are in office)
- Use conference rooms with shared screens and whiteboards
- One person designated to include remote participants via video
- Meeting notes shared digitally within 2 hours

**Hybrid Meetings** (mixed in-person/remote attendees)
- All participants join via individual devices for equity
- In-office participants use designated "hybrid meeting spaces"
- Required for meetings with 1+ remote attendees

**Remote Meetings** (all participants remote)
- Standard video conferencing protocols
- Recordings available for EU timezone participants when needed

*Fixes impossible meeting standards by creating practical formats based on actual attendee locations*

### Communication Requirements

**Synchronous Meetings**
- **Daily standups:** Team discretion on format (async/sync)
- **Weekly team meetings:** During core overlap hours (12-3 PM ET)
- **Cross-team collaboration:** Scheduled with 48-hour advance notice
- **One-on-ones:** Manager discretion, minimum monthly

**Asynchronous Updates**
- **Decision documentation:** All decisions in shared workspace within 48 hours
- **Project status:** Weekly updates for active projects
- **Team updates:** Bi-weekly summaries from team leads

## 3. EQUIPMENT & WORKSPACE POLICY

### Standard Equipment (Market-Researched Budget)

**Technology Package**
- Laptop (company standard) with docking station
- External monitor up to 27"
- Keyboard, mouse, and webcam as needed
- Noise-canceling headset

**Home Office Support**
- **One-time setup:** $800 reimbursement for desk and chair
- **Ongoing support:** $75/month internet contribution for employees working remote 3+ days/week
- **Annual refresh:** $200 for equipment updates/replacements

*Fixes equipment budget fantasy with realistic amounts based on market research*

**Office Resources**
- Hot-desking stations with full monitor setup
- Bookable focus rooms and phone booths
- Printing, scanning, and mailing services

### Workspace Requirements

**Home Office Standards**
- Dedicated workspace with reliable internet (minimum 25 Mbps upload)
- Quiet environment during core collaboration hours
- Ergonomic setup meeting basic safety standards

**Accommodation Process**
- Employees unable to meet home office requirements can request additional office access
- Alternative arrangements available for space, noise, or technical limitations
- Equipment loans available for temporary situations

*Fixes unrealistic assumptions about home situations by providing alternatives*

---

## 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION

### Objective Performance Framework

**Performance Measures (Equal Weight)**
- **Goal Achievement:** Completion of assigned OKRs and projects
- **Work Quality:** Deliverable standards and accuracy metrics
- **Collaboration:** Responsiveness during core hours and project participation
- **Professional Development:** Skill building and knowledge sharing

*Fixes subjective criteria by focusing on measurable outcomes*

### Evaluation Process

**Regular Check-ins**
- Monthly one-on-ones with clear agenda and documentation
- Quarterly goal review and adjustment
- Annual comprehensive review with 360-degree input

**Performance Support**
- Struggling performers receive coaching regardless of location
- High performers eligible for additional flexibility and development opportunities
- Performance improvement plans include clear, measurable objectives

**Location Neutrality**
- Performance evaluations conducted blind to work location preferences
- Managers trained on avoiding location bias in assessments
- Regular calibration sessions to ensure consistency

*Fixes gaming potential and bias issues through structured, objective processes*

## 5. TIMEZONE COORDINATION

### EU Team Integration (3 employees)

**Communication Windows**
- **Morning overlap:** 9:00-10:00 AM ET / 3:00-4:00 PM CET (daily)
- **Afternoon handoff:** 11:00 AM-12:00 PM ET / 5:00-6:00 PM CET (as needed)

*Fixes timezone math errors with correct calculations*

**EU-Specific Support**
- Monthly rotating meeting schedule (early US meetings alternate with late EU meetings)
- 24-hour response time standard for non-urgent communications
- Quarterly EU team gathering (company-sponsored)

### US Coast Coordination

**Core Collaboration Hours:** 12:00-3:00 PM ET / 9:00 AM-12:00 PM PT
- Critical meetings scheduled within this window
- Coast-specific flexibility outside core hours
- Asynchronous handoffs documented in shared systems

## 6. POLICY ENFORCEMENT & EXCEPTIONS

### Compliance Requirements

**Attendance Tracking**
- Simple badge-in system for office days
- Self-reporting for remote work locations
- Monthly review of patterns, not daily monitoring

**Exception Process**
- Emergency situations (weather, health, transportation): No advance approval needed
- Planned exceptions: 48-hour advance notice to manager
- Extended remote work: Formal request process with business justification

*Fixes missing enforcement mechanism with clear but reasonable compliance system*

**Consequences**
- First violation: Manager discussion and documentation
- Pattern of non-compliance: Performance improvement plan
- Continued violations: Progressive discipline per company policy

### Legal & Compliance

**Worker Safety**
- Workers' compensation covers home office injuries during work hours
- Home office safety checklist required annually
- Equipment inspection available upon request

**Data Protection**
- VPN required for all remote work
- Company data storage on approved cloud platforms only
- Annual security training mandatory

*Fixes legal/compliance gaps with specific provisions*
