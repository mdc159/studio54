Go-to-Market Strategy: KubeConfig CLI

Target Customer Segments:
1. Primary: Mid-market engineering teams (50-500 engineers)
- Cloud-native organizations
- Kubernetes adoption stage: Intermediate to advanced
- Vertical focus: Technology, finance, media, e-commerce
- Typical pain point: Complex multi-environment config management

2. Secondary: Enterprise platform/DevOps teams
- Large organizations with complex Kubernetes deployments
- Seeking standardization and governance of configuration workflows

Pricing Model:
- Freemium tier: Core open-source functionality (current state)
- Pro tier: $29/user/month
  - Advanced RBAC controls
  - Enterprise SSO integration
  - Audit logging
  - Priority support
- Enterprise tier: Custom pricing, contact sales
  - Includes professional services
  - Dedicated support
  - Custom integrations

Distribution Channels:
- Primary: GitHub marketplace
- Secondary: Direct outreach via:
  - DevOps conferences
  - Kubernetes community forums
  - LinkedIn targeted advertising
  - Technical blog content
- Partnership channel: Cloud-native consulting firms

First-Year Milestones:
Q1-Q2:
- Develop Pro tier features

- Reach 100 paid customers

Q3:
- Expand marketing efforts
- Develop enterprise integration capabilities
- Target 250 paid customers

Q4:
- Launch enterprise tier

- No custom development services
- No vertical-specific customizations

- No full infrastructure provisioning
- No professional services offerings

Competitive Differentiation:
- Deep Kubernetes expertise
- Open-source credibility
- Focused, opinionated design
- Community-driven development model

Recommended Initial Investment:
