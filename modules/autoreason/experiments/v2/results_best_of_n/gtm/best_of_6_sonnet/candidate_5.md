# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes CLI Configuration Tool

## Target Customer Segments

**Primary Segment: Mid-Market Engineering Teams (50-500 developers)**
- DevOps engineers and platform teams managing 10+ Kubernetes clusters
- Companies with moderate Kubernetes complexity but limited tooling budget
- Organizations transitioning from basic kubectl to more sophisticated config management

**Secondary Segment: Enterprise Platform Teams**
- Large organizations (500+ developers) with dedicated platform engineering teams
- Companies requiring advanced compliance, audit trails, and multi-environment management
- Organizations seeking vendor support for critical infrastructure tools

**Tertiary Segment: Individual Contributors & Consultants**
- Senior engineers managing personal/client Kubernetes environments
- DevOps consultants needing reliable, professional-grade tooling
- Technical leads evaluating tools for team adoption

## Pricing Model

**Freemium with Commercial Extensions**

**Community Edition (Free)**
- Core CLI functionality
- Basic configuration management
- Community support via GitHub/Discord
- Single-user license

**Professional Edition ($29/user/month)**
- Advanced config validation and testing
- Team collaboration features
- Priority email support
- Audit logging
- Integration with popular CI/CD platforms

**Enterprise Edition ($99/user/month)**
- SSO/SAML integration
- Advanced RBAC and policy enforcement
- Dedicated support with SLA
- Custom integrations
- Professional services credits

## Distribution Channels

**Primary: Product-Led Growth**
- Enhanced GitHub presence with comprehensive documentation
- Developer-focused content marketing (tutorials, best practices)
- Conference speaking at KubeCon, DevOps Days
- Integration partnerships with HashiCorp, GitLab, ArgoCD

**Secondary: Direct Sales (Enterprise)**
- Inbound lead qualification for Enterprise prospects
- Partnership with Kubernetes consulting firms
- Targeted outreach to platform engineering teams at Series B+ companies

**Tertiary: Community & Ecosystem**
- Package manager distribution (Homebrew, apt, yum)
- Kubernetes marketplace listings
- Developer community engagement (Reddit, Stack Overflow)

## What We Would Explicitly NOT Do Yet

**Avoid Premature Scaling**
- No venture funding or aggressive hiring until product-market fit is proven
- No expansion into adjacent markets (Docker, Terraform, etc.)
- No building of web UI or SaaS platform

**Resist Feature Bloat**
- No custom integrations for individual customers
- No support for non-Kubernetes orchestration platforms
- No building features requested by <10% of user base

**Don't Overcomplicate Go-to-Market**
- No complex partner channel programs
- No expensive paid advertising campaigns
- No hiring of enterprise sales team until >$500k ARR

**Avoid Operational Complexity**
- No 24/7 support commitments
- No SOC2 compliance until enterprise revenue justifies cost
- No multi-region infrastructure deployment

This strategy leverages the existing open-source momentum while building sustainable revenue through a clear value hierarchy, focusing on the team's core strengths in developer tooling rather than expanding prematurely into adjacent markets.
