# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes Configuration CLI Tool

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary Segment: Platform Engineering Teams (Years 1-2)
- **Profile**: Mid-market companies (500-5,000 employees) with 5-50 Kubernetes clusters
- **Pain Points**: Config sprawl, environment drift, compliance auditing, onboarding friction
- **Budget Authority**: $10K-100K annual tooling budget
- **Decision Process**: Technical evaluation → team trial → procurement approval (2-4 weeks)

### Secondary Segment: DevOps Consultancies (Year 2+)
- **Profile**: 10-100 person consulting firms managing client Kubernetes environments
- **Value Proposition**: Standardized delivery methodology, faster client onboarding
- **Revenue Potential**: Higher per-seat pricing, multi-client deployments

## Pricing Model

### Freemium SaaS Approach
**Community Edition (Free)**
- Core CLI functionality
- Up to 5 clusters
- Community support only
- Open-source features remain free forever

**Professional Edition ($49/user/month)**
- Unlimited clusters
- Web dashboard for config visualization
- RBAC and audit logging
- Email support with 48hr SLA
- SSO integration

**Enterprise Edition ($149/user/month)**
- Multi-tenant management
- Custom compliance policies
- Priority support (4hr SLA)
- Professional services credits
- Air-gapped deployment options

## Distribution Channels

### Phase 1: Developer-Led Growth (Months 1-6)
1. **Content Marketing**: Weekly technical blog posts on Kubernetes config management best practices
2. **Community Engagement**: Speaking at KubeCon, local Kubernetes meetups, CNCF webinars
3. **Product Hunt Launch**: Coordinated launch with existing GitHub community
4. **Integration Partnerships**: Helm, ArgoCD, and Flux marketplace listings

### Phase 2: Sales-Assisted Growth (Months 7-12)
1. **Inside Sales**: One technical sales hire focusing on inbound leads
2. **Partner Channel**: Reseller agreements with major cloud consultancies
3. **Marketplace Presence**: AWS/GCP/Azure marketplace listings for enterprise discovery

### Q2: Product-Market Fit

## What NOT To Do Yet

### Avoid These Common Mistakes:
1. **Don't build enterprise features first** - Focus on core workflow automation before advanced compliance features
2. **Don't hire a VP of Sales** - Too early for senior sales leadership; focus on product-market fit
3. **Don't pursue large enterprise deals** - Mid-market segments provide faster validation and revenue
4. **Don't build native mobile apps** - CLI users are desktop-focused; mobile adds complexity without value
5. **Don't expand to other tools** - Resist building Docker, Terraform, or other config management until Kubernetes dominance
6. **Don't white-label the solution** - Maintain brand recognition and direct customer relationships
7. **Don't offer on-premise only** - SaaS model provides better unit economics and faster iteration

### Timing Considerations:
- **Custom integrations**: Wait until $500K ARR to justify engineering investment
- **International expansion**: Focus on English-speaking markets until $1M ARR
- **Acquisition strategy**: Avoid acquiring complementary tools until achieving market leadership

This strategy leverages your existing GitHub momentum while building sustainable revenue through a proven freemium model targeting the most viable customer segment first.
