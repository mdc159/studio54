# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes CLI Tool

## Target Customer Segments (Prioritized)

**Primary Segment: DevOps Engineers at Scale-ups (50-500 employees)**
- Pain points: Manual K8s config management, environment inconsistencies, deployment bottlenecks
- Budget authority: $5K-50K annual tooling budgets
- Decision timeline: 2-4 weeks
- Validation: Survey existing GitHub users about company size and pain points

**Secondary Segment: Platform Engineering Teams at Mid-Market Companies**
- Pain points: Developer productivity, standardization across teams
- Budget authority: $25K-100K annual platform investments
- Decision timeline: 1-3 months
- Validation: Conduct user interviews with current power users

**Tertiary Segment: Individual Contributors at Large Enterprises**
- Champions who can influence bottom-up adoption
- Focus on free tier to build internal advocacy

## Pricing Model

**Freemium SaaS with Open-Core Strategy**

**Free Tier (OSS Core):**
- CLI tool with basic config management
- Up to 5 environments
- Community support
- Maintains current GitHub momentum

**Professional ($29/user/month):**
- Advanced config validation
- Team collaboration features
- Audit logs and compliance reporting
- Email support
- 10+ environments

**Enterprise ($99/user/month):**
- SSO/SAML integration
- Advanced RBAC
- Priority support + SLA
- On-premise deployment option
- Unlimited environments

*Rationale: Low entry point reduces friction; clear value differentiation drives upgrades*

## What NOT to Do Yet

**Avoid These Common Traps:**

1. **Don't build enterprise features first**
   - Resist pressure to build complex enterprise requirements
   - Focus on core workflow optimization for primary segment

2. **Don't pursue direct sales model**
   - No inside sales team or enterprise sales reps
   - 3-person team should focus on product and PLG motion

3. **Don't target Fortune 500 initially**
   - Enterprise sales cycles too long for current team size
   - Procurement processes will slow momentum

4. **Don't diversify beyond Kubernetes**
   - Avoid building Docker, Terraform, or other infrastructure tools
   - Stay laser-focused on K8s config management excellence

5. **Don't launch multiple pricing tiers simultaneously**
   - Start with Free + Professional only
   - Add Enterprise tier only after validating Professional PMF

6. **Don't invest in paid advertising**
   - Developer tools require trust and technical credibility
   - Focus budget on product development and community building

**Resource Allocation Recommendation:**
- 60% product development
- 25% community/content marketing
- 15% customer success and sales enablement

This strategy leverages your existing GitHub traction while building sustainable revenue through a proven freemium model targeted at your most viable customer segments.
