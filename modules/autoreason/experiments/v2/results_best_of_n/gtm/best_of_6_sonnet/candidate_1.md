# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes Config CLI Tool

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary Segment: DevOps Engineers at Mid-Market Companies (50-500 employees)
- **Profile**: Companies with 5-15 Kubernetes clusters, manual config management pain
- **Pain Points**: Time-consuming config deployments, environment inconsistencies, lack of config versioning
- **Budget Authority**: $10K-50K annual tooling budgets
- **Decision Timeline**: 30-90 days

### Secondary Segment: Platform Engineering Teams at Enterprise (500+ employees)
- **Profile**: Mature Kubernetes adoption, seeking standardization across teams
- **Pain Points**: Config sprawl, compliance requirements, developer productivity bottlenecks
- **Budget Authority**: $50K+ annual budgets
- **Decision Timeline**: 90-180 days

## Pricing Model

### Freemium SaaS + Professional Services
1. **Open Source Core** (Current): CLI tool remains free, builds community
2. **Professional Tier** ($49/user/month): 
   - Web dashboard for config visualization
   - Team collaboration features
   - Audit logs and compliance reporting
   - Priority support
3. **Enterprise Tier** ($149/user/month):
   - SSO/SAML integration
   - Advanced RBAC
   - Custom integrations
   - Dedicated customer success manager

## Distribution Channels

### Primary Channels
1. **Product-Led Growth**: Enhanced CLI with upgrade prompts to paid features
2. **Developer Community**: Conference speaking, technical blog content, GitHub engagement
3. **Partner Ecosystem**: Integrations with HashiCorp, GitLab, CircleCI

### Secondary Channels
4. **Content Marketing**: Technical tutorials, case studies, comparison guides
5. **Direct Sales**: Outbound to VP Engineering/Platform teams at target accounts

## First-Year Milestones

### Q1: Foundation
- Launch freemium SaaS platform

### Q2: Market Validation
- Achieve $10K MRR
- 50 paying customers
- Net Promoter Score >40

### Q3: Scale Preparation
- $25K MRR
- Hire first sales/marketing person
- Launch enterprise tier

### Q4: Growth Acceleration
- $50K MRR
- 200 paying customers
- Secure $500K seed funding

## What We Would Explicitly NOT Do Yet

### Avoid These Premature Investments:
1. **Multi-product strategy**: Focus solely on Kubernetes config management, resist expanding to general DevOps tooling
2. **Enterprise sales team**: No dedicated enterprise sales until proven PMF at mid-market
3. **International expansion**: Stay US/English-speaking markets only
4. **Complex integrations**: Avoid building for every possible tool; focus on top 3 most-requested
5. **VC fundraising**: Bootstrap until clear $50K+ MRR trajectory established
6. **Compliance certifications** (SOC2, etc.): Delay until enterprise deals require them

### Strategic Rationale:
With 3 people and existing GitHub traction, focus is critical. The freemium model leverages your open-source momentum while creating a clear upgrade path. Mid-market focus provides faster sales cycles and clearer feedback loops than enterprise, while avoiding the low-value trap of individual developers.

Success depends on converting GitHub engagement into paying customers through demonstrable ROI in config management efficiency.
