Go-to-Market Strategy for Kubernetes Config CLI

1. Target Customer
Primary Segment: Mid-market DevOps teams in cloud-native organizations (50-500 employees)
- Specific Persona: Senior DevOps/Platform Engineering leads managing complex Kubernetes deployments
- Pain Point: Configuration drift, inconsistent environment management, and high operational overhead in multi-cluster environments
- Budget Range: $500-$2,000/year for productivity tools
- Why Now: Increasing Kubernetes complexity (average organization runs 4.4 clusters per CNCF 2022 survey) and growing pressure to reduce operational toil

Justification: This segment represents teams experiencing acute Kubernetes management challenges but with budget and technical sophistication to adopt specialized tooling.

2. Pricing
Tier: Pro Enterprise
Price: $79/user/month
Value Proposition: Estimated 20 hours/month saved in configuration management
ROI Calculation:
- Average DevOps engineer salary: $135,000/year ($65/hour)
- Time saved: 20 hours × $65 = $1,300/month per user
- Tool cost: $79/user
- Net savings: $1,221/user/month or 94% ROI

- Sponsor 6 top Kubernetes/DevOps podcasts

5. What We Won't Do
a. No free tier — maintains product positioning as enterprise-grade solution
b. No direct sales team — relies on product-led growth and community momentum

Mitigation: Implement SOC 2 compliance and create detailed security documentation
Watch Metric: Security review completion and enterprise conversion rate
