**MEMORANDUM**

**TO:** All Employees  
**FROM:** Executive Team  
**DATE:** [Date]  
**RE:** AI Tool Usage Policy

## SCOPE

1. This policy applies to all 200 employees across engineering, sales, and other departments.
2. Covers all AI-powered tools, services, and applications used for work purposes.
3. Applies to all company devices, networks, and any personal devices accessing company systems.

## PERMITTED USES

1. GitHub Copilot Business for code generation and completion (80 licensed seats available).
2. AI coding assistants for writing new code that does not contain customer data, proprietary algorithms, or sensitive business logic.
3. AI tools for drafting internal communications, documentation, and non-customer-facing content.
4. AI-generated content for sales outreach templates and general marketing materials, subject to legal review before external use.
5. Use of company Slack with AI features disabled as currently configured.

## PROHIBITED USES

1. Input of customer PII, financial data, or any data covered by our SOC2 or GDPR obligations into any AI service, motivated by DPA violation risks and compliance requirements.
2. Input of proprietary code, database schemas, API specifications, or technical architecture into public AI services, motivated by the database schema incident and IP protection needs.
3. Use of AI-generated code without removing license headers and conducting license compatibility review, motivated by the GPL license header incident.
4. Direct copying of AI-generated text for external communications without verification of originality, motivated by the competitor copyright incident.
5. Use of AI tools not explicitly approved in this policy or through IT procurement process.
6. Input of any data that would violate existing customer Data Processing Agreements.

## ENFORCEMENT

1. Engineering managers will review AI-generated code during existing code review processes for license compliance and proprietary content exposure.
2. Sales leadership will review AI-assisted external communications during current approval workflows.
3. IT will monitor and block access to unauthorized AI services through existing network controls.
4. Violations involving customer data exposure will result in immediate suspension of AI tool access and disciplinary action up to termination.
5. All AI tool requests exceeding the $50K annual budget require executive approval through standard procurement processes.
6. Quarterly compliance audits will include AI tool usage review as part of existing SOC2 and FedRAMP preparation processes.
