# AI Tool Usage Policy
**Effective Date:** [Date] | **Owner:** Engineering & Legal | **Applies To:** All Employees

---

## Scope

1. This policy governs all AI tools—code assistants, text generators, and chat interfaces—used by all 200 employees in all functions.
2. This policy applies on company devices, personal devices used for work, and all third-party AI services, whether company-licensed or personal.

---

## Permitted Uses

1. Engineers with an assigned GitHub Copilot Business seat (80 of 120 seats licensed) may use it for code completion, generation, and review in approved repositories. Engineering leadership manages seat assignment.
2. Sales and other non-engineering functions have no approved AI tool for external drafting. Any AI tool request is submitted to Legal for DPA and IP review before first use and funded within the $50K annual budget.
3. All AI-generated code submitted via pull request goes through the existing PR approval process. Reviewers flag any license headers or license-referencing comments for Legal review before merge.

---

## Prohibited Uses

1. **No customer data in third-party AI tools.** Inputting PII, financial data, or any customer-identifiable information into any AI service outside company-controlled systems is prohibited. *[Basis: Incident 1—engineer pasted customer database schema into ChatGPT; outside counsel confirmed this likely violates existing DPA terms; SOC2 Type II, GDPR, and pending FedRAMP authorization require data handling controls.]*
2. **No AI-generated content sent externally without manager co-signature.** AI-generated text is not transmitted to customers, prospects, or partners unless the sender's direct manager co-signs or approves the communication in writing, confirming no verbatim third-party copyrighted material is present. *[Basis: Incident 2—sales rep transmitted verbatim competitor copyrighted marketing copy generated by AI.]*
3. **All AI-generated code requires legal review before commit.** Engineers submit all AI-generated code through the existing legal review checklist before commit, regardless of whether a license header is visible. *[Basis: Incident 3—intern committed GPL-licensed AI-generated code; outside counsel confirmed AI-generated code may not be copyrightable, a risk that applies to all such code, not only code containing visible license headers.]*
4. **No AI tools for the 40 unseated engineers.** Engineers without an assigned GitHub Copilot Business seat use no AI coding tool until a seat is assigned or Legal issues written approval for an alternative. *[Basis: 80 seats are licensed for 120 engineers; no other AI coding tool has completed DPA and IP review.]*
5. **No Slack AI features.** Employees do not request or enable Slack AI features. *[Basis: All three incidents involved uncontrolled AI output reaching external parties or company systems; Slack processes internal communications containing customer and financial data subject to SOC2, GDPR, and FedRAMP controls; outside counsel confirmed inputting customer data into third-party AI services likely violates DPA terms.]*
6. **No non-approved AI tools** until Legal issues written approval following DPA and IP review.

---

## Enforcement

1. Violations are reported to the employee's direct manager and Legal on the same business day via existing SOC2 incident reporting channels.
2. Violations involving customer PII, financial data, or any system relevant to FedRAMP authorization are escalated to Legal within one business day and treated as priority incidents given the Q3 FedRAMP target.
3. Confirmed violations follow the existing disciplinary process: documented warning, performance plan, or termination, based on severity and recurrence.
4. Managers are accountable for team compliance. Quarterly access reviews already required for SOC2 include verification of AI tool usage against this policy.
