# AI Tool Usage Policy
**Effective Date:** Date of CEO signature below | **Owner:** Engineering & Legal | **Applies To:** All Employees

---

## Scope

1. This policy governs all AI tools—code assistants, text generators, and chat interfaces—used by all 200 employees across all functions.
2. This policy applies on company devices, personal devices used for work, and all third-party AI services, whether company-licensed or personal accounts.
3. All informal AI tool use not explicitly permitted below must cease on the effective date of this policy.

---

## Permitted Uses

1. Engineers with an assigned GitHub Copilot Business seat (80 of 120 seats licensed) may use it for code completion, generation, and review in approved repositories. Engineering leadership manages seat assignment.
2. Additional AI tools may be submitted to Legal for DPA and IP review and approved for use within the $50K annual AI tooling budget. No tool is permitted until Legal issues written approval.

---

## Prohibited Uses

1. **No customer data in third-party AI tools.** Inputting PII, financial data, or any customer-identifiable information into any AI service outside company-controlled systems is prohibited. *[Basis: Incident 1—engineer pasted customer database schema into ChatGPT; outside counsel confirmed this likely violates existing DPA terms; SOC2 Type II, GDPR, and pending FedRAMP authorization require data handling controls.]*
2. **No AI-generated content transmitted externally without prior written manager approval.** Before any AI-generated text is sent to customers, prospects, or partners, the sender must obtain email approval from their direct manager. That email constitutes the record of approval and is retained under existing email retention controls. *[Basis: Incident 2—sales rep transmitted verbatim competitor copyrighted marketing copy generated by AI; outside counsel confirmed AI-generated content carries unresolved copyright risk.]*
3. **No AI-generated code merged without PR disclosure and license review.** Engineers must identify AI-generated code in the pull request description. Reviewers must confirm no license headers are present before merge, using the existing PR approval process. *[Basis: Incident 3—intern committed GPL-licensed AI-generated code; outside counsel confirmed AI-generated code may not be copyrightable.]*
4. **No unapproved AI tools.** All employees without an assigned GitHub Copilot Business seat—including all sales, all other non-engineering staff, and the 40 engineers without an assigned seat—use no AI tool for work purposes. *[Basis: 80 seats licensed for 120 engineers; no other tool has received Legal DPA and IP approval; outside counsel confirmed third-party AI input of customer data likely violates DPA terms.]*
5. **No enabling of Slack AI features.** Slack AI features are disabled at the administrative level. Employees must not submit requests to IT or to Slack to enable them. *[Basis: Slack AI features are currently disabled; Slack processes internal communications containing customer and financial data subject to SOC2, GDPR, and FedRAMP controls.]*

---

## Enforcement

1. All violations are reported to the employee's direct manager and Legal the same business day via existing SOC2 incident reporting channels. Violations involving customer PII, financial data, or FedRAMP-relevant systems are additionally escalated to the CEO the same business day.
2. Confirmed violations follow the existing disciplinary process: documented warning, performance plan, or termination.
3. Managers are accountable for team compliance. Quarterly access reviews required for SOC2 include verification of AI tool usage against this policy.
