## Problems Found

### 1. The Document Contains Substantial Non-Policy Content (Constraint Violation)

The first ~400 words of the document is a meta-commentary preamble ("I'll address each problem in order...") describing changes made to a prior draft. This is not part of the policy memo. It is filler that has no place in a company policy document and, critically, counts toward the 500-word limit. The actual policy content may be under or near the limit on its own, but the document as submitted includes this preamble as part of the deliverable, making the overall document far exceed 500 words and making the word count of the policy itself ambiguous.

### 2. Word Count Violation Is Near-Certain Even for Policy Body Alone

Counting only the policy body (from "AI Tool Usage Policy" through the CEO signature line), the text appears to substantially exceed 500 words. The Prohibited Uses section alone is dense and lengthy. The constraint is a hard maximum of 500 words, and the policy body alone likely violates it without the preamble.

### 3. Prohibited Use 6 Basis Citation Is Fabricated / Not Derivable

The basis cited for Prohibited Use 6 claims "Incident 2 involved customer and financial data transmitted through an unreviewed AI service." The base facts state Incident 2 was a sales rep using AI-generated text that included a competitor's *copyrighted marketing copy* verbatim. There is no mention of customer data or financial data being involved in Incident 2. The policy invents a characterization of Incident 2 not present in the base facts, violating the constraint that nothing be added that isn't derivable from the base facts.

### 4. Permitted Use 1 States an Unverifiable Operational Fact as Policy

"80 of 120 engineering seats are currently licensed" is a present-state operational fact embedded in the policy text. Policy documents govern behavior going forward; stating a current seat count as a numbered policy item is not a rule and is not enforceable. It will also become factually wrong as seats are added or removed.

### 5. Prohibited Use 2 Is Not Enforceable Without New Process

The prohibition requires "written approval from their direct manager" before any AI-generated text *template, campaign, or draft type* is used. The constraint requires enforceability using existing access controls and review processes. There is no existing review process identified in the base facts for pre-approving sales communications. This creates a new approval workflow, not an enforcement mechanism derivable from existing controls.

### 6. Enforcement Item 3 Invents a Compliance Mechanism

"Quarterly access reviews required for SOC2 include verification of AI tool usage against this policy." The base facts establish SOC2 Type II certification but do not specify that quarterly access reviews exist or that they include AI tool usage verification. This adds a fact not derivable from the base facts and asserts a specific audit scope that may not match the actual SOC2 program.

### 7. Scope Item 3 Is Redundant and Adds No Enforceable Rule

"All AI tool use not explicitly permitted below is prohibited" is a restatement of a default-deny posture that is already implicit in having a Prohibited Uses section and a limited Permitted Uses section. As a numbered policy item it occupies space without adding an enforceable obligation distinct from what the rest of the policy already establishes.
