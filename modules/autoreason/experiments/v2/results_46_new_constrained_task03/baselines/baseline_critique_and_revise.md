# AI Tool Usage Policy
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date Before Publication] | **Owner:** Engineering & Legal | **Applies To:** All Employees

---

## Scope

1. This policy governs all use of AI tools—including code assistants, text generators, and chat interfaces—by all employees across all functions.
2. This policy applies to AI use on company devices, personal devices used for work, and any third-party AI service, whether company-licensed or personal.
3. This policy supersedes all informal practices previously in use and takes effect on the date shown above.

---

## Approved Tools

1. The current list of approved AI tools, associated access controls, and authorized user groups is maintained by Legal and Engineering in the **AI Tools Registry** [link to internal registry]. This is the authoritative reference for whether a specific tool is approved.
2. Any tool not listed in the AI Tools Registry is unapproved for work use until the approval process described in the Governance section is completed.

---

## Permitted Uses

1. Employees with an allocated seat may use **GitHub Copilot Business** for code completion, generation, and review within approved repositories. Seat allocation is managed by Engineering leadership. Seat eligibility is determined by the Engineering leadership roster, not job title, and is not limited to engineering roles.
2. Employees in sales and other non-engineering functions may use approved AI tools for internal drafting tasks—including outlines and first drafts—provided that all output is reviewed and substantively edited by the authoring employee before any external communication is sent. The employee is responsible for the accuracy, originality, and appropriateness of any content they transmit.
3. All AI-generated or AI-assisted code merged into production must include an inline comment at the point of generation identifying it as AI-assisted. This satisfies the tracking requirement for pull request review but does not satisfy the separate license review requirement described in Prohibited Use 3.

---

## Prohibited Uses

1. **No customer data in third-party AI tools.** Inputting PII, financial data, or any customer-identifiable information into any AI service outside company-controlled systems is prohibited. This includes database schemas, query results, exported records, or any other artifact that could identify a customer or expose customer data. *[Basis: Incident 1—engineer pasted customer database schema into ChatGPT; outside counsel confirmed this likely violates existing DPA terms. SOC2 Type II, GDPR, and pending FedRAMP authorization require enforceable data handling controls.]*

2. **No unreviewed AI-generated external content.** AI-generated text sent externally—to customers, prospects, or partners—must be reviewed and substantively edited by the sending employee to confirm it is accurate, does not contain third-party copyrighted material, and reflects the employee's own judgment. Transmitting AI output verbatim, or with only superficial changes, does not satisfy this requirement. *[Basis: Incident 2—sales rep transmitted verbatim competitor copyrighted marketing copy generated by AI.]*

3. **No AI-generated code committed without license review.** Engineers must run all AI-generated or AI-assisted code through the existing legal review checklist before commit. This requirement applies regardless of whether the code contains a visible license header, a comment referencing a license, or a Copilot license filter flag. The absence of a visible license indicator does not eliminate IP exposure. *[Basis: Incident 3—intern committed GPL-licensed AI-generated code; outside counsel flagged that AI-generated code may not be copyrightable, creating IP exposure independent of whether a license is surfaced by the tool.]*

4. **No AI features in company Slack.** AI features in Slack remain disabled. Employees may not request, enable, or use them. *[Basis: Current approved configuration; enabling AI features in Slack would create uncontrolled data exposure channels inconsistent with existing DPA and SOC2 controls.]*

5. **No use of unapproved AI tools for work tasks.** Employees may not use any AI tool not listed in the AI Tools Registry for work-related tasks until Legal has completed a DPA and IP review and issued written approval. See the Governance section for the approval process.

---

## Governance

1. **Approved tools list.** Legal and Engineering jointly maintain the AI Tools Registry. Employees must consult the Registry before first use of any AI tool. If the tool is not listed, they must submit an approval request per item 3 below before using it for any work-related task.
2. **Budget.** The annual AI tooling budget is $50,000. This is the ceiling for new tool approvals in a given fiscal year. Requests that would exceed this ceiling require CFO approval before Legal review proceeds. The budget ceiling applies to net new tool costs and does not affect renewals of already-approved tools unless the renewal cost increases.
3. **New tool approval process.** Requests to approve a new AI tool are submitted to Legal and Finance using the AI Tool Request form [link]. Legal completes a DPA and IP review; Finance confirms budget availability. Written approval from Legal is required before use begins. Typical review time is [X] business days. Employees who submit a request may not use the tool while review is pending.
4. **Quarterly access reviews.** Quarterly access reviews, already required for SOC2 compliance, include verification of AI tool usage against this policy and the AI Tools Registry.

---

## Enforcement

1. Any employee who discovers a violation of this policy must report it to the violating employee's direct manager and the Legal team within 24 hours of discovery. Employees who report violations in good faith will not face retaliation.
2. Engineering violations are detectable through existing pull request history, commit logs, and access logs. Non-engineering violations—including external content transmission and use of unapproved tools—are subject to review through email and communication logs consistent with company monitoring policies. No new tooling is required to enforce this policy.
3. Confirmed violations follow the existing disciplinary process: documented warning, performance improvement plan, or termination, depending on severity and recurrence.
4. Managers are accountable for their team's compliance with this policy. Compliance is a standing agenda item in quarterly access reviews.

---

## Acknowledgment and Training

1. All employees must read this policy and complete the associated acknowledgment in the HR system within 10 business days of the effective date, or within 5 business days of their start date for employees hired after the effective date.
2. Employees who use approved AI tools must complete the AI tool usage training module [link] before first use. Completion is tracked by HR and verified during quarterly access reviews.
3. Legal will review this policy no less than annually and update it when material changes occur to approved tools, applicable regulations, or company risk posture. Material updates require re-acknowledgment from all employees within 10 business days of the update.

## Revision Notes

The following problems were identified and corrected in this revision:

**Permitted Uses, item 1** — The original contained a redundant, contradictory pair of sentences: it stated seat eligibility is "not limited to a single function" and then immediately restated the same point differently. Consolidated into one clear sentence.

**Permitted Uses, item 3** — The original said the inline comment "does not satisfy the license review requirement in the Prohibited Uses section" without specifying which prohibited use. Readers had to cross-reference by inference. Revised to cite "Prohibited Use 3" directly.

**Prohibited Uses, item 1** — The prohibition on customer data was stated abstractly. "Customer-identifiable information" is ambiguous in practice; engineers may not recognize that a schema, a query result, or an export qualifies. Added concrete examples of what counts as customer data to close that gap.

**Governance, item 2** — The $50,000 budget ceiling was stated without clarifying whether it applies to renewals of existing tools. A reasonable reader could interpret it as blocking renewal of already-approved tools if costs increase even slightly, or conversely assume renewals are fully exempt. Added a sentence clarifying that the ceiling applies to net new costs and affects renewals only if the renewal cost increases.

**Governance, item 3** — The original did not address whether employees could use a tool while approval was pending. This is the highest-risk gap in the document: an employee who submits a request in good faith might reasonably assume use is permitted during review. Added explicit language prohibiting use while review is pending.

**Enforcement, item 1** — The original required employees to report violations but contained no retaliation protection. Without it, employees have a practical disincentive to report. Added a good-faith retaliation protection clause.

**Enforcement, item 2** — The original stated that engineering violations are detectable through existing logs, then stopped. Non-engineering violations—the verbatim content transmission incident, use of unapproved tools—have no stated detection mechanism, which creates an implicit and incorrect impression that only engineering conduct is enforceable. Added a sentence addressing detection of non-engineering violations.

**Acknowledgment and Training, item 3** — The original required re-acknowledgment for material updates but set no deadline for completing it. Combined with the 10-business-day window established in item 1, the omission created an inconsistency. Added a consistent 10-business-day deadline for re-acknowledgment following material updates.
