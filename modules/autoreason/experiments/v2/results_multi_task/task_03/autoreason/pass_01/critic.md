## Critical Problems with This Hybrid Remote Work Policy

### Unworkable Complexity and Administrative Burden

**Meeting rotation nightmare**: The "rotating times monthly to share timezone burden" for all-hands meetings creates a scheduling impossibility. With 50 people across 3+ timezones, rotating to accommodate everyone means some meetings will be at 5 AM for West Coast or midnight for EU. This will either fail immediately or create resentment.

**Booking system chaos**: Hot-desking with "first-come, first-served" plus advance reservations up to 2 weeks creates a complex dual system. Sales has permanent desks but others are competing for space, which will create immediate friction and inequality.

**Performance metric overload**: Different evaluation criteria for each department, plus 360-feedback, plus bi-weekly 1:1s, plus quarterly informal reviews, plus bi-annual formal reviews creates an unsustainable administrative burden for managers.

### Contradictory Requirements

**Engineering mandate contradiction**: Engineering is "remote-first" but must attend monthly sprint retrospectives, quarterly planning (2 days), and monthly cross-functional meetings. This actually requires 6-8 office days minimum, not the stated 1 day per month.

**EU team impossibility**: EU employees cannot meaningfully participate in "quarterly US office visits" while also maintaining their regular work responsibilities. The logistics and cost make this unrealistic for most roles.

**Core overlap window failure**: 11 AM-2 PM EST (3 hours) is insufficient for meaningful collaboration across all functions, especially when EU team has only until 7 PM local time before personal time.

### Missing Critical Infrastructure

**No conflict resolution mechanism**: When Sales needs conference rooms during their mandatory Tuesday-Thursday presence but other functions also need space on Wednesday (their mandatory day), there's no system to resolve conflicts.

**Equipment logistics gap**: $1,500 home office stipend with $500 annual refresh sounds generous but there's no process for equipment return, theft/damage policies, or what happens when employees leave.

**No enforcement mechanism**: The policy describes "compliance monitoring" but provides no consequences for non-compliance beyond "department head discussion."

### Fundamentally Flawed Assumptions

**Meeting recording assumption**: "All strategic meetings recorded" assumes every strategic discussion can be captured this way, ignoring informal hallway conversations, whiteboard sessions, and relationship-building that happens organically in person.

**Output-based measurement illusion**: Claiming "outcome-based performance" while still requiring specific days in office reveals the policy doesn't actually trust output measurement—it's still measuring presence.

**Equal collaboration myth**: The policy assumes all functions can collaborate equally in hybrid mode, but Sales relationship-building and Engineering deep work have completely different collaboration needs that this one-size-fits-all approach ignores.

### Operational Impossibilities

**Timezone math doesn't work**: EU team working 9 AM-5 PM CET means they're offline when West Coast starts their day. The "24-hour response protocol with regional handoffs" has no staffing to support it.

**New hire onboarding contradiction**: Requiring 4 days/week in office for 30 days when hot-desking is first-come, first-served means new hires might not have workspace during their critical onboarding period.

**Emergency escalation fantasy**: "@here mentions" for urgent issues across timezones means waking people up or expecting responses outside business hours, contradicting the work-life balance goals.

### Economic and Practical Failures

**Office space economics**: Permanent desks for 15 Sales people plus space for rotating presence of others requires maintaining nearly full office capacity while most people work remotely most of the time.

**Technology support gap**: "2-day turnaround for critical failures" for remote workers could mean complete work stoppage, but there's no backup equipment provision or expedited replacement for truly critical roles.

**Travel budget explosion**: EU quarterly visits, local meetups, and company retreats for 50 people represents a massive unbudgeted expense that will likely get cut, undermining the entire EU inclusion strategy.

The policy attempts to solve too many problems simultaneously while creating new ones through its complexity. The fundamental tension between "remote-first" and mandatory in-person requirements is never resolved—it's just papered over with elaborate scheduling systems that won't survive contact with reality.
