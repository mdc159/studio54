# Hybrid Remote Work Policy (Revised)
**Effective Date:** [Date]  
**Policy Version:** 1.0

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a department-specific hybrid work model that respects Engineering's remote-first approach while meeting Sales' in-person collaboration needs. The policy focuses on clear, enforceable requirements with minimal administrative overhead.

## Core Principles

- **Department-Specific Requirements**: Different functions have different collaboration needs
- **Outcome-Based Performance**: Clear deliverables with flexible execution
- **Practical Timezone Management**: Realistic expectations across regions
- **Sustainable Operations**: Policies that can be maintained long-term

## Required In-Office Days

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 4 days per quarter (1 day per month average)
- **Mandatory Events**: 
  - Quarterly planning sessions (1 day per quarter)
  - Annual company retreat
- **Flexible Days**: Additional days as needed for specific projects
- **EU Engineers**: Participate virtually in all events; no US travel required

*Fixes: Engineering mandate contradiction - reduces actual requirements to match stated minimums*

### Sales Team (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 3 days per week in office
- **Core Days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Flexible Days**: Monday and Friday (optional)
- **Exceptions**: Client travel, pre-approved remote work

### All Other Functions (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 1 day per week in office
- **Recommended Day**: Wednesday for cross-team alignment
- **Flexibility**: Choose any day based on collaboration needs

*Fixes: Booking system chaos - reduces competition for office space*

## Meeting Norms

### Timezone Accommodation
- **Core Collaboration Hours**: 11 AM - 1 PM EST (8 AM - 10 AM PST, 4 PM - 6 PM CET)
- **All-Hands Meetings**: Fixed at 12 PM EST monthly (compromise time)
- **EU Accommodation**: Maximum 1 early/late meeting per person per week

*Fixes: Meeting rotation nightmare - establishes fixed, predictable schedule*

### Meeting Structure
- **Default Mode**: Assume hybrid participation
- **Documentation**: Meeting notes required within 48 hours
- **Recording**: Available upon request, not mandatory

*Fixes: Meeting recording assumption - makes recording optional rather than required*

### Critical In-Person Meetings
- Quarterly business planning
- Performance reviews
- Client presentations (Sales)
- New employee onboarding

## Equipment and Technology Policy

### Company-Provided Equipment
- Laptop with docking station
- Monitor (for home office)
- Headset
- Basic office supplies

### Home Office Support
- **One-time Allowance**: $1,000 for desk and chair
- **Internet Stipend**: $50/month for upgraded internet
- **Equipment Ownership**: Company retains ownership; return required upon departure

*Fixes: Equipment logistics gap - clarifies ownership and return policy*

### Office Resources
- **Desk Assignment**: Sales team has assigned desks; others use available workspace
- **Booking**: Conference rooms bookable 1 week in advance
- **Conflict Resolution**: Sales team gets priority Tuesday-Thursday; others get priority Monday/Friday

*Fixes: No conflict resolution mechanism - establishes clear priority system*

## Performance Evaluation Approach

### Evaluation Principles
- **Outcome-Focused**: Judge results, not location or hours
- **Regular Check-ins**: Monthly 1:1 meetings
- **Consistent Standards**: Same expectations regardless of work location

### Standard Process
- **Frequency**: Quarterly check-ins, annual formal review
- **Format**: Video conference for consistency
- **Metrics**: Department-specific goals aligned with company objectives

*Fixes: Performance metric overload - simplifies to manageable system*

## Timezone Management

### Communication Expectations
- **Response Times**: 
  - Urgent: 4 hours during business hours
  - Standard: 24 hours
  - Non-urgent: 48 hours
- **After-Hours Policy**: No expectation of immediate response outside local business hours

*Fixes: Emergency escalation fantasy - sets realistic response expectations*

### EU Team Integration
- **Meeting Participation**: Virtual attendance at all company meetings
- **Local Support**: EU team works with local vendors for equipment/IT support
- **Connection**: Monthly EU-only team meeting for culture building
- **Travel**: Optional annual visit to US office (not required)

*Fixes: EU team impossibility - removes mandatory travel requirement*

## Implementation and Compliance

### Enforcement
- **Monitoring**: Monthly office utilization tracking
- **Non-Compliance**: Discussion with manager after 30 days below requirements
- **Escalation**: HR involvement after repeated non-compliance
- **Flexibility**: Temporary adjustments for personal circumstances

*Fixes: No enforcement mechanism - provides clear escalation path*

### Support Systems
- **IT Support**: Remote support during business hours; 48-hour equipment replacement
- **Office Coordination**: Facilities manager tracks space usage and resolves conflicts
- **Policy Questions**: HR available for clarification and accommodation requests

*Fixes: Technology support gap - extends replacement timeline to realistic 48 hours*

## Budget Considerations

### Estimated Costs
- Home office allowances: $50,000 one-time
- Monthly internet stipends: $30,000 annually
- Office space: Maintain current lease (sized for Sales team + 30% flex capacity)
- EU support: $15,000 annually for local services

*Fixes: Travel budget explosion - eliminates mandatory EU travel costs*

## Success Metrics

### Primary Measures
- Employee satisfaction with work arrangement (quarterly survey)
- Achievement of department goals
- Office space utilization rates
- Employee retention

### Review Process
- **Monthly**: Utilization and satisfaction data review
- **Quarterly**: Policy adjustment discussions with department heads
- **Annually**: Comprehensive policy review

*Fixes: Fundamentally flawed assumptions - focuses on practical, measurable outcomes*

## Key Changes Made:

1. **Simplified Engineering requirements** to actually match "remote-first" approach and eliminated EU travel mandate
2. **Established clear office space priorities** to prevent booking conflicts
3. **Fixed meeting schedule** instead of rotating times across timezones
4. **Streamlined performance evaluation** to reduce administrative burden
5. **Clarified equipment ownership and return policies** 
6. **Removed unrealistic 24-hour support** and emergency response expectations
7. **Eliminated mandatory EU travel** while maintaining inclusion
8. **Added clear enforcement mechanisms** with realistic consequences
9. **Provided realistic budget estimates** instead of unfunded mandates
10. **Focused on measurable success metrics** rather than aspirational goals

The revised policy trades comprehensive coverage for practical enforceability, addressing the core criticism that the original was too complex to implement successfully.
