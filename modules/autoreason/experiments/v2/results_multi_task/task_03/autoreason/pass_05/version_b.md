# Hybrid Remote Work Policy - Revised Version
**Effective Date:** [Date]  
**Policy Version:** 2.0

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a department-specific hybrid work model based on demonstrated collaboration needs. Engineering maintains maximum flexibility with minimal office requirements, while Sales has structured in-office time for client-facing activities. The policy prioritizes measurable implementation and consistent enforcement.

## Core Principles

- **Evidence-Based Requirements**: Office presence tied to specific business needs
- **Measurable Implementation**: Clear metrics with enforceable standards
- **Consistent Standards**: Same performance expectations regardless of location
- **Practical Operations**: Realistic expectations for scheduling and compliance

## Required In-Office Days

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 4 days per year (1 per quarter) for team planning sessions
- **Scheduling**: Team planning dates set annually in December for following year
- **Duration**: Full business day (8 hours minimum)
- **Purpose**: Architecture decisions, sprint planning, and knowledge transfer requiring physical collaboration

*Fixes: "Engineering's quarterly requirement is unenforceable" - Changes to annual compliance tracking with 4 specific dates.*

### Sales Team (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 days per week in office (104 days per year)
- **Core Days**: Tuesday and Wednesday
- **Duration**: Full business day (8 hours minimum, with 1-hour lunch flexibility)
- **Remote Work Process**: Submit written request 48 hours in advance with business justification (client meeting requiring travel, documented family emergency, pre-approved vacation). Manager must respond within 24 hours with written approval/denial and business rationale.

*Fixes: "Manager approval creates inconsistent application" - Defines specific criteria and written process with response requirements.*

### All Other Functions (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 1 day per week in office (52 days per year)
- **Core Day**: Thursday (changed from Wednesday to reduce desk conflicts)
- **Duration**: Full business day (8 hours minimum, with 1-hour lunch flexibility)
- **Purpose**: Cross-department coordination meetings

*Fixes: "Hot-desking booking bottleneck" - Moves other functions to Thursday to separate from Sales' Tuesday/Wednesday requirement.*

### EU Team Members (3 people)
- **Engineering EU (2 people)**: Same 4 annual planning days, participate via video for planning portions, travel to US office once annually (company-sponsored, required)
- **Other Functions EU (1 person)**: Weekly Thursday requirement waived, participates in monthly cross-department video sessions during 11 AM - 1 PM EST window

*Fixes: "Virtual participation undefined" and "Core hours don't align" - Separates EU requirements and defines specific participation methods.*

## Meeting Norms

### Meeting Standards
- **Default**: All meetings include video option for remote participants
- **In-Person Only Meetings**: Physical whiteboarding sessions, equipment demonstrations, client entertainment (must be scheduled during required office days)
- **Documentation**: Meeting notes with decisions and action items required within 24 hours

*Fixes: "Remote-first meetings contradict office presence" - Eliminates contradiction by allowing both in-person and remote participation without defaulting to remote-first.*

### Timezone Management
- **Core Collaboration Hours**: 11 AM - 1 PM EST (8 AM - 10 AM PST, 5 PM - 7 PM CET)
- **Response Expectations**: 
  - Urgent: 4 hours during local business hours
  - Standard: 24 hours
- **EU Integration**: Monthly cross-department sync during core hours, weekly for active project teams only

## Equipment and Technology Policy

### Standard Equipment (All Employees)
- Laptop ($1,200 budget)
- Docking station ($200 budget)
- External monitor ($300 budget)
- Headset ($100 budget)
- **Total**: $1,800 equipment budget per employee

*Fixes: "$500 won't cover listed equipment" - Increases budget to realistic amount covering all specified items.*

### Office Resources
- **Desk Policy**: Reserved desk assignments for required office days
  - Sales: 15 assigned desks for Tuesday/Wednesday
  - Others: 15 assigned desks for Thursday
  - Engineering: 5 flexible desks for quarterly sessions
- **Meeting Rooms**: Video conferencing equipment in all rooms
- **Booking**: No advance booking required for assigned desks, 48-hour booking for meeting rooms

*Fixes: "Hot-desking with 1-week booking doesn't support required attendance" - Assigns specific desks for required days, eliminating booking conflicts.*

## Performance Evaluation Approach

### Universal Standards (All Functions)
- **Goal Achievement**: Measurable objectives met by deadline (0-100% scale)
- **Communication Effectiveness**: Response time compliance (measured quarterly)
- **Office Presence Compliance**: Percentage of required days attended (target: 95%+)
- **Collaboration Quality**: Peer feedback scores on cross-team projects (quarterly survey, 1-5 scale)

*Fixes: "Performance evaluation lacks measurable criteria" - Defines specific metrics and measurement methods.*

### Performance Issue Process
**Office Presence Issues:**
- **Engineering**: Missing 2+ annual required days triggers manager conversation, 3+ requires HR involvement
- **Sales/Others**: Missing 3+ required days per quarter triggers manager conversation, 6+ requires HR involvement
- **Resolution**: Written improvement plan with specific attendance targets and 60-day review period

*Fixes: "Math doesn't work for quarterly violations" - Aligns violation thresholds with actual requirement frequency.*

## Compliance Tracking

### Attendance Verification
- **Badge System**: Electronic entry/exit tracking for all office visits
- **Manager Verification**: Weekly attendance reports showing badge times for required days
- **Minimum Presence**: Badge entry before 10 AM, exit after 4 PM on required days (6+ hour minimum)

*Fixes: "Badge access won't capture actual compliance" - Implements entry/exit tracking with minimum duration requirements.*

### Reporting
- **Weekly**: Manager review of attendance reports
- **Monthly**: Compliance summary to department heads
- **Quarterly**: Individual performance review including attendance metrics

*Fixes: "Policy governance timeline misaligned" - Aligns reporting frequency with requirement patterns.*

## Implementation Timeline

### Week 1-4: Infrastructure Setup
- Install badge entry/exit system with reporting capabilities
- Assign desk spaces by function and required days
- Order equipment within defined budgets
- Configure meeting rooms with video equipment

### Week 5-6: Policy Launch
- Department meetings explaining specific requirements and measurement methods
- Manager training on attendance reporting and performance standards
- Distribute equipment and assign desks

### Week 7+: Full Operation
- Begin compliance tracking and reporting
- Monthly effectiveness reviews
