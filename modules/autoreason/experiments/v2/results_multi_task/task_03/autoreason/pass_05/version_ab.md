# Hybrid Remote Work Policy - Final Version
**Effective Date:** [Date]  
**Policy Version:** 1.0

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a department-specific hybrid work model based on demonstrated collaboration needs. Engineering maintains maximum flexibility with minimal office requirements, while Sales has structured in-office time for client-facing activities. The policy prioritizes measurable implementation with realistic enforcement.

## Core Principles

- **Evidence-Based Requirements**: Office presence tied to specific business needs
- **Measurable Implementation**: Clear metrics with enforceable standards
- **Consistent Standards**: Same performance expectations regardless of location
- **Practical Operations**: Realistic expectations for scheduling and compliance

## Required In-Office Days

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 4 days per year (1 per quarter) for team planning sessions
- **Scheduling**: Team planning dates set annually in December for following year
- **Duration**: Full business day (8 hours minimum)
- **Purpose**: Architecture decisions, sprint planning, and knowledge transfer requiring physical collaboration

*Rationale: Measurable annual compliance tracking while respecting Engineering's remote-first preference*

### Sales Team (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 days per week in office (104 days per year)
- **Core Days**: Tuesday and Wednesday
- **Duration**: Full business day (8 hours minimum, with 1-hour lunch flexibility)
- **Remote Work Process**: Submit written request 48 hours in advance with business justification (client travel, documented family emergency). Manager must respond within 24 hours with written approval/denial and business rationale.

*Rationale: Evidence-based requirement with standardized approval process to ensure consistency*

### All Other Functions (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 1 day per week in office (52 days per year)
- **Core Day**: Thursday
- **Duration**: Full business day (8 hours minimum, with 1-hour lunch flexibility)
- **Purpose**: Cross-department coordination meetings

*Rationale: Separate day from Sales to eliminate desk conflicts*

### EU Team Members (3 people)
- **Engineering EU (2 people)**: Same 4 annual planning days, participate via video for planning portions, required annual US office visit (company-sponsored)
- **Other Functions EU (1 person)**: Weekly Thursday requirement waived, participates in monthly cross-department video sessions during core hours

*Rationale: Maintains consistency while acknowledging timezone and travel constraints*

## Meeting Norms

### Meeting Standards
- **Default**: All meetings include video option for remote participants
- **In-Person Only Meetings**: Physical whiteboarding sessions, equipment demonstrations, client entertainment (must be scheduled during required office days)
- **Documentation**: Meeting notes with decisions and action items required within 24 hours

*Rationale: Supports both in-person and remote participation without contradicting office requirements*

### Timezone Management
- **Core Collaboration Hours**: 11 AM - 1 PM EST (8 AM - 10 AM PST, 5 PM - 7 PM CET)
- **Response Expectations**: 
  - Urgent: 4 hours during local business hours
  - Standard: 24 hours
- **EU Integration**: Monthly cross-department sync during core hours, weekly for active project teams only

## Equipment and Technology Policy

### Standard Equipment (All Employees)
- Laptop ($1,200 budget)
- Docking station ($200 budget)
- External monitor ($300 budget)
- Headset ($100 budget)
- **Total**: $1,800 equipment budget per employee

*Rationale: Realistic budget covering all specified equipment needs*

### Office Resources
- **Desk Policy**: Reserved desk assignments for required office days
  - Sales: 15 assigned desks for Tuesday/Wednesday
  - Others: 15 assigned desks for Thursday
  - Engineering: 5 flexible desks for quarterly sessions
- **Meeting Rooms**: Video conferencing equipment in all rooms
- **Booking**: No advance booking required for assigned desks, 48-hour booking for meeting rooms

*Rationale: Eliminates booking conflicts while ensuring desk availability on required days*

## Performance Evaluation Approach

### Universal Standards (All Functions)
- **Goal Achievement**: Measurable objectives met by deadline (0-100% scale)
- **Communication Effectiveness**: Response time compliance within defined windows
- **Office Presence Compliance**: Percentage of required days attended (target: 95%+)
- **Collaboration Quality**: Peer feedback scores on cross-team projects (quarterly survey, 1-5 scale)

*Rationale: Measurable criteria with specific metrics while maintaining universal standards*

### Performance Issue Process
**Office Presence Issues:**
- **Engineering**: Missing 2+ annual required days triggers manager conversation
- **Sales/Others**: Missing 3+ required days per quarter triggers manager conversation
- **Resolution**: Direct manager conversation with HR escalation for repeated issues (3+ instances)

*Rationale: Simplified process aligned with requirement frequency, avoiding bureaucracy*

## Compliance Tracking

### Attendance Verification
- **Badge System**: Electronic entry/exit tracking for all office visits
- **Manager Verification**: Monthly attendance reports showing compliance for required days
- **Minimum Presence**: Badge entry before 10 AM, exit after 4 PM on required days (6+ hour minimum)

*Rationale: Actual compliance measurement without excessive granularity*

### Reporting
- **Monthly**: Manager review of attendance compliance
- **Quarterly**: Individual performance review including attendance metrics

## Implementation Timeline

### Week 1-4: Infrastructure Setup
- Install badge entry/exit system with reporting capabilities
- Assign desk spaces by function and required days
- Order equipment within defined budgets

### Week 5-6: Policy Launch
- Department meetings explaining specific requirements and measurement methods
- Manager training on attendance reporting and performance standards

### Week 7+: Full Operation
- Begin compliance tracking and reporting
- Monthly effectiveness reviews

## Success Metrics

### Quarterly Measurements
- **Employee Satisfaction**: Department-specific survey scores (target: >4.0/5.0)
- **Goal Achievement Rates**: Department performance against objectives
- **Office Utilization**: Percentage of assigned desks used on required days (target: 90%+)
- **Compliance Rates**: Percentage of employees meeting attendance requirements (target: 95%+)

*Rationale: Focus on outcomes that matter to business success*

## Policy Governance

### Review Process
- **Monthly**: Manager feedback on compliance issues
- **Quarterly**: Employee satisfaction and goal achievement review
- **Annually**: Full policy assessment with potential revisions

### Amendment Authority
- **Attendance Requirements**: Leadership team approval with 60-day notice
- **Operational Details**: Department head approval with 30-day notice
