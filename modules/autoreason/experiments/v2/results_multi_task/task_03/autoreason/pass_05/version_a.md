# Hybrid Remote Work Policy - Revised Version
**Effective Date:** [Date]  
**Policy Version:** 1.0

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a department-specific hybrid work model based on demonstrated collaboration needs. Engineering maintains maximum flexibility with minimal office requirements, while Sales has structured in-office time for client-facing activities. The policy prioritizes simple implementation and clear enforcement.

## Core Principles

- **Evidence-Based Requirements**: Office presence tied to specific business needs
- **Simple Implementation**: Clear rules with straightforward enforcement
- **Consistent Standards**: Same performance expectations regardless of location
- **Practical Operations**: Realistic expectations for scheduling and compliance

## Required In-Office Days

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 1 day per quarter for team planning session
- **Scheduling**: Team planning date set quarterly, 4 weeks in advance
- **Purpose**: Architecture decisions, sprint planning, and knowledge transfer requiring whiteboarding

*Addresses: "Remote-first contradiction" - Reduces to truly minimal requirement. "Scheduled 2 weeks advance" - Extends to 4 weeks for quarterly planning.*

### Sales Team (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 days per week in office
- **Core Days**: Tuesday and Wednesday
- **Purpose**: Client calls requiring multiple participants, deal strategy sessions, pipeline reviews
- **Remote Work Requests**: Manager approval for client travel or family emergencies

*Addresses: "No evidence for 3 days/week" - Reduces to 2 days with specific business justification. "Pre-approved process" - Defines approval authority and criteria.*

### All Other Functions (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 1 day per week in office
- **Core Day**: Wednesday for cross-team meetings
- **Purpose**: Coordination meetings requiring multiple departments

*Addresses: "Function-specific complexity" - Simplifies to single requirement for non-core functions.*

### EU Team Members (3 people)
- **Requirement**: Same as their functional team (Engineering: quarterly, Others: weekly Wednesday)
- **Implementation**: Virtual participation in required sessions
- **Annual Visit**: Optional company-sponsored visit, not required

*Addresses: "Two-tier system" - Applies same requirements but allows virtual participation to avoid fairness issues.*

## Meeting Norms

### Hybrid Meeting Standards
- **Default**: All meetings are remote-first via video platform
- **In-Person Meetings**: Only for activities requiring physical presence (whiteboarding, equipment setup, client entertainment)
- **Documentation**: Meeting notes required within 24 hours for all decisions

*Addresses: "Video platform contradiction" - Eliminates requirement for office attendees to join virtually, making in-person presence meaningful.*

### Timezone Management
- **Core Collaboration Hours**: 11 AM - 1 PM EST (8 AM - 10 AM PST, 5 PM - 7 PM CET)
- **Response Expectations**: 
  - Urgent: 4 hours during local business hours
  - Standard: 24 hours
- **EU Integration**: Monthly EU-US sync during core hours (not weekly)

*Addresses: "Weekly coordination assumption" - Reduces to monthly unless specific projects require more frequent coordination.*

## Equipment and Technology Policy

### Standard Equipment (All Employees)
- Laptop with docking station
- External monitor
- Headset
- $500 home office setup allowance (adjusted by local cost of living)

*Addresses: "$1,000 flat rate inadequacy" - Reduces amount and allows regional adjustment.*

### Office Resources
- **Desk Policy**: Hot-desking with online booking system
- **Meeting Rooms**: Standard video conferencing equipment
- **Booking Window**: 1 week advance booking

*Addresses: "Office reconfiguration complexity" - Eliminates assigned desks and complex space conversion.*

## Performance Evaluation Approach

### Universal Standards (All Functions)
- Goal achievement and deadline adherence
- Communication effectiveness and responsiveness
- Professional development progress
- Required office presence compliance

*Addresses: "Department-specific complexity" - Creates single evaluation framework for all employees.*

### Performance Issue Process
**Office Presence Issues:**
- Missing required office days triggers manager conversation
- Repeated non-compliance (3+ instances per quarter) requires HR involvement
- Resolution through schedule adjustment or role modification

*Addresses: "30-day improvement plan bureaucracy" - Simplifies to direct manager conversation with HR escalation only for repeated issues.*

## Implementation Timeline

### Week 1-2: Equipment and Setup
- Order and distribute equipment
- Set up booking system
- Install basic badge tracking for office access

*Addresses: "Badge swipe granularity" - Uses badges only for basic access, not detailed time tracking.*

### Week 3-4: Policy Launch
- Department meetings to explain requirements
- Manager training on performance standards
- Begin policy implementation

### Week 5+: Full Operation
- Monthly compliance review by managers
- Quarterly policy effectiveness assessment
