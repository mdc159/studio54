# Hybrid Remote Work Policy - Revised Version
**Effective Date:** [Date]  
**Policy Version:** Revised

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a department-specific hybrid work model that balances meaningful in-office collaboration with operational flexibility. Engineering has structured requirements tied to development cycles, Sales has predictable presence with verification processes, and all teams operate under measurable performance standards focused on business outcomes.

## Core Principles

- **Business-Outcome Focused**: Office presence tied to measurable business results
- **Practical Implementation**: Realistic expectations for scheduling and compliance  
- **Consistent Standards**: Same performance expectations regardless of location
- **Operational Flexibility**: Accommodates actual business workflow patterns

## Required In-Office Days

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 full business days per quarter (8 total days annually)
- **Scheduling**: Quarterly dates set at beginning of each quarter with 30-day notice
- **Verification**: Badge scan entry/exit with minimum 6-hour presence
- **Purpose**: Quarterly planning, architecture review sessions, and cross-team coordination
- **Compliance Standard**: Missing 2+ sessions annually triggers performance review discussion

*Fixes Engineering enforceability problem by replacing subjective "collaborative work" measurement with objective badge scan verification and clear 6-hour minimum.*

### Sales Team (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 days per week in office when not on pre-approved client business
- **Core Days**: Tuesday and Wednesday (when not traveling)
- **Verification**: Badge scan entry/exit with minimum 6-hour presence
- **Travel Approval**: Client meetings requiring absence from core days must be pre-approved by sales manager with client contact verification
- **Documentation**: Submit client meeting calendar invites or confirmations for approval
- **Compliance Standard**: Missing more than 6 core office days annually without pre-approved client business triggers performance improvement plan

*Fixes Sales travel loophole by requiring pre-approval with verifiable client documentation instead of retroactive weekly schedules.*

### All Other Functions (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 1 day per week in office
- **Flexible Scheduling**: Any day Monday-Friday based on business needs
- **Verification**: Badge scan entry/exit with minimum 6-hour presence
- **Purpose**: Available for cross-department coordination as needed

### EU Team Members (3 people)
- **Engineering EU (2 people)**: Participate in quarterly planning via video; no required US travel
- **Other Functions EU (1 person)**: Weekly office requirement waived; available during 9 AM - 11 AM EST overlap window for coordination

*Fixes EU timezone problem by shifting to 9 AM - 11 AM EST (3 PM - 5 PM CET), which aligns with standard European work hours.*

## Meeting Norms

### Meeting Standards
- **Default**: All meetings accommodate both in-person and remote participants equally
- **Video-Required Meetings**: Must be scheduled in equipped conference rooms with 72-hour advance notice
- **Backup Protocol**: If video rooms unavailable, meeting moves to all-remote format
- **Documentation**: Meeting notes with decisions and action items required within 24 hours

### Timezone Management
- **Core Collaboration Hours**: 9 AM - 11 AM EST (6 AM - 8 AM PST, 3 PM - 5 PM CET)
- **Response Expectations**: 
  - Urgent: 4 hours during local business hours
  - Standard: 24 hours
- **EU Integration**: Available during core hours for active project coordination

## Equipment and Technology Policy

### Standard Equipment (All Employees)
- **Domestic Employees**: $2,800 equipment budget (includes laptop, docking station, monitor, headset, plus IT setup and shipping)
- **EU Employees**: $3,500 equipment budget (includes international shipping and customs)
- **Total Domestic**: $2,800 × 47 employees = $131,600
- **Total EU**: $3,500 × 3 employees = $10,500

*Fixes budget reality problem by increasing budgets to reflect actual procurement costs including IT overhead and international shipping.*

### Office Resources
- **Desk Policy**: 40 assigned desks for hybrid workers with reservation system
  - Engineering: 8 desks (for quarterly sessions)
  - Sales: 15 desks (for Tuesday/Wednesday requirement)
  - Other Functions: 15 desks (flexible scheduling)
  - Reserve desk 48 hours in advance via online system
- **Meeting Room Infrastructure**: 8 rooms with professional video conferencing setup
- **Booking**: Mandatory 72-hour advance booking for video rooms

*Fixes desk math problem by providing 40 desks (enough for peak usage) and increasing video rooms to 8 to handle demand. Extends booking window to 72 hours to reduce conflicts.*

## Performance Evaluation Approach

### Universal Standards (All Functions)
- **Goal Achievement**: Measurable objectives met by deadline (0-100% scale)
- **Communication Effectiveness**: Response time compliance during core hours (tracked via email/Slack timestamps)
- **Project Delivery**: Individual deliverable completion rates within assigned timelines
- **Office Presence**: Badge scan compliance with required attendance

*Fixes subjective performance standards by replacing unmeasurable "collaboration quality" with objective "project delivery" metrics and using timestamp data for communication tracking.*

### Performance Issue Process
**Attendance Pattern Concerns:**
- **Step 1**: Manager discussion within 30 days of missed requirement
- **Step 2**: HR involvement and formal improvement plan if pattern continues
- **Step 3**: Role modification or termination consideration per standard HR processes
- **Authority**: Department heads have authority to modify individual arrangements with HR approval

*Fixes authority structure problem by creating clear escalation path and defining who can make arrangement modifications.*

## Compliance Tracking

### Attendance Verification
- **Method**: Electronic badge scan system tracking entry/exit times
- **Reporting**: Automated weekly reports to department heads showing compliance status
- **Focus**: Pattern identification and early intervention for compliance issues

*Fixes compliance mechanism problem by implementing objective badge scan system instead of subjective "manager observation."*

### IT Infrastructure Requirements
- **VPN Capacity**: Upgrade to support 50 concurrent users with 25 Mbps minimum per connection
- **Security**: Mandatory VPN for all remote work, home network security assessment required
- **Video Conferencing**: Enterprise Zoom license supporting 50 concurrent participants across 8 rooms
- **Budget**: $25,000 annual IT infrastructure upgrade and maintenance

*Fixes missing infrastructure problem by specifying VPN, security, and video conferencing requirements with dedicated budget.*

## Implementation Timeline

### Week 1-12: Infrastructure Setup
- Week 1-4: IT infrastructure upgrades (VPN, security systems)
- Week 5-8: Badge scan system installation and testing
- Week 9-12: Equipment procurement and distribution (extended timeline for enterprise procurement)

- Manager training on compliance tracking and escalation procedures

## Success Metrics

### Quarterly Measurements
- **Business Outcomes**: Revenue per employee, client retention rates, project completion within budget
- **Compliance Rates**: Badge scan data showing attendance compliance by department
- **Employee Satisfaction**: Work arrangement effectiveness survey (target: >3.5/5.0)
- **Operational Efficiency**: Video room utilization rates, desk reservation accuracy

*Fixes unmeasurable metrics problem by using specific business outcomes that can be isolated (revenue per employee, client retention) rather than complex collaborative measures.*

- **Location Impact**: Compare individual performance metrics before/after policy implementation
- **Control Variables**: Account for market conditions, resource changes, and project complexity in performance analysis
