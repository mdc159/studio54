## Critical Problems

### Engineering Requirements Are Still Unenforceable

The "8 days annually" requirement creates a false sense of structure. With only 2 sessions per quarter, if someone misses 2 sessions they're at 50% non-compliance but haven't triggered any consequences yet. The gap between "3+ sessions triggers discussion" and "5+ sessions requires improvement plan" means someone can miss 4 sessions (half their requirement) with only a conversation. This renders the policy meaningless for anyone who doesn't want to comply.

### Sales Travel Exception Creates Massive Loophole

"No advance approval required for client business" combined with "submit travel schedule weekly" means sales people can retroactively claim any absence was client-related. There's no verification mechanism for whether claimed "client meetings" actually occurred or were necessary. A sales person could work remotely 100% of the time by claiming all absences are client travel.

### Desk Reservation System Will Fail Immediately

25 desks for 50 employees assumes 50% daily attendance, but the policy requires different attendance rates by department (Sales: 40%, Others: 20%, Engineering: 4%). This creates unpredictable demand that will either leave desks empty most days or create shortages when multiple departments need office space simultaneously. The "no penalties for unreserved desk usage" means the reservation system provides no actual desk guarantee.

### "6+ Hours Office Presence" Is Unverifiable

Without badge tracking, there's no way to measure whether someone was in the office for 6+ hours. "Manager awareness through observation" only works if managers are also in the office for 6+ hours and actively monitoring employee presence. This creates an honor system that defeats the purpose of having specific time requirements.

### Cross-Department Coordination Is Undefined

"Available for cross-department coordination as needed" provides no mechanism for determining when coordination is actually needed. Different departments have different office days with no overlap requirements, so the person who needs coordination might not be in the office when the other department is present.

### EU Integration Window Is Still Problematic

The 10 AM - 12 PM EST window (4 PM - 6 PM CET) occurs during European end-of-workday when people are leaving the office. This assumes EU employees will extend their workday for US coordination rather than shifting their schedule earlier.

### Equipment Budget Ignores Deployment Complexity

The $1,800 per employee budget assumes all equipment can be purchased and deployed simultaneously. There's no account for procurement lead times, IT setup time, shipping delays, or the fact that 50 employees receiving equipment simultaneously will overwhelm IT support capacity.

### Performance Evaluation Lacks Concrete Standards

"Stakeholder satisfaction for collaborative work" requires stakeholders to exist and provide feedback, but many roles may not have clear internal stakeholders. "Communication effectiveness" measured by "response time compliance" doesn't account for different communication urgency levels or the fact that urgent vs. standard designation is subjective.

### Meeting Room Video Equipment Assumption

Installing video equipment in 2 rooms assumes the existing meeting rooms have appropriate electrical, network, and acoustic infrastructure for video conferencing. Many office meeting rooms lack proper power outlets, network drops, or acoustic treatment necessary for effective video meetings.

### Quarterly Planning Sessions Assume Stable Staffing

The engineering quarterly planning requirement assumes team composition remains stable enough for meaningful quarterly planning. With remote work enabling easier job switching, the people attending Q1 planning may be largely different from those attending Q4 planning, making continuity impossible.

### Success Metrics Lack Baseline Data

Measuring "project completion rates" and "client satisfaction scores" assumes these metrics are currently tracked and have established baselines. Without baseline data, there's no way to determine if changes are due to the hybrid policy or other factors.

### Amendment Process Lacks Operational Details

"Leadership team approval based on business outcome data" doesn't specify which leaders, what constitutes sufficient data, or how conflicting data interpretations will be resolved. This creates potential gridlock when policy adjustments are needed.

### Budget Replacement Rate Is Arbitrary

The 30% annual equipment replacement rate has no basis in actual usage data. Remote equipment may have lower replacement needs (less physical handling) or higher needs (home environment risks). Without usage data, the budget could be severely over or under actual needs.
