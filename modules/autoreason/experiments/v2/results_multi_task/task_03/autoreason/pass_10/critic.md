## Critical Problems

### Fundamental Design Contradictions

**Engineering quarterly planning requirement doesn't match actual development workflows.** Modern engineering teams work in 2-week sprints with continuous integration. Forcing quarterly "architecture decisions" into 8 specific days creates artificial constraints that will either be ignored or force teams into waterfall planning that contradicts agile methodologies.

**Sales "when not traveling" exception is unenforceable.** The policy requires 2 days/week but exempts travel without defining what constitutes legitimate client business. Sales can claim any meeting as "client business" making the requirement meaningless. The weekly travel schedule submission has no verification mechanism.

**Mixed in-person/remote meetings will fail consistently.** Despite equipment budgets, hybrid meetings create systematic disadvantages for remote participants due to audio quality, body language visibility, and side conversations. The "backup protocol" of going all-remote when video fails admits the system doesn't work reliably.

### Resource Planning Failures

**Desk reservation math doesn't work.** 25 desks for 50 employees assumes perfect scheduling coordination that won't happen. When Engineering's quarterly sessions coincide with Sales' Tuesday/Wednesday requirements, there will be insufficient desk space. No buffer for unplanned office visits or equipment failures.

**Video room capacity planning ignores meeting overlap.** 4 rooms for 50 employees with different in-office schedules will create booking conflicts during peak collaboration times. The 48-hour advance booking requirement conflicts with agile team needs for spontaneous collaboration.

**Equipment budget assumes uniform needs.** $2,250 per employee doesn't account for role differences - engineers need multiple monitors and powerful hardware while other functions need basic setups. The 25% annual replacement rate has no basis in actual equipment lifecycle data.

### Compliance and Enforcement Gaps

**"Manager observation" tracking is subjective and inconsistent.** Different managers will interpret "business outcomes" and "attendance patterns" differently. No standardized criteria for when attendance becomes a "pattern concern" or what constitutes adequate "business impact."

**Performance review triggers are vague and legally problematic.** "Missing 3+ sessions triggers performance review discussion" doesn't define what happens in that discussion or what standards apply. Creates potential discrimination issues when applied inconsistently across protected classes.

**EU timezone "availability window" conflicts with local labor laws.** Requiring availability during 4-6 PM CET may violate European working time directives depending on local contracts and collective bargaining agreements.

### Technology Infrastructure Assumptions

**Professional video conferencing setup cost estimate is unrealistic.** $20,000 per room ($80,000 total) won't provide enterprise-grade systems that handle 50-person hybrid meetings reliably. Doesn't include network infrastructure upgrades, IT support, or integration with existing systems.

**Desk reservation system complexity underestimated.** $5,000 setup cost assumes simple booking software but doesn't account for integration with badge systems, calendar systems, or facilities management. Ongoing $1,000 annual cost won't cover support and maintenance.

### Business Logic Flaws

**Department-specific requirements create coordination problems.** Engineering quarterly sessions, Sales Tuesday/Wednesday schedule, and other functions' flexible days will create periods of office overcrowding and periods of empty offices. No mechanism to balance utilization.

**"Business outcome focused" performance measurement lacks baseline data.** Policy assumes correlation between office presence and business results without establishing current performance metrics or causal relationships. Can't measure improvement without knowing starting point.

**Implementation timeline ignores procurement and installation realities.** 8-week setup assumes immediate vendor availability, no installation delays, and no integration problems. Doesn't account for facilities modifications, network upgrades, or employee training time.

### Policy Governance Problems

**Amendment authority structure creates conflicting decision-making.** Department heads can change "operational details" with 30-day notice while leadership needs 60-day notice for "attendance requirements." No clear boundary between these categories will create disputes and inconsistent policy application.

**Success metrics measurement frequency doesn't match policy adjustment timeline.** Quarterly measurements of business outcomes can't inform monthly policy adjustments. Creates reactive rather than proactive policy management.

**No escalation path for policy disputes.** When employees disagree with manager assessment of attendance patterns or business impact, there's no defined appeals process or objective review mechanism.
