## Critical Problems with This Proposal

### 1. Engineering Quarterly Requirement is Unenforceable
- One day per quarter means 3-month gaps between required presence
- No mechanism to track or enforce attendance at quarterly sessions
- Team members can easily skip with minimal consequences since next session is months away
- "Team planning session" for 20 people in one day is operationally impossible for meaningful work

### 2. Hot-Desking System Will Fail Under Load
- Sales team (15 people) required Tuesdays/Wednesdays creates 30 desk bookings for just 2 days
- Other functions (15 people) all need Wednesday desks = 15 more bookings
- Policy provides no desk capacity numbers or booking priority system
- "1 week advance booking" insufficient for recurring weekly/bi-weekly needs

### 3. EU Virtual Participation is Technically Unworkable
- "Quarterly team planning" at 11 AM EST is 5 PM CET (end of EU workday)
- Whiteboarding and architecture decisions explicitly stated as requiring physical presence
- EU team members cannot meaningfully participate in activities defined as needing in-person presence
- Creates exactly the two-tier system the policy claims to solve

### 4. Core Collaboration Hours are Mathematically Impossible
- 2-hour window (11 AM-1 PM EST) insufficient for meaningful collaboration
- EU team working until 7 PM CET regularly is unsustainable
- West Coast team starting meetings at 8 AM conflicts with school drop-offs and commutes
- No consideration for daylight saving time changes between regions

### 5. Sales Remote Work Approval Process is Undefined
- "Manager approval for client travel or family emergencies" provides no criteria
- No process for recurring client travel or extended family situations
- Creates arbitrary manager discretion without guidelines
- Family emergency approval timeframe not specified

### 6. Performance Evaluation Lacks Measurable Standards
- "Communication effectiveness" and "responsiveness" have no objective criteria
- "Required office presence compliance" is only measurable metric
- Different job functions cannot be evaluated with identical standards
- No baseline data exists to set meaningful targets

### 7. Budget Numbers Don't Account for Regional Variations
- $500 equipment allowance "adjusted by local cost of living" provides no adjustment methodology
- EU employees may need different equipment (power adapters, keyboards, etc.)
- No consideration for currency fluctuations or international shipping costs
- Equipment replacement calculation assumes uniform 5% failure rate across all items

### 8. Badge Access System Cannot Track Compliance
- Policy uses badges "only for basic access, not detailed time tracking"
- No way to verify employees stayed for required activities vs. just entering building
- Cannot distinguish between required office days and voluntary office use
- Compliance measurement impossible without time tracking

### 9. Meeting Documentation Requirement is Unenforceable
- "Meeting notes required within 24 hours for all decisions" applies to all meetings
- No specification of who writes notes, reviews them, or enforces compliance
- Creates massive administrative burden with no enforcement mechanism
- Informal discussions and decisions will simply move outside documented meetings

### 10. Equipment Booking System Creates Scheduling Conflicts
- Meeting rooms bookable 1 week in advance but office days planned quarterly/weekly
- No integration between desk booking and meeting room availability
- Teams required to be in office may find no meeting spaces available
- Video conferencing equipment maintenance and failures not addressed

### 11. Success Metrics are Disconnected from Policy Goals
- Employee satisfaction target (>4.0/5.0) has no baseline or benchmark
- Office utilization measurement undefined (peak vs. average vs. required days)
- Response time compliance cannot be measured without detailed tracking systems
- Goal achievement rates don't distinguish between in-office and remote work contributions
