# Hybrid Remote Work Policy - Final Version
**Effective Date:** [Date]  
**Policy Version:** Final

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a department-specific hybrid work model that balances meaningful in-office collaboration with operational flexibility. Engineering has structured requirements tied to development cycles, Sales has predictable presence with streamlined travel processes, and all teams operate under consistent performance standards focused on business outcomes.

## Core Principles

- **Business-Outcome Focused**: Office presence tied to measurable business results
- **Practical Implementation**: Realistic expectations for scheduling and compliance  
- **Consistent Standards**: Same performance expectations regardless of location
- **Operational Flexibility**: Accommodates actual business workflow patterns

## Required In-Office Days

### Engineering Team (20 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 full-day planning sessions per quarter (8 total days annually)
- **Scheduling**: Quarterly planning dates set at beginning of each quarter with 30-day notice
- **Duration**: Full business day (6+ hours of collaborative work)
- **Purpose**: Architecture decisions, sprint planning, code reviews requiring pair programming, and knowledge transfer
- **Compliance Standard**: Missing 3+ sessions annually triggers performance review discussion; missing 5+ sessions requires formal improvement plan

*Uses Version Y's approach - meaningful but realistic requirements that respect engineering's resistance to change while ensuring actual collaboration occurs.*

### Sales Team (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 2 days per week in office when not traveling for client business
- **Core Days**: Tuesday and Wednesday (when in town)
- **Duration**: 6+ hours of office presence
- **Travel Exception**: No advance approval required for client meetings requiring travel - submit travel schedule weekly showing client meetings and expected return
- **Compliance Standard**: Missing more than 8 core office days annually without documented client business triggers performance improvement plan

*Combines Version Y's streamlined travel process with Version X's verification mechanism - eliminates bottlenecks while preventing abuse.*

### All Other Functions (15 people)
- **Minimum Requirement**: 1 day per week in office
- **Flexible Scheduling**: Any day Monday-Friday based on business needs
- **Duration**: 6+ hours of office presence
- **Purpose**: Available for cross-department coordination as needed

*Uses Version Y's flexible approach rather than arbitrary fixed days.*

### EU Team Members (3 people)
- **Engineering EU (2 people)**: Participate in quarterly planning via video; no required US travel
- **Other Functions EU (1 person)**: Weekly office requirement waived; available during 10 AM - 12 PM EST overlap window for coordination

*Uses Version Y's approach - eliminates legal risks while maintaining reasonable coordination expectations.*

## Meeting Norms

### Meeting Standards
- **Default**: All meetings accommodate both in-person and remote participants equally
- **Video-Required Meetings**: Must be scheduled in equipped conference rooms with 48-hour advance notice
- **Backup Protocol**: If video rooms unavailable, meeting moves to all-remote format
- **Documentation**: Meeting notes with decisions and action items required within 24 hours

*Uses Version X's structured approach to ensure video meetings actually work.*

### Timezone Management
- **Core Collaboration Hours**: 10 AM - 12 PM EST (7 AM - 9 AM PST, 4 PM - 6 PM CET)
- **Response Expectations**: 
  - Urgent: 4 hours during local business hours
  - Standard: 24 hours
- **EU Integration**: Available during core hours for active project coordination

*Uses Version Y's corrected timezone window that avoids European dinner time.*

## Equipment and Technology Policy

### Standard Equipment (All Employees)
- Laptop ($1,500 budget including setup and shipping)
- Docking station ($200 budget)
- External monitor ($400 budget)
- Headset ($150 budget)
- **Total**: $2,250 equipment budget per employee

*Uses Version X's realistic budget that accounts for procurement reality.*

### Office Resources
- **Desk Policy**: Shared desk space with mandatory reservation system
  - 25 desks available for 50 employees
  - Reserve desk 24 hours in advance via online system
  - Violations result in loss of desk reservation privileges for 30 days
- **Meeting Room Infrastructure**: 4 rooms with professional video conferencing setup, 2 rooms for in-person only meetings
- **Booking**: Mandatory 48-hour advance booking for video rooms, 24-hour for standard rooms

*Combines Version Y's realistic desk count with Version X's enforcement mechanism and expanded video room capacity.*

## Performance Evaluation Approach

### Universal Standards (All Functions)
- **Goal Achievement**: Measurable objectives met by deadline (0-100% scale)
- **Communication Effectiveness**: Response time compliance during core hours
- **Collaboration Quality**: Project completion rates and stakeholder satisfaction for collaborative work
- **Office Presence**: Attendance tracking for pattern identification focused on business impact

*Uses Version Y's business-outcome focus while maintaining Version X's measurable standards.*

### Performance Issue Process
**Attendance Pattern Concerns:**
- **Any Function**: Chronic absence from required days triggers conversation about work arrangement effectiveness
- **Resolution**: Collaborative problem-solving to address barriers to office attendance or adjust role expectations
- **Documentation**: Focus on business impact rather than compliance percentages

*Uses Version Y's supportive approach rather than punitive compliance tracking.*

## Compliance Tracking

### Attendance Verification
- **Manager Observation**: Department heads track attendance patterns through team interaction and business outcomes
- **Focus**: Identifying employees who may need support or role adjustment rather than punitive compliance
- **Business Impact**: Track correlation between attendance patterns and team/individual performance outcomes

*Uses Version Y's practical approach that focuses on outcomes rather than badge tracking.*

### Reporting
- **Monthly**: Department heads review attendance patterns and business impact
- **Quarterly**: Individual performance review including work arrangement effectiveness
