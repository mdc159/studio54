## Critical Review of the Proposal

### Real Problems Identified:

1. **Engineering Requirements Still Too Demanding**: "1 day per quarter" may seem minimal, but for a remote-first team that's "resistant to change," even quarterly forced office days could trigger departures. The proposal doesn't address what happens if key engineers refuse.

2. **Sales Coverage Math Still Broken**: "Minimum 10 reps Tuesday-Thursday" with 15 total reps means 67% attendance, but the "high performer exception" could still create coverage gaps if multiple top performers opt out simultaneously.

3. **Budget Underestimates Hidden Costs**: $28,500 doesn't include productivity loss during transition, management time for enforcement, or replacement costs if people quit. Also missing: office snacks, utilities increase, cleaning costs for higher occupancy.

4. **EU Integration Remains Tokenistic**: 2 visits per year doesn't solve daily collaboration needs. The 2-4 PM CET overlap is only 2 hours and assumes EU people work late regularly.

5. **Implementation Timeline Ignores Resistance**: "Week 4: Full Implementation" with enforcement assumes compliance. No plan for what happens when engineering team pushes back or key people threaten to quit.

6. **Performance Metrics Create Surveillance Culture**: "Badge-in or Slack check-in" contradicts the trust-based culture that attracted remote-first talent.

7. **Equipment Policy Assumes Everyone Needs Office Setup**: Many remote workers deliberately don't want office equipment. Forcing dual setups wastes money.

8. **Meeting Standards Too Rigid**: "Core collaboration hours" and "primary windows" don't account for startup urgency and changing priorities.

9. **Policy Doesn't Address Core Conflict**: Engineering wants remote-first, Sales wants office culture. The policy tries to split the difference instead of solving the underlying collaboration problem.

10. **No Exit Strategy**: What happens if this doesn't work? The policy locks the company into an expensive, potentially unsuccessful approach.

# Hybrid Work Policy - Conflict Resolution Approach
**Effective Date:** [Date + 4 weeks]  
**Policy Version:** 1.0 (3-month checkpoint, 6-month major review)

## Executive Summary

This policy resolves the Engineering vs. Sales conflict through **outcome-based flexibility** rather than attendance mandates. **Implementation budget: $18,500** focused on collaboration tools, not office requirements.

**Core Principle**: Teams earn autonomy through results delivery. Office access supports collaboration but doesn't mandate it.

## The Real Problem We're Solving

**Engineering's Fear**: Forced office time will kill productivity and drive talent away  
**Sales' Need**: Face-to-face collaboration for complex deals and team energy  
**CEO's Challenge**: Keep both teams effective without losing key people

**Our Solution**: Results-first policy with team-specific collaboration requirements.

## Team Operating Models

### Engineering Team (20 people) - Earned Autonomy Model
**Default State**: Fully remote with voluntary office access
**Earning Autonomy**: Teams that hit delivery targets get maximum scheduling freedom
**Office Requirements**: None, unless delivery targets are missed

**Delivery Targets** (existing metrics, no changes):
- **Sprint commitments**: >85% completion rate (measured monthly)
- **Code quality**: <5% critical bug rate post-deployment
- **Feature delivery**: On-time delivery for quarterly roadmap items

**Office Access Available**:
- **Voluntary collaboration days**: Wednesdays (advance notice in Slack)
- **Project war rooms**: Available for complex features requiring intensive collaboration
- **All-hands events**: Quarterly company meetings (optional attendance, full remote participation)

**If Targets Missed**:
- **Month 1 miss**: Engineering lead works with team to identify issues
- **Month 2 consecutive miss**: Team discusses whether office collaboration would help
- **Month 3 consecutive miss**: CEO and engineering lead develop intervention plan (may include office time)

### Sales Team (15 people) - Performance-Based Flexibility
**Baseline Requirement**: 2 days per week in office (Tuesday/Wednesday)
**Earning Flexibility**: Reps at quota or above can modify their schedule

**Performance Tiers**:
- **Above 120% quota**: Choose your own schedule (must maintain team meeting attendance)
- **100-120% quota**: Standard 2 days/week (Tuesday/Wednesday)  
- **Below 100% quota**: 3 days/week (Monday/Tuesday/Wednesday) for additional coaching and collaboration

**Team Coverage Guarantee**:
- **Minimum 8 reps in office Tuesday/Wednesday** (covers client meetings and team energy)
- **High performers coordinate**: If multiple top performers want same days off, they coordinate coverage
- **Manager override**: Sales director can require specific people for important client meetings (48-hour notice)

**Monthly Schedule Planning**: Teams plan coverage 1 month ahead, adjust weekly as needed

### Other Functions (15 people) - Hybrid Standard
**Baseline**: 1 day per week + project-based needs
**Fixed Day**: Wednesday (ensures cross-team overlap)
**Project Surges**: Additional days for launches, events, or cross-team initiatives (planned monthly)
**Flexibility**: High performers in any function can negotiate modified schedules with their manager

### EU Team Members (3 people) - Remote Integration Focus
**Office Visits**: 1 visit per year (1 week) aligned with company planning sessions
**Daily Integration**: Focus on async collaboration with scheduled overlap

**Scheduled Overlap**: 
- **Tuesday 9 AM EST / 2 PM CET**: 1-hour cross-team sync (rotating agenda)
- **Wednesday 10 AM EST / 3 PM CET**: All-hands participation
- **Thursday 8 AM EST / 1 PM CET**: EU team leads check-in with US counterparts

**Local Support**: 
- **Co-working allowance**: €100/month (researched actual usage rates)
- **Home office setup**: Same equipment standards as US team
- **Timezone accommodation**: 25% of meetings scheduled for EU-friendly times
