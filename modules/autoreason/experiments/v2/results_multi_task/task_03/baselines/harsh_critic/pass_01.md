# HYBRID WORK POLICY - REVISED
**Effective Date:** [Date]  
**Policy Version:** 1.0

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a performance-driven hybrid model that maximizes Engineering productivity while enabling Sales collaboration, with clear enforcement mechanisms and economic accountability. **Decision: Remote-first with mandatory collaboration touchpoints.**

## Core Framework

### The Three-Pillar Model
1. **Individual Productivity** (70% of work) - Location irrelevant
2. **Team Collaboration** (20% of work) - Function-specific requirements  
3. **Company Alignment** (10% of work) - Universal in-person requirements

## Department-Specific Requirements

### Engineering Team (20 people)
**Base Model:** Fully remote with structured touchpoints
- **Mandatory In-Person:** 8 days per quarter (2 days monthly + quarterly planning)
  - Monthly architecture review (1 day)
  - Monthly cross-team integration (1 day)  
  - Quarterly planning sprint (2 consecutive days)
- **Advance Notice:** All mandatory days scheduled quarterly with 90-day notice
- **Enforcement:** Missing >50% of mandatory days triggers performance review

### Sales Team (15 people)  
**Base Model:** Office-centric with remote flexibility
- **Mandatory In-Office:** Tuesday-Thursday (3 days/week)
- **Remote Options:** Monday/Friday for prospecting, follow-up, admin
- **Client Exception:** In-person client meetings override office requirements
- **Enforcement:** <80% office compliance over 30 days triggers coaching

### All Other Functions (15 people)
**Base Model:** Hybrid with team coordination
- **Mandatory In-Office:** Wednesday (company alignment) + 1 team-choice day
- **Department Clustering:** Teams choose same secondary day for collaboration
- **Enforcement:** Department heads track and address non-compliance monthly

### Universal Requirements (All 50 people)
- **Monthly All-Hands:** First Wednesday of each month (mandatory in-person)
- **Quarterly Business Reviews:** Full day, all-hands (mandatory in-person)
- **New Hire Onboarding:** First 10 business days in office
- **Performance Improvement:** Increased office presence as determined by manager

## Timezone and EU Team Management

### EU Team Integration (3 people)
**Reality Check:** EU team cannot fully integrate with US-centric operations
- **Core EU Hours:** 9 AM - 1 PM CET (3 AM - 7 AM EST overlap)
- **US Integration:** 2 PM - 6 PM CET (8 AM - 12 PM EST overlap)
- **Quarterly US Visits:** 1 week per quarter, company-sponsored
- **Local Autonomy:** EU team operates semi-independently with weekly US sync

### Meeting Protocol
**Fixed Schedule Approach:**
- **Daily Standups:** 8:30 AM EST / 2:30 PM CET (15 minutes max)
- **Weekly Team Meetings:** Rotating between 9 AM EST and 1 PM EST monthly
- **Monthly All-Hands:** 10 AM EST (4 PM CET) - EU team joins virtually when in Europe
- **No Meeting Fridays:** EU team focus time

### Communication Standards
- **Urgent (0-2 hours):** Phone call + Slack
- **Same Day (2-8 hours):** Direct Slack message
- **Next Business Day:** Email or async documentation
- **EU Handoffs:** All work requiring EU input submitted by 2 PM EST

## Equipment and Office Policy

### Equipment Allocation
**One Location, Full Setup:**
- Employees choose primary work location (home OR office)
- Company provides complete setup for chosen location only
- Hot-desking setup for secondary location (monitor, keyboard, mouse only)

**Home Office (35 people maximum):**
- $1,500 setup stipend (one-time)
- MacBook Pro, external monitor, desk accessories
- $300/year maintenance allowance

**Office Primary (15 people maximum):**
- Assigned desk with full setup
- Take-home laptop only
- No additional stipend

### Office Space Management
**Assigned Desks:** Sales team (15) + 5 floating desks for other functions
**Hot-Desking Zone:** 15 stations for remote-primary employees visiting
**Meeting Rooms:** 4 rooms, bookable 2 weeks in advance
**Enforcement:** Assigned desk holders must use desk minimum 60% of required office days

## Performance Evaluation

### Universal Performance Standards
**Quarterly Review Cycle:**
1. **Goal Achievement** (40% weight) - Measurable deliverables
2. **Collaboration Effectiveness** (30% weight) - 360-degree feedback scores
3. **Communication Quality** (20% weight) - Response time, clarity, proactivity
4. **Company Culture** (10% weight) - Values demonstration, team support

### Location-Agnostic Metrics
**Engineering:**
- Story points completed vs. committed
- Code review participation and quality
- Documentation contributions
- Cross-team collaboration scores (peer feedback)

**Sales:**
- Revenue vs. target
- Pipeline development and conversion
- Account relationship depth scores
- Team knowledge sharing participation

**Other Functions:**
- Project delivery vs. timeline
- Stakeholder satisfaction scores  
- Process improvement contributions
- Cross-functional collaboration effectiveness

### Performance Review Process
- **Monthly 1:1s:** Mandatory, video-first
- **Quarterly Reviews:** Structured feedback with specific examples
- **Annual Reviews:** Comprehensive evaluation with development planning
- **Location Bias Prevention:** All reviews conducted via video, standardized rubrics

## Implementation and Enforcement

### Phase 1: Setup (Weeks 1-4)
- Employee location choice declaration (binding for 12 months)
- Equipment procurement and office desk assignment
- Calendar blocking for all mandatory in-person days
- Performance metric baseline establishment

### Phase 2: Pilot (Weeks 5-8)  
- Soft enforcement with weekly feedback collection
- Office utilization tracking and adjustment
- Meeting schedule optimization
- EU team integration refinement

### Phase 3: Full Enforcement (Week 9+)
- Compliance tracking begins
- Monthly performance discussions include policy adherence
- Quarterly policy effectiveness review

### Compliance and Consequences
**Tracking Metrics:**
- Badge swipe data for office requirements
- Meeting attendance for mandatory sessions
- Performance metric achievement
- Peer feedback scores

**Escalation Process:**
1. **First Violation:** Manager coaching conversation
2. **Pattern Violation:** Formal improvement plan with HR
3. **Continued Non-Compliance:** Performance improvement plan with potential termination
