Here are real problems with this proposal:

**Market and Competition**

1. **Courier, Knock, Novu, Engagespot, and Notifi all exist** and are well-funded. The pitch positions AWS SNS and OneSignal as the only alternatives, which is a significant competitive blind spot that any informed investor will immediately notice. This undermines credibility.

2. **The "8,000 companies" TAM is thin and poorly constructed.** Companies in the 1-50M MAU band are not all notification-infrastructure buyers. Many already have solutions, many are in verticals where notifications are trivial, and many will never reach that scale. The number is presented with false precision.

3. **The target band is temporary by definition.** The pitch acknowledges customers age out of it when they staff a platform team. This means you're constantly churning your best customers as they succeed, and the replacement pipeline depends entirely on new companies entering the band.

**Business Model and Metrics**

4. **$38K MRR from 47 customers after 8 months is weak for infrastructure.** That's roughly $800 average — consistent with the disclosure — but it signals the free tier is converting small, not valuable, customers. The expansion revenue claim needs data to support it.

5. **The free tier economics are unaddressed.** Delivering notifications at scale is not free. The cost structure of the free tier — compute, carrier fees, support burden — is never mentioned. "Not a subsidy problem" is an assertion, not a demonstration.

6. **No churn data is provided.** For developer infrastructure, churn is the central question. Its absence is conspicuous.

**Reliability Claims**

7. **The 99.97% vs. 94% comparison is methodologically suspect.** The pitch defines their metric as "gateway acceptance plus confirmed downstream delivery where platforms return it" — but the 94% industry average almost certainly uses a different measurement. Comparing self-defined metrics to an industry average without a common methodology is not a meaningful comparison.

8. **"Carrier relationships" is vague and possibly not defensible.** Carrier relationships as a moat erodes quickly. Other well-capitalized competitors have them too.

**Team and Execution**

9. **Three infrastructure engineers with no distribution is not just a stated risk — it's evidence.** Eight months in, $38K MRR suggests the product may be technically sound but commercially underdeveloped. Acknowledging it doesn't neutralize it.

10. **The SOC2 gap is more damaging than framed.** Three stalled enterprise deals means pipeline has been sitting idle while the company operated without basic enterprise hygiene. This suggests the team underestimated go-to-market requirements, not just a correctable oversight.

11. **"Built by engineers who ran these systems at Twilio and AWS SNS" is unverified and vague.** Ran what, at what level, with what scope? This credential is doing heavy lifting without specifics.

**Fundraising**

12. **$2M for two engineers, SOC2, and 18 months of runway is a thin plan.** It implies very low burn, which is consistent with three founders but means the company will still be small and distribution-constrained at the end of the runway. The path from that endpoint to a Series A is not articulated.
