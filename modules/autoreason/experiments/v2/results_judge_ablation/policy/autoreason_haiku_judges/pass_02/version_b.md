MEMORANDUM

SUBJECT: Artificial Intelligence Tool Usage Policy

1. SCOPE
   1.1 This policy applies to all employees of [Company Name]
   1.2 Covers all AI tools, including:
      a) Code generation and assistance
      b) Text generation and editing
      c) Conversational and productivity tools
   1.3 Effective immediately upon distribution
   1.4 Supersedes all previous informal practices regarding AI tool usage

2. PERMITTED USES
   2.1 Authorized AI Tools:
      a) GitHub Copilot Business (code generation)
      b) Company-approved platforms identified by IT and Legal
   2.2 Engineers may use GitHub Copilot for code completion and suggestion purposes, with the following mandatory controls:
      a) Prohibit direct paste of customer database schemas or full proprietary code blocks
      b) Require manual review and modification of all AI-suggested code
   2.3 Sales team may use approved AI tools for email drafting, with:
      a) Mandatory originality check to prevent verbatim copying
      b) Explicit removal of any detected third-party marketing language
   2.4 AI-generated content requirements:
      a) Minimum 40% manual modification of generated content
      b) Explicit documentation of AI tool usage and modifications
      c) Preservation of original attribution and potential non-copyrightability

3. PROHIBITED USES
   3.1 Prohibited Actions (Ref: Base Fact #2, Incidents #1-3):
      a) Inputting customer PII into external AI services
      b) Generating complete work products without minimum 40% manual modification
      c) Using AI-generated text or code with unverified copyright status
      d) Incorporating third-party license headers without explicit legal review
      e) Inputting proprietary architectural diagrams into external AI services
   3.2 Compliance Violations:
      a) Using unapproved AI tools that may compromise:
         i. Pending FedRAMP authorization requirements
         ii. Existing GDPR data protection agreements
         iii. Current SOC2 Type II certification standards

4. ENFORCEMENT
   4.1 Violation Assessment Criteria:
      a) First Violation: Quantitative assessment
         i. Less than 40% manual content modification
         ii. Unauthorized AI tool usage
         Action: Mandatory retraining, written documentation
      b) Second Violation: Potential compliance risk
         i. Repeated pattern of unauthorized usage
         ii. Evidence of intentional policy circumvention
         Action: Performance improvement plan, potential suspension
      c) Third Violation: Severe compliance breach
         i. Demonstrable risk to company IP or customer data
         ii. Repeated disregard of previous warnings
         Action: Potential termination

   4.2 Compliance Oversight:
      a) Managers conduct monthly team AI usage reviews
      b) Quarterly comprehensive audits by IT and Legal
      c) Annual mandatory AI tool usage and compliance training
