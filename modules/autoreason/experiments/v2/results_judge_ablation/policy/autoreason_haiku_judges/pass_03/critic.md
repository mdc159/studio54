STRUCTURAL PROBLEMS:

2. Section 4.1's enforcement escalation seems arbitrary - there's no clear distinction between what constitutes a first vs. second violation

COMPLIANCE GAPS:
3. The policy does not address the specific legal counsel warning about code copyrightability (referenced in base facts), despite mentioning copyright verification

4. No clear mechanism exists for how the "substantive review" requirement in 2.4 will be operationalized or documented

TECHNICAL INCONSISTENCIES:
5. Section 2.1(b) creates an undefined loophole with "Company-approved platforms identified by IT and Legal" without specifying a process for such identification

6. The policy claims to use existing access controls (per constraints) but introduces new reporting and audit mechanisms that suggest additional infrastructure would be required

RISK MANAGEMENT FAILURES:
7. The policy does not address the specific incidents mentioned in the base facts (database schema paste, competitor marketing copy usage, GPL license header issue) with targeted preventative language

8. No explicit guidance on how AI-generated work interacts with existing intellectual property assignment agreements
