After a critical review, I've identified several substantive problems:

CRITICAL POLICY FLAWS:

1. Scope Problem
- Section 1.2 creates ambiguity about what constitutes an "AI tool"
- No clear definition distinguishes between AI assistants, plugins, and general software
- Lacks specificity that could lead to inconsistent enforcement

2. Compliance Contradiction
- Section 3.2 references compliance standards (SOC2, GDPR, FedRAMP) without concrete mechanisms for verification
- No clear process exists to ensure AI tool usage actually meets these standards
- Compliance claims appear performative rather than actionable

3. Enforcement Weakness
- Section 4.1 enforcement mechanisms are vague and potentially legally unenforceable
- "Potential financial liability" is not defined
- Termination criteria lack specificity

4. Intellectual Property Risk
- Section 4.4 does not address the specific legal counsel warning about non-copyrightability of AI-generated code
- Fails to create a clear framework for IP ownership of AI-assisted work

5. Practical Implementation Gap
- No mechanism exists to track or validate the "line-by-line review" requirement in 2.4
- No technical controls proposed to prevent prohibited actions
- Relies entirely on employee self-reporting and manual oversight

These problems create significant policy implementation risks.
