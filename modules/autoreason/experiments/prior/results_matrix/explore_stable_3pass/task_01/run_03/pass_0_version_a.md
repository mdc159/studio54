# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes CLI Configuration Tool

## Executive Summary

This strategy focuses on converting your existing developer mindshare into sustainable revenue through a targeted approach to DevOps teams at mid-market companies. The plan leverages your 5k GitHub stars as social proof while building enterprise-grade features and support services that justify paid subscriptions.

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary Segment: Mid-Market DevOps Teams (50-500 employees)
**Profile:**
- Companies with 5-20 Kubernetes clusters
- DevOps teams of 3-15 engineers
- Annual infrastructure budgets of $100K-$1M
- Pain points: Config drift, compliance, multi-environment management

**Why this segment:**
- Large enough budgets to pay for tools ($2K-$20K annually)
- Complex enough needs to require advanced features
- Small enough to make direct sales feasible with 3-person team
- Less bureaucratic procurement than enterprise

### Secondary Segment: Platform Engineering Teams at Scale-ups
**Profile:**
- Series B-D companies (100-1000 employees)
- Managing platform for 50-200 internal developers
- Building internal developer platforms
- Need standardization and governance at scale

### Tertiary Segment: Individual Contributors & Small Teams
**Profile:**
- Senior engineers at any company size
- Open-source maintainers
- Consultants and freelancers
- Currently your core GitHub audience

## Pricing Model

### Three-Tier SaaS Model

**Free Tier (Community)**
- Current open-source functionality
- Up to 3 clusters
- Basic CLI features
- Community support only
- *Goal: Maintain adoption funnel and community*

**Professional ($49/user/month)**
- Unlimited clusters
- Advanced config validation and drift detection
- Policy enforcement
- SSO integration
- Email support
- Audit logging
- *Target: 5-person DevOps teams = $245/month*

**Enterprise ($149/user/month)**
- Multi-tenant management
- Advanced RBAC
- Compliance reporting (SOC2, PCI, HIPAA templates)
- Custom policies and templates
- Dedicated support channel
- Professional services credits
- *Target: 15-person teams = $2,235/month*

**Rationale:**
- Usage-based pricing aligns value with scale
- Professional tier targets sweet spot of mid-market budgets
- Enterprise tier justifiable for compliance-heavy industries

## What NOT to Do Yet

### 1. Enterprise Sales Team
**Why not:** With only 3 people, building enterprise sales capability would consume entire team bandwidth. Direct founder-led sales more effective at this stage.

### 2. Multi-Product Strategy
**Why not:** Dilutes focus and confuses market positioning. Master one tool before expanding portfolio.

### 3. On-Premise Deployment
**Why not:** Operational complexity would overwhelm small team. SaaS-only keeps operational burden manageable.

### 4. International Expansion
**Why not:** US/English-speaking markets provide sufficient TAM for year one. Localization adds complexity without proportional returns.

### 5. Venture Capital Fundraising
**Why not:** Focus on achieving default-alive state first. Strong unit economics will enable better fundraising terms later if needed.

### 6. White-Label/OEM Partnerships
**Why not:** Complex integration work that distracts from core product development and direct customer acquisition.
