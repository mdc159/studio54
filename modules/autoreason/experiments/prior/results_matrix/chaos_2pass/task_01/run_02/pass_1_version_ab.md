# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes Config CLI Tool (VERSION AB)

## Executive Summary

This GTM strategy focuses on monetizing enterprise compliance and governance requirements that cannot be addressed through open-source contributions alone. With 5k GitHub stars indicating strong developer adoption, we'll build a sustainable business by solving mandatory enterprise problems while maintaining the open-source core that drives community growth.

**Key Insight:** Mid-market companies will pay for compliance and governance capabilities they cannot build themselves, but won't pay premium prices for enhanced versions of free developer tools.

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary: Mid-Market Technology Companies with Compliance Requirements (100-1000 employees)
**Profile:**
- Companies with 10-50 Kubernetes clusters in regulated environments
- 5-20 platform/DevOps engineers with compliance mandates (SOC2, HIPAA, PCI)
- Already using your OSS tool in production
- Platform team budget authority for compliance tools ($50K-200K annually)

**Value Proposition:** Meet mandatory compliance requirements for Kubernetes config management with audit trails, governance controls, and commercial support that cannot be satisfied by open-source tools.

**Rationale for Change:** Version A's mid-market focus was correct for deal size and sales efficiency, but Version B's compliance angle solves the "why pay for this?" problem. Combined: target mid-market companies but only those with compliance mandates.

### Secondary: Platform Engineering Teams at Fortune 500 Companies
**Profile:**
- Large enterprises (500+ employees) with dedicated platform teams
- Managing 20+ clusters across multiple regulated environments
- Strict security/compliance requirements with audit obligations
- Budget authority for enterprise governance tools ($100K-500K annually)

**Value Proposition:** Enterprise-grade governance and compliance controls that enable auditable self-service config management for development teams.

## Pricing Model

### Commercial Compliance + Open Core Strategy

**Community Edition (Free):**
- CLI tool with full config management functionality
- Basic validation and templating capabilities
- Community support via GitHub/Discord
- Unlimited use (no artificial limitations)

**Professional Edition ($99/user/month, 10-user minimum):**
- Audit trail and compliance reporting capabilities
- Team approval workflows with governance controls
- SSO integration with enterprise identity providers
- Commercial support with 24-hour SLA for critical issues
- Compliance documentation package

**Enterprise Edition ($199/user/month, 25-user minimum):**
- Advanced compliance reporting (SOC2, HIPAA, PCI templates)
- Role-based access controls with policy enforcement
- Professional liability coverage and commercial licensing
- Dedicated customer success manager
- Priority feature development influence
- Custom compliance integration development

**Rationale for Change:** Version A's per-user pricing structure works for mid-market sales efficiency, but Version B's compliance focus solves the value proposition problem. Combined: per-user pricing but justified by compliance value that scales with team size.

## What We Will Explicitly NOT Do (Year 1)

### Product Scope
- **No feature paywalls on existing OSS functionality:** All current CLI features remain free
- **No SaaS hosting or dashboards:** Stay CLI-first to maintain developer appeal and avoid infrastructure complexity
- **No custom consulting/training:** Focus on product-based compliance solutions only
- **No non-compliance feature development:** Every paid feature must solve a compliance/governance requirement

### Go-to-Market Scope
- **No horizontal marketing to non-regulated companies:** Focus exclusively on compliance-driven buyers
- **No individual developer plans:** Team-focused pricing only with governance value
- **No international expansion:** US compliance market only for Year 1
- **No venture capital in Year 1:** Bootstrap through early compliance contracts to maintain focus

### Market Approach
- **No broad conference advertising:** Compliance and security events only
- **No reseller partnerships:** Direct sales to maintain compliance expertise and customer relationships
- **No sub-$50K deals:** Minimum viable compliance engagement size

**Rationale for Change:** Version A's scope limitations were practical, but Version B's compliance focus provides better strategic discipline. Combined: maintain A's practical constraints while adding B's strategic focus on compliance-only development.
