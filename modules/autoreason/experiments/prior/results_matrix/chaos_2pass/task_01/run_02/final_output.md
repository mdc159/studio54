# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes Config CLI Tool (VERSION AB)

## Executive Summary

This GTM strategy focuses on serving mid-market technology companies (100-1000 employees) with compliance requirements through enterprise-grade configuration management capabilities. With 5K GitHub stars indicating strong developer adoption, we'll build a sustainable business by solving mandatory compliance problems that platform teams cannot address with free tools alone.

**Key Insight:** Platform engineering teams at regulated mid-market companies will pay for compliance capabilities they cannot build themselves, delivered through familiar CLI tools rather than complex enterprise platforms.

**Synthesis Rationale:** Version B correctly identifies that platform engineering teams are the actual buyers with budget authority, but Version A's mid-market focus enables better sales efficiency and shorter cycles than enterprise-only. The compliance angle from Version A solves the "why pay for free tools?" problem while Version B's buyer identification ensures we target decision makers.

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary: Platform Engineering Teams at Regulated Mid-Market Companies (100-1000 employees)
**Profile:**
- Technology companies with 10-50 Kubernetes clusters in regulated environments (SOC2, HIPAA, PCI)
- 5-20 platform/DevOps engineers with compliance mandates and budget authority ($50K-200K annually)
- Already using the OSS tool in production with demonstrated value
- Technical buyers who also control purchasing decisions (faster sales cycles)

**Value Proposition:** Meet mandatory compliance requirements for Kubernetes config management with audit trails, governance controls, and commercial support that cannot be satisfied by open-source tools.

**Synthesis Rationale:** Version A's mid-market sizing is correct for deal efficiency, but Version B's identification of platform teams as the actual buyers prevents the compliance officer targeting mistake. Combined: platform teams at mid-market regulated companies where technical buyers have budget authority.

### Secondary: Platform Engineering Teams at Fortune 500 Companies
**Profile:**
- Large enterprises (500+ employees) with dedicated platform teams
- Managing 20+ clusters across multiple regulated environments
- Platform team budget authority for governance tools ($100K-500K annually)
- Longer sales cycles but higher deal values

**Value Proposition:** Enterprise-grade compliance and governance controls that enable auditable self-service config management at scale.

## Pricing Model

### Commercial Compliance + CLI-First Strategy

**Community Edition (Free):**
- CLI tool with full config management functionality
- Basic validation and templating capabilities
- Community support via GitHub/Discord
- Unlimited use (no artificial limitations)

**Professional Edition ($99/user/month, 10-user minimum):**
- Audit trail and compliance reporting capabilities
- Team approval workflows with governance controls
- SSO integration with enterprise identity providers
- Commercial support with 24-hour SLA
- Compliance documentation package

**Enterprise Edition ($25,000 annual site license + $150/user/month for 25+ users):**
- Advanced compliance reporting (SOC2, HIPAA, PCI templates)
- Role-based access controls with policy enforcement
- Professional liability coverage and commercial licensing
- Dedicated customer success manager
- Advanced templating engine with enterprise integrations

**Synthesis Rationale:** Version A's per-user pricing works for mid-market, but Version B's enterprise site license hybrid is necessary for larger deals. The compliance focus from Version A provides clear value justification while Version B's pricing structure accommodates different market segments efficiently.

## What We Will Explicitly NOT Do (Year 1)

### Product Scope
- **No feature paywalls on existing OSS functionality:** All current CLI features remain free
- **No SaaS hosting or compliance dashboards:** Stay CLI-first to avoid infrastructure complexity while maintaining compliance value through reporting
- **No custom consulting/training:** Focus on product-based compliance solutions only
- **No non-compliance feature development:** Every paid feature must solve compliance/governance requirements for platform teams

### Go-to-Market Scope
- **No horizontal marketing to non-regulated companies:** Focus exclusively on compliance-driven platform teams
- **No individual developer plans:** Team-focused pricing only with governance value
- **No international expansion:** US compliance market only for Year 1
- **No venture capital in Year 1:** Bootstrap through compliance contracts to maintain strategic focus

### Market Approach
- **No broad developer conferences:** Platform engineering and security events only
- **No reseller partnerships:** Direct sales to maintain compliance expertise and customer relationships
- **No sub-$25K annual deals:** Minimum viable compliance engagement size for platform teams

**Synthesis Rationale:** Version A's compliance-only development discipline prevents feature drift, while Version B's CLI-first constraint avoids infrastructure complexity. Combined focus ensures every investment serves regulated platform teams specifically.
