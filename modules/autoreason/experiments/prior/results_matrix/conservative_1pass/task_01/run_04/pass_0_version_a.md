# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes CLI Configuration Tool

## Executive Summary

This GTM strategy focuses on converting your existing 5k GitHub star community into revenue through a freemium SaaS model targeting DevOps engineers and platform teams. With a 3-person team, we'll prioritize high-impact, low-resource activities that leverage your existing open-source momentum.

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary Segment: Mid-Market DevOps Teams (50-500 employees)
**Profile:**
- Companies with 5-50 Kubernetes clusters
- DevOps teams of 3-15 engineers
- Annual infrastructure spend: $100K-$2M
- Pain points: Configuration drift, compliance auditing, multi-environment management

**Why this segment:**
- Large enough budgets for tooling ($5K-$50K annually)
- Small enough to have direct access to decision makers
- Complex enough to need advanced features beyond open-source

### Secondary Segment: Platform Engineering Teams at Scale-ups
**Profile:**
- Series B-D companies (200-2000 employees)
- Dedicated platform/infrastructure teams
- Managing 20+ microservices across multiple environments
- Pain points: Developer self-service, standardization, governance

**Why this segment:**
- High willingness to pay for developer productivity
- Clear ROI metrics (developer time savings)
- Often have budget allocated for "developer experience"

## Pricing Model

### Freemium SaaS with Open-Source Core

**Free Tier (Open Source CLI + Basic Cloud Features):**
- Core CLI functionality (current open-source features)
- Basic web dashboard
- Up to 3 clusters
- Community support
- Individual user accounts

**Professional Tier - $49/user/month:**
- Unlimited clusters
- Advanced policy enforcement
- Audit logging and compliance reports
- RBAC and team management
- Slack/Teams integrations
- Email support with 24-hour SLA

**Enterprise Tier - $149/user/month:**
- SSO/SAML integration
- Advanced analytics and cost optimization
- Custom policy templates
- Dedicated customer success manager
- Phone support with 4-hour SLA
- On-premise deployment option

**Rationale:**
- Per-user pricing aligns with value delivery (each user gets productivity gains)
- Price points tested in similar DevOps tools market
- Clear upgrade path from free to paid tiers

## Distribution Channels

### Primary Channels (70% of effort):

**1. Product-Led Growth via Open Source**
- Convert GitHub stars to trial users through in-CLI upgrade prompts
- Add "powered by [Company]" branding to CLI output
- Implement telemetry (opt-in) to identify high-usage candidates for outreach

**2. Developer Community Engagement**
- Weekly technical blog posts on Kubernetes best practices
- Monthly webinars on configuration management
- Active participation in KubeCon, local Kubernetes meetups
- Sponsor 2-3 key developer conferences annually

**3. Direct Sales to Warm Leads**
- Outbound to companies whose engineers star/fork the repo
- LinkedIn outreach to DevOps engineers at target companies
- Referral program offering 3 months free for successful referrals

### Secondary Channels (30% of effort):

**4. Partner Integrations**
- Marketplace listings (AWS, GCP, Azure)
- Integration partnerships with GitLab, ArgoCD, Flux
- Reseller partnerships with DevOps consultancies

## What We Explicitly Won't Do Yet

### Enterprise Sales Motion
- **Why not:** Requires dedicated sales team and 6-12 month cycles
- **When to revisit:** After reaching $50K MRR with product-led growth

### Multi-Product Strategy
- **Why not:** Dilutes focus with limited team resources
- **When to revisit:** After achieving strong product-market fit with core offering

### Extensive Channel Partner Program
- **Why not:** Requires significant partner enablement resources
- **When to revisit:** After establishing repeatable direct sales process

### International Expansion
- **Why not:** Adds complexity in support, compliance, and localization
- **When to revisit:** After dominating English-speaking markets

### On-Premise First Strategy
- **Why not:** Higher support costs and slower iteration cycles
- **When to revisit:** When enterprise customers specifically demand it
