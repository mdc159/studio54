## Objection Handling

### "On-premise solutions are more expensive"
**Response:** "Yes, the initial infrastructure investment is higher, but consider the total cost of a security breach or regulatory violation. Our customers typically see ROI within 12-18 months when factoring in security team productivity gains and intellectual property protection. You also eliminate per-developer licensing fees and external data exposure risks."

### "We don't trust AI for security decisions" *(Added B's critical objection)*
**Response:** "Neither should you—SecureCode AI doesn't make security decisions. It identifies potential issues for your security team to evaluate, similar to SAST tools. Your security engineers remain the decision makers; AI provides additional analysis capacity and consistency."

### "What about false positives overwhelming our team?"
**Response:** "We expect 15-25% false positives initially, decreasing to <15% after optimization. More importantly, we integrate with your existing security workflows and provide confidence scoring, so your team can prioritize high-confidence findings first. Many customers report this still reduces overall security review time by 30-40%."

### "How do we know AI quality matches cloud providers?"
**Response:** "Our models are trained on equivalent datasets and optimized specifically for security analysis. We can fine-tune on your codebase for superior accuracy on your specific patterns. In security-focused benchmarks, we match or exceed cloud solutions, with the advantage of understanding your unique environment."

### "Our developers are already overloaded with tool alerts" *(Added B's workflow concern)*
**Response:** "SecureCode AI findings go to your security team first, not developers. Only validated, high-confidence issues reach developers through your existing security review process. This reduces developer alert fatigue while improving security team efficiency."
