## Justification for Departures from Version A

**1. Flexible Infrastructure Options (vs. Dedicated Only)**
- **Justification:** Version A's dedicated-only approach creates economically unviable unit economics for most prospects. The synthesis maintains dedicated options for customers who truly need them while offering enhanced multi-tenant for broader market access.

**2. Realistic Company Size Thresholds (1,000+ vs. 5,000+)**
- **Justification:** Version A's 5,000+ employee threshold severely limits addressable market. Many 1,000+ employee companies have real compliance needs and budgets that justify the solution.

**3. Flexible Sales Cycle Timelines (6-24 months vs. 18-36 months)**
- **Justification:** Version A's 18-36 month cycles make customer acquisition costs economically impossible. The synthesis recognizes that compliance requirements vary, with some customers moving faster than others.

**4. Tiered Pricing Model (vs. Single Premium Tier)**
- **Justification:** Version A's single premium pricing model limits market penetration. The synthesis maintains premium pricing for dedicated deployments while offering enhanced multi-tenant options for broader adoption.

**5. Enhanced Support Pillar Addition**
- **Justification:** Version B's enterprise support pillar is a realistic differentiator that doesn't require impossible technical architecture changes and provides genuine value to enterprise customers.

All other elements favor Version A's superior compliance positioning, technical reality checks, and market understanding.
