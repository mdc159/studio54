# Blind Evaluation: task_03_pair_04

## Task
Write a remote work policy for a 50-person startup that just went from fully remote to hybrid. Engineering (20 people) is remote-first and resistant to change. Sales (15 people) wants everyone in-office for culture and collaboration. The CEO is undecided and wants a policy that both sides can live with. Cover: required in-office days, meeting norms, equipment policy, performance evaluation approach, and how to handle timezone spread (US East, US West, and 3 people in EU).

---

## Proposal X

# Hybrid Work Policy Proposal
**FlexForward Remote-Hybrid Framework**

## Executive Summary

This policy establishes a compromise framework that preserves engineering autonomy while meeting sales collaboration needs. The approach uses function-based flexibility with shared accountability structures, designed to satisfy both camps while maintaining operational excellence across timezones.

## 1. Required In-Office Days

### Core Framework: "Anchor Days + Function Choice"

**Mandatory Company-Wide Anchor Days:**
- **Tuesdays and Wednesdays** are designated "Collaboration Core Days"
- All US-based employees (35 people) must be in office on these days
- EU team members join virtually during overlap hours (see Section 4)

**Function-Specific Requirements:**

**Engineering (20 people):**
- Required: 2 days/week (Anchor Days)
- Optional: Up to 3 additional days based on project needs
- Sprint planning and retrospectives occur on Anchor Days
- Individual contributors can work fully remote on Mon/Thu/Fri

**Sales (15 people):**
- Required: 4 days/week minimum (Anchor Days + 2 flexible days)
- Recommended: Monday-Thursday for maximum client interaction
- Account reviews and team meetings scheduled on Anchor Days
- One remote day allowed for focused work/client calls

**Other Functions (15 people):**
- Required: 2 days/week (Anchor Days)
- Department heads determine additional requirements based on role needs

### Flexibility Mechanisms:
- **Project Exception Process:** Engineering teams can request temporary full-remote periods during critical development phases (max 4 weeks, requires VP approval)
- **Client Exception Process:** Sales can work remote when traveling for client meetings
- **Personal Exception Process:** 4 remote days/month available for personal needs (advance notice required)

## 2. Meeting Norms

### Meeting Types and Modalities:

**In-Person Required:**
- All-hands meetings (monthly, Anchor Days)
- Sprint planning and retrospectives (Engineering)
- Weekly sales team meetings and pipeline reviews
- Quarterly planning sessions
- New employee onboarding (first week)

**Hybrid Default:**
- Daily standups (in-person participants in conference rooms, remote dial-in)
- Department meetings
- 1:1s between managers and reports
- Client presentations (sales leads in-person, support team remote)

**Remote Default:**
- Individual contributor deep work sessions
- Cross-timezone collaboration with EU team
- Training and development sessions
- Informal coffee chats and social interactions

### Meeting Best Practices:

**Hybrid Meeting Standards:**
- All meetings start with 2-minute tech check
- In-room participants use individual devices for screen sharing
- Meeting notes captured in shared documents with real-time editing
- Action items assigned through project management tools (not just verbal)
- 5-minute buffer between meetings for room transitions

**Scheduling Requirements:**
- Core collaboration hours: 9 AM - 3 PM PT / 12 PM - 6 PM ET
- No mandatory meetings before 9 AM PT or after 6 PM ET
- EU team inclusion: 8 AM - 11 AM PT / 11 AM - 2 PM ET (3-hour daily overlap)
- Friday afternoons reserved for async work (no mandatory meetings after 2 PM PT)

## 3. Equipment and Technology Policy

### Standard Equipment Package (All Employees):
- Laptop (MacBook Pro or equivalent PC)
- External monitor (27" minimum)
- Wireless keyboard and mouse
- Noise-canceling headphones with microphone
- Webcam (if laptop camera insufficient)
- Ergonomic chair (up to $300 reimbursement)
- Desk setup stipend: $500/year

### Location-Specific Equipment:

**Home Office:**
- Internet stipend: $50/month
- Phone stipend: $30/month
- Printer access (shared office space or personal, up to $200/year)
- VPN and security software (company-provided)

**Office Locations:**
- Hot-desking setup with docking stations
- Shared printers and office supplies
- Conference room booking system
- Dedicated phone booths for private calls

### Hybrid-Specific Technology:
- Conference room cameras with speaker tracking
- Wireless presentation systems in all meeting rooms
- Digital whiteboarding tools (Miro/Mural licenses)
- Slack for async communication
- Zoom for video conferencing
- Shared calendar system with room booking

### Security and Access:
- VPN required for all remote work
- Two-factor authentication mandatory
- Company data must remain on company devices
- Monthly security training (remote-friendly format)

## 4. Performance Evaluation Approach

### Evaluation Philosophy: "Outcome-Based Assessment"

**Core Principles:**
- Results matter more than presence
- Transparent goal-setting with measurable outcomes
- Regular feedback loops replace "management by walking around"

### Function-Specific Metrics:

**Engineering:**
- Code quality and review participation
- Sprint commitment accuracy
- Technical debt reduction contributions
- Cross-team collaboration effectiveness (measured via peer feedback)
- Innovation/improvement initiative participation

**Sales:**
- Pipeline generation and conversion rates
- Client relationship quality (NPS scores, retention)
- Team collaboration contributions (knowledge sharing, mentoring)
- Response time to leads and client communications

**All Functions:**
- Goal achievement (quarterly OKRs)
- Communication effectiveness (360 feedback)
- Professional development progress
- Company culture participation

### Evaluation Process:

**Quarterly Check-ins:**
- Self-assessment with specific examples
- Manager evaluation with data-driven metrics
- Peer feedback (2-3 colleagues, rotating quarterly)
- Goal adjustment and next quarter planning

**Annual Reviews:**
- Comprehensive performance summary
- Career development planning
- Compensation and promotion discussions
- Work arrangement preferences review

**Continuous Feedback:**
- Weekly 1:1s (hybrid format acceptable)
- Monthly team retrospectives
- Real-time project feedback through tools
- Async feedback channels (anonymous suggestion box)

### Performance Concerns Protocol:
- Early intervention for communication/collaboration issues
- Work arrangement adjustment as performance improvement tool
- Clear documentation of expectations and improvements needed
- Support resources (training, mentoring, equipment upgrades)

## 5. Timezone Management Strategy

### EU Team Integration (3 people):

**Daily Overlap Requirements:**
- **Core Overlap Hours:** 8 AM - 11 AM PT (4 PM - 7 PM EU)
- EU team members must be available during these hours
- US team accommodates one early meeting per week (7 AM PT start)

**Meeting Distribution:**
- 70% of EU-inclusive meetings in overlap hours
- 20% US-only meetings (with async follow-up for EU)
- 10% EU-convenient timing (US team accommodates)

**Communication Protocols:**
- **Urgent communications:** Slack with @here notifications during overlap hours
- **Non-urgent communications:** Email or async Slack messages
- **Project handoffs:** Detailed documentation required for timezone transitions
- **Weekend coverage:** Rotating schedule for critical support issues

### Cross-Coast US Coordination:

**Meeting Scheduling:**
- Default meeting time: 10 AM PT / 1 PM ET
- East Coast accommodates meetings until 6 PM ET
- West Coast accommodates meetings starting 7 AM PT
- No mandatory meetings during lunch hours (12-1 PM local time)

**Async Work Optimization:**
- **Follow-the-sun development:** Engineering tasks passed between coasts
- **Documentation standards:** All decisions recorded in searchable formats
- **Handoff protocols:** End-of-day summaries for next timezone pickup
- **Overlap maximization:** Core collaboration during 9 AM - 3 PM PT

### Timezone Equity Measures:

**Meeting Leadership Rotation:**
- Monthly rotation of meeting leadership to share timezone burden
- EU team members lead monthly all-hands (early US, comfortable EU timing)
- Documentation of all meetings for async consumption

**Communication Tool Optimization:**
- Loom for async video updates
- Slack threads for timezone-spanning discussions
- Shared calendars showing all timezone availability
- Automated timezone conversion in scheduling tools

## Implementation Timeline

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): Policy Communication
- All-hands presentation of policy
- Department-specific Q&A sessions
- Manager training on hybrid evaluation methods
- Equipment ordering and delivery

### Phase 2 (Weeks 3-4): Pilot Testing
- Two-week pilot with feedback collection
- Meeting room setup and technology testing
- Process refinement based on initial feedback
- EU team integration testing

### Phase 3 (Week 5+): Full Implementation
- Policy goes live with full enforcement
- Monthly policy effectiveness reviews
- Quarterly adjustments based on business needs
- Ongoing feedback collection and iteration

## Success Metrics

**Quantitative Measures:**
- Employee satisfaction scores (quarterly survey)
- Meeting effectiveness ratings
- Project delivery timelines
- Employee retention rates
- Client satisfaction scores (sales team)

**Qualitative Measures:**
- Cross-functional collaboration quality
- Innovation and creativity indicators
- Company culture strength
- Professional development progress
- Work-life balance indicators

This policy balances engineering autonomy with sales collaboration needs while maintaining operational excellence across timezones. The anchor day system ensures face-to-face collaboration when needed, while preserving flexibility for deep work and personal needs.
