# Blind Evaluation: task_03_pair_02

## Task
Write a remote work policy for a 50-person startup that just went from fully remote to hybrid. Engineering (20 people) is remote-first and resistant to change. Sales (15 people) wants everyone in-office for culture and collaboration. The CEO is undecided and wants a policy that both sides can live with. Cover: required in-office days, meeting norms, equipment policy, performance evaluation approach, and how to handle timezone spread (US East, US West, and 3 people in EU).

---

## Proposal X

# HYBRID WORK POLICY
**Effective Date: [Date]**  
**Version 1.0**

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid framework that respects departmental needs while maintaining company cohesion. Engineering retains remote-first flexibility, Sales achieves in-person collaboration goals, and clear guidelines ensure equitable treatment across all teams and timezones.

---

## 1. WORK LOCATION REQUIREMENTS

### 1.1 Department-Specific Requirements

**Engineering Team (Remote-First)**
- **Minimum office days:** 1 day per month for all-hands meetings
- **Recommended:** 1-2 days per quarter for sprint planning and team building
- **Flexibility:** Can work from anywhere within approved timezones
- **Core collaboration hours:** 11 AM - 2 PM PT (mandatory overlap)

**Sales Team (Office-Centric)**
- **Minimum office days:** 3 days per week (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday)
- **Flexibility:** Monday and Friday remote work permitted
- **Required in-person:** All customer meetings when clients visit office
- **Core hours:** 9 AM - 5 PM local time with 2-hour overlap with other timezones

**All Other Departments**
- **Minimum office days:** 2 days per week, including one "Core Day" (Wednesday)
- **Flexibility:** Choose second day based on team needs and project requirements
- **Core collaboration hours:** 11 AM - 2 PM PT

### 1.2 Core Company Days
- **All-Hands Wednesday:** All local employees in office (except Engineering, who join virtually)
- **Monthly Engineering Day:** First Tuesday of each month - Engineering team encouraged to come in
- **Quarterly Company Days:** Full company gathering (4 times per year)

---

## 2. MEETING NORMS & COLLABORATION

### 2.1 Meeting Guidelines

**Hybrid Meeting Standards:**
- All meetings must accommodate remote participants with quality A/V setup
- Default to video-on for engagement
- Use collaborative tools (Miro, Figma) for visual work sessions
- Record meetings for timezone-shifted colleagues (auto-delete after 30 days)

**Meeting Types:**
- **Daily standups:** Async via Slack for Engineering; in-person + video for Sales
- **Sprint planning:** Engineering remote; Sales in-person with remote dial-in option
- **All-hands:** Monthly, Wednesdays, hybrid format with recording
- **1:1s:** Manager's choice of format based on employee preference

### 2.2 Communication Protocols
- **Urgent (< 2 hours):** Slack with @channel or phone call
- **Standard (same day):** Slack DM or email
- **Non-urgent:** Email or async tools
- **Documentation:** All decisions recorded in Notion with timezone-appropriate notifications

---

## 3. EQUIPMENT & TECHNOLOGY POLICY

### 3.1 Standard Equipment Package (All Employees)
- Laptop (MacBook Pro or equivalent Windows machine)
- External monitor (27" minimum)
- Ergonomic keyboard and mouse
- Noise-canceling headphones with microphone
- Webcam (if laptop camera insufficient)
- Standing desk converter or ergonomic chair stipend ($500)

### 3.2 Home Office Stipend
- **One-time setup:** $1,200 for desk, chair, lighting, and accessories
- **Monthly internet:** $75 reimbursement for high-speed internet
- **Annual refresh:** $300 for equipment upgrades or replacements

### 3.3 Office Equipment
- Hot-desking stations with dual monitors and docking stations
- Conference rooms equipped with 360° cameras and wireless presentation tools
- Phone booths for private calls
- Collaboration spaces with large displays and whiteboarding capabilities

### 3.4 Equipment Management
- IT ships equipment directly to home addresses
- Annual equipment audit and refresh cycle
- Remote troubleshooting via IT helpdesk within 4-hour SLA
- Loaner equipment available for urgent replacements

---

## 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION APPROACH

### 4.1 Core Principles
- **Output-focused:** Evaluate results and deliverables, not hours or location
- **Objective metrics:** Clear, measurable KPIs for each role
- **Regular feedback:** Quarterly check-ins with continuous feedback culture
- **Equal treatment:** No location bias in reviews or promotion decisions

### 4.2 Performance Metrics by Department

**Engineering:**
- Sprint velocity and story point completion
- Code quality metrics (peer review scores, bug rates)
- Technical innovation and problem-solving contributions
- Cross-team collaboration effectiveness

**Sales:**
- Revenue targets and pipeline metrics
- Client relationship quality scores
- Team collaboration and knowledge sharing
- Customer satisfaction ratings

**All Departments:**
- Goal achievement (OKRs)
- Peer feedback scores (360 reviews)
- Professional development progress
- Company culture contribution

### 4.3 Review Process
- **Monthly:** Manager check-ins (15-30 minutes)
- **Quarterly:** Formal performance review with peer feedback
- **Annually:** Comprehensive review with promotion/compensation discussions
- **Continuous:** Real-time feedback via 15Five or similar tool

### 4.4 Remote Performance Support
- Weekly 1:1s for remote employees (optional for in-office)
- Mentorship program pairing remote and in-office employees
- Professional development budget: $2,000 annually for courses, conferences
- Career advancement explicitly includes remote leadership opportunities

---

## 5. TIMEZONE COORDINATION

### 5.1 Timezone Distribution
- **US East Coast (EST/EDT):** 25 employees
- **US West Coast (PST/PDT):** 22 employees  
- **Europe (CET/CEST):** 3 employees

### 5.2 Core Collaboration Windows
- **Primary overlap:** 11 AM - 2 PM PT / 2 PM - 5 PM ET (daily)
- **EU inclusion:** Tuesday/Thursday 8 AM - 10 AM PT / 5 PM - 7 PM CET
- **All-hands meetings:** Rotating times monthly to accommodate EU team

### 5.3 Timezone-Specific Guidelines

**European Employees:**
- Participate in 2 weekly meetings during US hours (compensation time adjustment)
- Lead European market initiatives and early customer outreach
- Async handoff protocols with US teams
- Annual budget for US office visits (2 trips per year)

**Cross-Timezone Collaboration:**
- 24-hour response SLA for non-urgent communications
- Shared calendars showing all timezone availability
- Async decision-making processes with clear deadlines
- Documentation-first culture for knowledge transfer

### 5.4 Meeting Scheduling Protocol
- Use Calendly or similar tool showing all timezone availability
- Default meeting times favor largest timezone group
- Record all cross-timezone meetings
- Rotate inconvenient meeting times quarterly

---

## 6. POLICY IMPLEMENTATION & GOVERNANCE

### 6.1 90-Day Transition Plan
- **Days 1-30:** Equipment distribution and home office setup
- **Days 31-60:** Team-specific training on hybrid collaboration tools
- **Days 61-90:** Full policy implementation with feedback collection

### 6.2 Success Metrics
- Employee satisfaction scores by department and work location
- Productivity metrics (department-specific KPIs)
- Collaboration effectiveness surveys
- Retention rates by work preference

### 6.3 Policy Review & Updates
- **Monthly:** Department head feedback sessions
- **Quarterly:** Employee survey and policy adjustment
- **Annually:** Comprehensive policy review with potential revisions
- **Ad-hoc:** Immediate review if success metrics decline >15%

### 6.4 Enforcement & Exceptions
- **Compliance tracking:** Automated badge swipe and calendar integration
- **Exception process:** Manager approval required, documented in HRIS
- **Performance impact:** Consistent policy violations addressed in reviews
- **Special circumstances:** Medical, family, visa issues handled case-by-case

---

## 7. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Can Engineering employees work internationally?**
A: Yes, within approved countries for tax/legal compliance. Current approval: US, Canada, UK, Germany.

**Q: What if Sales team members can't come in 3 days due to client travel?**
A: Client-facing travel counts as business requirement. Notify manager and adjust schedule accordingly.

**Q: How do promotions work for remote vs. in-office employees?**
A: Promotion criteria are identical regardless of location. Leadership tracks this metric to ensure equity.

**Q: What happens if office capacity is exceeded?**
A: Hot-desking system with advance booking. Overflow works from nearby co-working spaces (company-paid).
