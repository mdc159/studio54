# Blind Evaluation: task_03_pair_01

## Task
Write a remote work policy for a 50-person startup that just went from fully remote to hybrid. Engineering (20 people) is remote-first and resistant to change. Sales (15 people) wants everyone in-office for culture and collaboration. The CEO is undecided and wants a policy that both sides can live with. Cover: required in-office days, meeting norms, equipment policy, performance evaluation approach, and how to handle timezone spread (US East, US West, and 3 people in EU).

---

## Proposal X

# HYBRID WORK POLICY
## Balancing Flexibility with Collaboration

**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Policy Owner:** CEO  
**Review Cycle:** Quarterly for first year, then annually

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid framework that respects Engineering's remote-first preferences while addressing Sales' collaboration needs. The approach prioritizes outcomes over location while ensuring adequate face-time for culture building and strategic alignment.

---

## 1. REQUIRED IN-OFFICE PRESENCE

### Core Framework: "Anchor Days + Flex"

**Minimum Requirements:**
- **All employees:** 2 days per week in designated office (for US-based staff)
- **EU employees:** Exempt from daily requirements; quarterly in-person gatherings (see Section 1.3)

### 1.1 Department-Specific Requirements

**Engineering (20 people)**
- **Minimum:** 2 anchor days per month (not per week)
- **Recommended:** 1-2 days per week during sprint planning and retrospectives
- **Flexibility:** Can bank days (e.g., 4 days one week, 0 the next)
- **Override:** Project leads can request additional in-office time for critical launches (max 1 week per quarter)

**Sales (15 people)**  
- **Minimum:** 3 days per week in office
- **Required days:** Tuesday (team meetings), Thursday (client collaboration)
- **Flexible day:** One additional day of their choosing
- **Exception:** Travel weeks count as in-office time for calculation purposes

**All Other Departments (15 people)**
- **Standard:** 2 days per week
- **Recommended:** Wednesday (company all-hands), plus one department-specific day

### 1.2 Office Designation
- **Primary offices:** SF Bay Area, Austin, NYC
- **Employees must work from their designated office location when in-office**
- **Office changes require 30-day notice and manager approval**

### 1.3 EU Employee Integration
- **3 EU-based employees:** Participate in quarterly in-person gatherings
- **Quarterly budget:** $2,500 per EU employee for travel to US offices
- **Duration:** 1 week per quarter, timing aligned with strategic planning cycles
- **Alternative:** EU employees host US colleagues once per year

---

## 2. MEETING NORMS

### 2.1 Meeting Defaults
- **All meetings are hybrid-enabled by default**
- **No meetings scheduled outside 9 AM - 3 PM Pacific (12 PM - 6 PM Eastern)**
- **EU employees join 7 AM - 11 AM Pacific when critical**

### 2.2 Meeting Categories

**Level 1 - Remote Default**
- Daily standups, 1:1s, routine project updates
- All participants join via video, even if in same office

**Level 2 - Hybrid Optimized**
- Weekly team meetings, sprint planning, client calls
- In-office participants use conference rooms with quality A/V
- Remote participants have equal speaking time and visibility

**Level 3 - In-Person Preferred**  
- Quarterly strategy sessions, annual planning, team building
- All US-based staff expected in-office
- EU staff join virtually with dedicated facilitator for remote inclusion

### 2.3 Meeting Quality Standards
- **Maximum 6 people per meeting** (effectiveness rule)
- **All meetings require agenda 24 hours in advance**
- **Recording mandatory for Level 2 and 3 meetings** for async review
- **Action items documented in shared workspace within 2 hours**

---

## 3. EQUIPMENT POLICY

### 3.1 Standard Equipment Package (All Employees)
- MacBook Pro (M2 or equivalent)
- External monitor (27" minimum)
- Webcam (1080p minimum)
- Noise-canceling headphones
- Ergonomic desk chair
- Standing desk converter or adjustable desk

**Budget cap:** $3,500 per employee initial setup

### 3.2 Home Office Stipend
- **Monthly stipend:** $150 for internet, utilities, supplies
- **One-time setup:** $1,000 additional for desk, lighting, office setup
- **Refresh cycle:** Equipment replaced every 3 years or as needed

### 3.3 Office Equipment Standards
- **Hot-desking setup:** Docking stations, monitors, keyboards at every desk
- **Conference room standards:** 4K cameras, ceiling microphones, 75" displays
- **Booking system:** Desks and rooms reservable via Slack integration

### 3.4 EU Employee Considerations
- **Equipment shipping:** Company covers international shipping costs
- **Local procurement:** EU employees can purchase locally and expense (pre-approval required)
- **Currency adjustment:** Equipment budgets adjusted for local pricing variations

---

## 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION APPROACH

### 4.1 Core Principle: Outcome-Based Assessment
Performance measured by **results delivered**, not location or hours worked.

### 4.2 Evaluation Framework

**Quarterly OKR Reviews (70% of evaluation)**
- Individual objectives aligned with company goals
- Measurable outcomes with clear success criteria
- Progress tracked in shared dashboard (Lattice or similar)

**Collaboration & Culture Fit (20% of evaluation)**
- Peer feedback on communication effectiveness
- Participation in team activities (both virtual and in-person)
- Mentoring and knowledge sharing contributions

**Professional Development (10% of evaluation)**
- Skill advancement and learning goals
- Conference attendance, training completion
- Internal presentation or workshop delivery

### 4.3 Location-Blind Calibration
- **Cross-location peer reviews:** Remote and in-office employees review each other
- **Standardized rubrics:** Same criteria applied regardless of work location
- **Bias training:** All managers complete remote work bias training quarterly
- **Data tracking:** Monitor promotion and raise rates by work location to identify disparities

### 4.4 Special Considerations

**Engineering Performance Metrics**
- Code quality and review participation
- Sprint commitment reliability
- Technical mentorship and documentation
- Innovation and process improvement contributions

**Sales Performance Metrics**  
- Pipeline generation and conversion rates
- Client relationship strength (measured via surveys)
- Team collaboration on complex deals
- Market intelligence and competitive insights

---

## 5. TIMEZONE COORDINATION

### 5.1 Core Hours Framework
- **US Operations:** 9 AM - 6 PM Pacific (12 PM - 9 PM Eastern)
- **EU Integration:** 7 AM - 11 AM Pacific overlap required for EU staff
- **Response expectations:** 4-hour response time during core hours, 24-hour otherwise

### 5.2 Timezone-Specific Protocols

**Daily Operations**
- **Standups:** Recorded asynchronously, with live sync 3x per week during overlap hours
- **Urgent issues:** Slack escalation protocol with phone backup
- **Documentation:** All decisions documented in Notion within 4 hours

**Cross-Timezone Collaboration**
- **EU-US meetings:** Rotate meeting times monthly (early Pacific, late EU)
- **Follow-the-sun development:** Engineering tasks handed off between timezones
- **Weekend coverage:** Voluntary rotation for critical system monitoring

### 5.3 Communication Tools & Protocols

**Primary Channels**
- **Slack:** Real-time communication with clear response expectations
- **Loom:** Async video updates for complex topics
- **Notion:** Single source of truth for decisions and documentation
- **Calendar blocking:** Protected focus time clearly marked across timezones

**Communication Standards**
- **Status indicators:** Accurate Slack status with timezone and availability
- **Message threading:** Use threads to prevent channel noise
- **Urgent escalation:** Phone/text protocol for true emergencies only

---

## 6. IMPLEMENTATION & GOVERNANCE

### 6.1 Rollout Timeline
- **Month 1:** Policy communication and manager training
- **Month 2:** Equipment procurement and office setup
- **Month 3:** Full policy implementation with daily monitoring
- **Month 4:** First policy review and adjustment cycle

### 6.2 Monitoring & Metrics
**Monthly tracking:**
- Office utilization rates by department
- Meeting effectiveness scores (post-meeting surveys)
- Employee satisfaction with hybrid setup (pulse surveys)
- Performance metric consistency across locations

**Quarterly reviews:**
- Policy effectiveness assessment
- Department-specific adjustment requests
- Budget impact analysis
- Competitive benchmarking

### 6.3 Escalation & Exceptions
- **Department leads** can request temporary policy modifications for specific projects
- **Individual exceptions** require CEO approval and 90-day review
- **Emergency protocols** allow full remote during travel restrictions or health concerns

### 6.4 Success Criteria (6-Month Evaluation)
- Employee satisfaction score: >4.0/5.0 on hybrid work experience
- Productivity metrics: Maintain or improve current performance levels
- Retention rate: <5% voluntary turnover attributed to work arrangement
- Culture metrics: >85% feel connected to team and company mission
