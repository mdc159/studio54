# HYBRID WORK POLICY
**Effective Date: [Date]**  
**Version 2.0**

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid framework that respects departmental needs while maintaining operational efficiency and cost control. Engineering retains remote-first flexibility, Sales achieves in-person collaboration goals, and clear guidelines ensure equitable treatment and legal compliance across all teams.

---

## 1. WORK LOCATION REQUIREMENTS

### 1.1 Department-Specific Requirements

**Engineering Team (Remote-First)**
- **Minimum office days:** 1 day per month for all-hands meetings
- **Recommended:** 1-2 days per quarter for sprint planning and team building
- **Geographic restriction:** Must work from approved US states only
- **Core collaboration hours:** 11 AM - 2 PM PT (mandatory overlap)

**Sales Team (Office-Centric)**
- **Minimum office days:** 3 days per week (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday)
- **Flexibility:** Monday and Friday remote work permitted
- **Required in-person:** All customer meetings when clients visit office
- **Core hours:** 9 AM - 5 PM local time

**All Other Departments**
- **Minimum office days:** 2 days per week, including one "Core Day" (Wednesday)
- **Flexibility:** Choose second day based on team needs and project requirements
- **Core collaboration hours:** 11 AM - 2 PM PT

### 1.2 Core Company Days
- **All-Hands Wednesday:** All local employees in office (except Engineering, who join virtually)
- **Monthly Engineering Day:** First Tuesday of each month - Engineering team encouraged to come in
- **Quarterly Company Days:** Full company gathering (4 times per year)

*JUSTIFICATION: Keeps Version A's department-specific approach that recognizes legitimate business differences, but adopts Version B's US-only geographic restriction to eliminate complex international compliance issues that were disproportionate for 3 EU employees.*

---

## 2. MEETING NORMS & COLLABORATION

### 2.1 Meeting Guidelines

**Hybrid Meeting Standards:**
- All meetings must accommodate remote participants with quality A/V setup
- Default to video-on for engagement
- Use collaborative tools (Miro, Figma) for visual work sessions
- Meeting notes and decisions recorded in shared workspace

**Meeting Types:**
- **Daily standups:** Async via Slack for Engineering; in-person + video for Sales
- **Sprint planning:** Engineering remote; Sales in-person with remote dial-in option
- **All-hands:** Monthly, Wednesdays, hybrid format
- **1:1s:** Manager's choice of format based on employee preference

### 2.2 Communication Protocols
- **Urgent (< 2 hours):** Slack with @channel or phone call
- **Standard (same day):** Slack DM or email
- **Non-urgent:** Email or async tools
- **Documentation:** All decisions recorded in Notion

*JUSTIFICATION: Removes Version A's expensive recording requirements and auto-deletion complexity while maintaining essential collaboration tools and clear communication hierarchy.*

## 3. EQUIPMENT & TECHNOLOGY POLICY

### 3.1 Standard Equipment Package

**All Employees Receive:**
- Laptop (MacBook Pro or equivalent Windows machine)
- External monitor (single 24" display)
- Ergonomic keyboard and mouse
- Noise-canceling headphones with microphone
- $500 annual home office stipend

**Office-Centric Roles Additionally Receive:**
- Assigned desk with dual monitors when in office 3+ days/week

### 3.2 Office Equipment
- Hot-desking stations with monitors and docking stations for flexible employees
- Conference rooms equipped with quality A/V and wireless presentation tools
- Phone booths for private calls
- Collaboration spaces with large displays and whiteboarding capabilities

### 3.3 Equipment Management
- IT ships equipment directly to primary work address
- IT support via ticket system with 24-hour response SLA
- Annual equipment refresh based on depreciation schedule
- Loaner equipment available for urgent replacements

*JUSTIFICATION: Adopts Version B's realistic equipment costs ($500 vs $2,700 annually) and achievable SLA (24-hour vs impossible 4-hour remote support) while maintaining Version A's thoughtful office setup for different work patterns.*

## 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION APPROACH

### 4.1 Core Principles
- **Output-focused:** Evaluate results and deliverables, not hours or location
- **Objective metrics:** Clear, measurable KPIs for each role
- **Regular feedback:** Quarterly check-ins with continuous feedback culture
- **Equal treatment:** No location bias in reviews or promotion decisions

### 4.2 Performance Metrics by Department

**Engineering:**
- Sprint velocity and story point completion
- Code quality metrics (peer review scores, bug rates)
- Technical innovation and problem-solving contributions
- Cross-team collaboration effectiveness

**Sales:**
- Revenue targets and pipeline metrics
- Client relationship quality scores
- Team collaboration and knowledge sharing
- Customer satisfaction ratings

**All Departments:**
- Goal achievement (OKRs)
- Peer feedback scores (360 reviews)
- Professional development progress
- Company culture contribution

### 4.3 Review Process
- **Monthly:** Manager check-ins (15-30 minutes)
- **Quarterly:** Formal performance review with peer feedback
- **Annually:** Comprehensive review with promotion/compensation discussions

*JUSTIFICATION: Keeps Version A's comprehensive performance framework that addresses location bias concerns, but removes contradictory continuous tracking tools that create surveillance concerns.*

## 5. OPERATIONAL COMPLIANCE

### 5.1 Attendance and Compliance
- **Office days:** Badge entry tracked for compliance reporting only
- **Core hours:** Calendar availability during required collaboration time
- **Exception process:** Manager approval for schedule deviations, documented monthly

### 5.2 Geographic and Legal Requirements
- **Approved locations:** US states where company maintains employment compliance
- **Travel flexibility:** Occasional work travel (under 30 days annually) permitted
- **Home office requirements:** Employees certify safe workspace setup annually

*JUSTIFICATION: Adopts Version B's simplified compliance approach that eliminates complex international issues while maintaining necessary tracking for operational planning.*

## 6. COST MANAGEMENT AND SPACE PLANNING

### 6.1 Office Space Optimization
- **Desk ratio:** 1.0 desks per office-centric employee + 0.3 per flexible employee
- **Hot-desking:** Booking system with 48-hour advance notice
- **Capacity management:** Quarterly space utilization review

### 6.2 Budget Framework
- **Remote work cost:** $500 annual stipend + equipment depreciation per employee
- **Space savings:** Reduced square footage based on actual occupancy patterns
- **ROI tracking:** Quarterly cost-benefit analysis of hybrid model

*JUSTIFICATION: Incorporates Version B's essential cost management and realistic space planning that was completely absent from Version A, necessary for sustainable policy implementation.*

## 8. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Can Engineering employees work from any US state?**
A: Yes, within US states where we maintain employment compliance. HR maintains current approved state list.

**Q: What if Sales team members can't come in 3 days due to client travel?**
A: Client-facing travel counts as business requirement. Notify manager and adjust schedule accordingly.

**Q: How do promotions work for remote vs. in-office employees?**
A: Promotion criteria are identical regardless of location. Leadership tracks this metric to ensure equity.

**Q: What happens if office capacity is exceeded on hot-desking days?**
A: Book desks 48 hours in advance. Overflow works from home or nearby company-paid co-working spaces.
