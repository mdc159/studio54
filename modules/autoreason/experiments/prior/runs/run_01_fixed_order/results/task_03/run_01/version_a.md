# HYBRID WORK POLICY
## Effective Date: [Insert Date]

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid framework that respects departmental needs while maintaining company cohesion. Engineering remains largely remote with strategic in-person touchpoints, while Sales maintains stronger office presence for collaboration. All decisions prioritize business outcomes over rigid adherence to location preferences.

---

## 1. REQUIRED IN-OFFICE PRESENCE

### 1.1 Department-Specific Requirements

**Engineering Team (20 people)**
- **Minimum requirement**: 1 day per month in-office
- **Core collaboration days**: Second Tuesday of each month (all-hands engineering day)
- **Optional presence**: Any additional days based on project needs
- **Sprint planning**: In-person preferred for major releases (quarterly)

**Sales Team (15 people)**
- **Minimum requirement**: 3 days per week in-office
- **Core days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (non-negotiable)
- **Flexible days**: Monday and Friday (remote option available)
- **Client meetings**: In-person when client is local, otherwise team's discretion

**Leadership/Other Functions (15 people)**
- **Minimum requirement**: 2 days per week in-office
- **Core day**: Wednesday (leadership alignment day)
- **Flexible day**: One additional day based on team needs and meetings

### 1.2 Exceptions and Accommodations
- New hires: First 30 days require 50% in-office presence regardless of department
- Performance improvement plans: May require increased office presence
- Medical/family accommodations: Case-by-case review with HR
- EU employees: Exempt from US office requirements (separate coworking stipend provided)

---

## 2. MEETING NORMS AND COLLABORATION STANDARDS

### 2.1 Meeting Types and Location Requirements

**All-Hands Meetings**
- Monthly: Virtual-first with office broadcast locations
- Quarterly: Hybrid format with 50% in-person attendance target

**Department Meetings**
- Engineering: Virtual-first, recorded for async review
- Sales: In-person on core office days, virtual backup always available
- Leadership: Hybrid with video conference for remote participants

**Cross-Functional Meetings**
- Default to virtual to accommodate all participants
- Strategic planning sessions: In-person preferred (quarterly)
- Client-facing meetings: Follow client preference

### 2.2 Meeting Technology Standards
- All meeting rooms equipped with 360-degree cameras and omnidirectional microphones
- Mandatory camera-on policy for virtual participants
- Meeting recordings required for sessions with >5 participants
- 24-hour advance notice required for in-person meeting requirements

### 2.3 Communication Protocols
- **Synchronous communication**: Slack for urgent matters (4-hour response expectation)
- **Asynchronous communication**: Email for non-urgent items (24-hour response)
- **Documentation**: All decisions must be documented in shared systems
- **Timezone consideration**: No required meetings between 6 PM PT and 6 AM ET

---

## 3. EQUIPMENT AND WORKSPACE POLICY

### 3.1 Remote Work Equipment Allocation

**Standard Remote Setup (All Employees)**
- Laptop: Company-provided (MacBook Pro or equivalent)
- Monitor: Up to $800 allowance for external display
- Ergonomic equipment: $500 annual allowance (desk, chair, accessories)
- Internet stipend: $75/month for high-speed internet

**Department-Specific Additions**
- **Engineering**: Additional $1,000 for development tools, second monitor, or specialized hardware
- **Sales**: $300 for professional lighting/camera setup for client calls
- **EU Employees**: €200/month coworking space stipend in lieu of office access

### 3.2 Office Equipment and Reservations
- Hot-desking system with 24-hour advance booking
- Dedicated desks available for employees exceeding 3 days/week office presence
- Conference room booking system with hybrid capabilities
- Phone booths available for private calls (first-come, first-served)

### 3.3 Security and IT Support
- VPN required for all remote connections
- Monthly security training (virtual format)
- IT support available via Slack during business hours (6 AM - 6 PM PT)
- Equipment replacement: 2-day shipping for critical failures

## 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION FRAMEWORK

### 4.1 Core Principles
- **Outcome-based assessment**: Results matter more than location
- **Communication quality**: Proactive updates and stakeholder management
- **Collaboration effectiveness**: Contribution to cross-functional goals
- **Professional development**: Continuous learning and skill advancement

### 4.2 Department-Specific Metrics

**Engineering Performance Indicators**
- Code quality and delivery timelines (40%)
- Technical leadership and mentoring (25%)
- Cross-functional collaboration (20%)
- Documentation and knowledge sharing (15%)

**Sales Performance Indicators**
- Revenue targets and pipeline development (50%)
- Client relationship management (25%)
- Team collaboration and culture building (15%)
- Market intelligence and feedback (10%)

**Universal Performance Indicators**
- Goal achievement (quantifiable OKRs)
- Stakeholder feedback (360-degree reviews)
- Communication effectiveness rating
- Cultural contribution score

### 4.3 Review Process
- **Quarterly check-ins**: Virtual format with structured templates
- **Annual reviews**: In-person preferred, virtual acceptable
- **Peer feedback**: Anonymous system accommodating timezone differences
- **Manager training**: Quarterly sessions on remote performance management

---

## 5. TIMEZONE COORDINATION STRATEGY

### 5.1 Core Business Hours Definition
- **Primary overlap window**: 9 AM - 12 PM PT (12 PM - 3 PM ET, 5 PM - 8 PM GMT)
- **Secondary overlap window**: 6 AM - 9 AM PT (accommodates EU start-of-day)
- **Protected focus time**: No meetings scheduled outside overlap windows without explicit consent

### 5.2 EU Employee Integration
- **Dedicated EU liaison**: Rotating quarterly among US-based leadership
- **EU-friendly meetings**: Alternate monthly between early PT and late GMT times
- **Documentation priority**: All EU contributions must be documented for async consumption
- **Career development**: Equal access to growth opportunities regardless of timezone

### 5.3 Meeting Scheduling Protocols
- **Calendar transparency**: All employees maintain visible calendars with timezone indicators
- **Rotation policy**: Inconvenient meeting times rotate quarterly among participants
- **Recording mandate**: All cross-timezone meetings must be recorded
- **Follow-up requirements**: Written summaries within 24 hours of any cross-timezone meeting

### 5.4 Emergency Communication
- **Escalation matrix**: Clear protocols for urgent issues requiring immediate attention
- **On-call rotation**: Shared responsibility across timezones for critical systems
- **Response time expectations**: 
  - Critical issues: 2 hours regardless of timezone
  - High priority: 4 hours during business hours, 8 hours outside
  - Normal priority: 24 hours

## 6. IMPLEMENTATION AND COMPLIANCE

### 6.1 90-Day Trial Period
- Policy effective for 90 days with mandatory review
- Weekly pulse surveys to gauge satisfaction and effectiveness
- Monthly adjustment meetings with department heads
- Data collection on productivity, satisfaction, and collaboration metrics

### 6.2 Success Metrics
- **Retention rate**: <5% voluntary turnover during implementation
- **Productivity indicators**: Department-specific KPIs maintained or improved
- **Collaboration score**: Internal survey rating >4.0/5.0
- **Cost efficiency**: Office space utilization >70% on required days

### 6.3 Enforcement and Flexibility
- **Attendance tracking**: Badge data and calendar integration for office requirements
- **Variance requests**: Monthly allowance for schedule adjustments with 1-week notice
- **Performance correlation**: Office attendance not used as performance indicator
- **Appeal process**: HR review for policy conflicts or hardships

### 6.4 Policy Review and Evolution
- **Quarterly assessment**: Data-driven review with all stakeholders
- **Annual revision**: Formal policy update based on business needs and feedback
- **Industry benchmarking**: Comparison with similar-stage companies
- **Exit interview integration**: Departing employee feedback on policy effectiveness
