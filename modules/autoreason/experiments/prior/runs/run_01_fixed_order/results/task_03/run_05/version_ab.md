# HYBRID WORK POLICY
## Practical Framework for Distributed Collaboration

**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Policy Owner:** CEO  
**Review Cycle:** Monthly for first 3 months, then quarterly

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid framework that accommodates Engineering's remote-first preferences while addressing Sales' collaboration needs. The approach prioritizes outcomes over location while ensuring essential face-time through structured team days and minimal viable presence requirements.

---

## 1. OFFICE PRESENCE REQUIREMENTS

### Core Framework: "Team Days + Minimal Flex"

**Minimum Requirements:**
- **All US employees:** 1 designated team day per week + 1 flexible day per month
- **EU employees:** Monthly video team meetings (no travel requirements)

### 1.1 Department-Specific Requirements

**Engineering (20 people)**
- **Team day:** Wednesday (sprint planning, code reviews, technical discussions)
- **Flexible requirement:** 1 additional office day per month (any day, any week)
- **Tracking:** Simple monthly confirmation with engineering manager
- **Override:** Project leads can request additional team days for critical launches (max 2 additional weeks per quarter)

*Justification: Version B's simplicity prevents enforcement issues while keeping Version A's project override for legitimate business needs*

**Sales (15 people)**  
- **Team day:** Tuesday (pipeline reviews, deal strategy, client collaboration)
- **Flexible requirement:** 1 additional office day per month
- **Travel exception:** No office requirements during client travel weeks (3+ consecutive days away from home base)
- **Tracking:** Manager confirms travel schedule monthly

*Justification: Version B's travel definition fixes Version A's conflicting requirements while maintaining Sales' higher collaboration needs*

**All Other Departments (15 people)**
- **Team day:** Thursday (department meetings, cross-functional work) 
- **Flexible requirement:** 1 additional office day per month

### 1.2 Office Designation
- **Primary office:** SF Bay Area location
- **Remote employees:** No office requirements, participate in team days via video

*Justification: Version B's single office eliminates Version A's unworkable multi-office coordination*

### 1.3 EU Employee Integration
- **Monthly team video calls:** 1-hour department meetings during 10 AM - 2 PM Pacific overlap
- **Optional quarterly visits:** EU employees can expense one US visit per quarter (up to $2,000 per person)
- **No mandatory travel requirements**

*Justification: Version B's optional approach eliminates Version A's $30K mandatory travel budget while preserving opportunity for in-person connection*

## 2. MEETING APPROACH

### 2.1 Meeting Defaults
- **Team day meetings:** In-person for office attendees, video for remote participants
- **All other meetings:** Video-first (all participants join via video regardless of location)
- **Core collaboration hours:** 10 AM - 2 PM Pacific for cross-timezone meetings

*Justification: Version B's video-first default eliminates Version A's expensive hybrid A/V requirements while maintaining inclusive participation*

### 2.2 Meeting Categories

**Weekly Team Meetings (Team Days)**
- Department-specific agenda focusing on collaboration-intensive work
- In-person participants use existing conference room with external webcam
- Remote participants have equal participation via shared screen and video

**All Other Meetings**
- Default to video calls using individual laptops
- EU employees join during 10 AM - 2 PM Pacific window when critical
- No special conference room technology required

*Justification: Version B's approach uses existing infrastructure while Version A's Level 1-3 categories create unnecessary complexity*

### 2.3 Meeting Quality Standards
- **Maximum 6 people per meeting** (effectiveness rule)
- **Meeting agendas:** Shared 2 hours in advance via Slack
- **Action items:** Posted in relevant Slack channel within 24 hours
- **Important decisions:** Documented in existing project management tools

*Justification: Keeps Version A's meeting effectiveness standards while using Version B's existing tool integration*

## 3. EQUIPMENT APPROACH

### 3.1 Essential Equipment Package
**Remote employees only:**
- MacBook Pro (M2 or equivalent)
- External monitor (27" minimum) 
- Webcam (1080p minimum)
- Noise-canceling headphones

**Budget cap:** $2,000 per remote employee (one-time)

*Justification: Version A's comprehensive equipment list reduced to Version B's essential items, balancing functionality with cost control*

### 3.2 Home Office Support
- **Internet reimbursement:** Actual costs up to $100/month (with receipts)
- **One-time setup stipend:** $500 for desk and ergonomic setup

*Justification: Version A's ongoing stipends reduced to Version B's receipt-based approach plus minimal setup support*

### 3.3 Office Equipment Standards
- **Assigned seating:** Each employee has designated desk
- **Conference room upgrade:** One $200 external webcam per conference room
- **Existing infrastructure:** Utilize current desks and meeting spaces

*Justification: Version B's assigned seating eliminates Version A's expensive hot-desking while maintaining basic hybrid meeting capability*

## 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION APPROACH

### 4.1 Core Principle: Outcome-Based Assessment

**Collaboration Assessment (Secondary Component)**

*Justification: Version A's detailed framework simplified to Version B's existing process integration while maintaining outcome focus*

- **Simple monitoring:** Monthly check that minimum office requirements are met

*Justification: Version A's comprehensive bias training reduced to Version B's practical manager education while preserving fairness principle*
