# HYBRID WORK POLICY (REVISED)
## Effective Date: [Insert Date]

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a unified hybrid framework with consistent expectations across all departments. All employees work 2-3 days per week in-office with department-specific core days to ensure cross-functional collaboration. The policy prioritizes operational simplicity, cost control, and equitable treatment while maintaining business effectiveness.

---

## 1. REQUIRED IN-OFFICE PRESENCE

### 1.1 Universal Requirements (All Departments)

**Standard Schedule for All Employees**
- **Minimum requirement**: 2 days per week in-office
- **Core collaboration days**: Tuesday and Wednesday (mandatory for all employees)
- **Flexible third day**: Monday, Thursday, or Friday (employee choice, minimum 1 day per month)
- **Maximum office days**: 3 days per week (cost control measure)

*This change fixes the asymmetric policy that created internal equity issues by establishing uniform requirements for all employees.*

**Department-Specific Considerations Within Standard Framework**
- **Engineering**: Technical deep-work sessions scheduled for remote days
- **Sales**: Client calls scheduled around core office days when possible
- **All Functions**: Wednesday designated as cross-functional collaboration day

*This change fixes the fragmented office population by ensuring all departments are present on the same core days.*

### 1.2 Exceptions and Accommodations
- **New hires**: Follow standard 2-day requirement from day one (no special requirements)
- **Performance issues**: Addressed through existing performance management process
- **Medical/family accommodations**: Standard ADA/FMLA processes apply
- **EU employees**: Follow same 2-day requirement using designated coworking spaces

*This change fixes the inconsistent onboarding by applying uniform rules to all employees including new hires.*

---

## 2. OFFICE LOGISTICS AND COLLABORATION

### 2.1 Workspace Allocation

**Assigned Desk System**
- All employees receive assigned desks (no hot-desking)
- Desk assignments accommodate 60% office capacity on peak days (Tuesday/Wednesday)
- Shared team areas for collaboration on core days

*This change fixes the unworkable hot-desking system by providing assigned seating that matches actual capacity needs.*

### 2.2 Meeting Standards

**Simple Meeting Technology**
- Conference rooms equipped with standard video conferencing (existing equipment)
- One designated room per floor for hybrid meetings with quality audio/video
- All other meetings default to fully remote or fully in-person

*This change fixes the unrealistic technology assumptions by using standard, existing equipment rather than specialized 360-degree cameras.*

**Meeting Guidelines**
- Tuesday/Wednesday: In-person meetings preferred when all attendees are in office
- Remote days: All meetings virtual
- Cross-timezone meetings: Scheduled during 9 AM - 12 PM PT overlap window
- No mandatory recording requirements

*This change fixes the operational complexity of recording requirements and mandatory camera policies.*

### 2.3 EU Employee Integration
- **Coworking requirement**: 2 days per week at company-approved coworking spaces
- **Core overlap**: 9 AM - 12 PM PT daily for EU collaboration
- **Monthly sync**: One EU employee travels to US office quarterly (rotating, company-paid)

*This change fixes the superficial EU integration by creating real connection points rather than just a stipend.*

## 3. EQUIPMENT AND EXPENSES

### 3.1 Simplified Equipment Policy

**Standard Remote Setup (All Employees)**
- Company laptop and basic peripherals (mouse, keyboard)
- $500 one-time home office setup allowance
- $50/month internet stipend

*This change fixes the administrative nightmare of multiple stipend amounts by providing simple, uniform allowances.*

**EU Employees Additional**
- €150/month coworking space allowance (company contracts with 2-3 approved locations)

*This change fixes the EU stipend complexity by pre-negotiating corporate rates rather than individual reimbursements.*

### 3.2 Office Resources
- Assigned desks with standard monitor and docking station
- Shared printers and office supplies
- Kitchen facilities and basic amenities

## 4. PERFORMANCE AND ACCOUNTABILITY

### 4.1 Simplified Performance Framework

**Universal Performance Standards**
- Goal achievement (60% of evaluation)
- Communication and collaboration effectiveness (40% of evaluation)
- Same performance review process and timing for all employees

*This change fixes the overly elaborate performance framework by using simple, universal metrics.*

### 4.2 Office Attendance Tracking
- Badge-in data tracked for compliance monitoring
- Monthly reporting to managers for pattern identification
- Non-compliance addressed through standard performance management

*This change fixes the enforcement gap by creating clear tracking and consequences.*
