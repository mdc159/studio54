## REAL PROBLEMS WITH THIS HYBRID WORK POLICY

### **FUNDAMENTAL DESIGN FLAWS**

**Arbitrary Department Requirements Don't Match Actual Work Needs**
- Engineering "1 day per month" is performative - provides zero business value but creates administrative overhead
- Sales "Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday" ignores that client calls often happen Monday/Friday
- No justification for why these specific patterns were chosen over alternatives

**The "Core Office Hours" Concept Is Broken**
- 12-3 PM EST overlap requirement conflicts with Engineering's remote-first preference
- West Coast team forced into 9 AM meetings contradicts their stated flexibility
- EU team gets 6-8 PM local time for "core hours" - unsustainable

**Meeting Equity Standards Are Technically Impossible**
- "No sidebar conversations" cannot be enforced in practice
- 360-degree cameras create privacy/security issues in confidential discussions
- "Remote-first facilitation" requires skills most managers don't have and won't develop

### **OPERATIONAL COMPLEXITY THAT CREATES MORE PROBLEMS**

**Equipment Policy Is a Logistical Nightmare**
- $500 home office allowance with no clear approval criteria or audit process
- Hot-desking reservation system requires dedicated staff and software licensing
- 3-year refresh cycles ignore actual usage patterns (remote workers need different equipment lifespans)

**Performance Evaluation Framework Is Unworkable**
- "Outcome-based assessment" sounds good but provides no actual metrics for most roles
- "360-degree feedback including remote collaboration skills" - no one knows how to measure this
- Quarterly calibration sessions will consume massive management time with unclear ROI

**Timezone Coordination Is Overly Complex**
- 50/50 meeting split requires constant scheduling mathematics
- "Rotating meeting times" creates calendar chaos and reduces attendance
- EU quarterly visits assume $15K+ annual travel budget per EU employee

### **WRONG ASSUMPTIONS ABOUT HUMAN BEHAVIOR**

**People Won't Follow Location-Agnostic Rules**
- Managers will still favor people they see in person despite training
- Sales team will pressure for more office time during busy periods regardless of policy
- Engineering will game the "1 day per month" requirement with minimal compliance

**Technology Adoption Assumptions Are Flawed**
- Assumes all employees want/can use the prescribed collaboration tools effectively
- Ignores that many people prefer phone calls over video for sensitive discussions
- 24-hour documentation requirement assumes people work at the same pace

**Cost Assumptions Don't Add Up**
- Monthly $75 internet reimbursement for all remote workers = $45K annually just for internet
- Office space still needed for peak capacity but underutilized most days
- Equipment duplication (home + office setups) significantly increases IT costs

### **MISSING CRITICAL IMPLEMENTATION PIECES**

**No Conflict Resolution Mechanisms**
- What happens when Engineering refuses Tuesday meetings but Sales needs them there?
- How do you handle timezone conflicts when EU team can't attend required meetings?
- No process for handling manager resistance to remote-first practices

**Legal and Compliance Gaps**
- No consideration of state/country employment law differences for remote workers
- Missing data security requirements for home offices
- No worker's compensation coverage clarification for home office injuries

**Change Management Is Absent**
- 90-day implementation assumes instant adoption of complex new behaviors
- No transition plan for existing informal arrangements
- Missing stakeholder buy-in process for department heads who may resist

### **ENFORCEMENT AND ACCOUNTABILITY PROBLEMS**

**Unenforceable Requirements**
- "Remote-first facilitation" has no measurable standards
- "Async updates by 10 AM local time" with no consequences for non-compliance
- "Equal treatment" in performance reviews cannot be objectively verified

**Measurement Gaps**
- "Collaboration effectiveness ratings" - no baseline or comparison methodology
- Success metrics are feel-good surveys rather than business outcomes
- No way to distinguish policy problems from execution problems

**Resource Requirements Underestimated**
- Policy requires dedicated office management platform and staff
- Manager training program needs curriculum development and ongoing delivery
- IT support expansion needed but not budgeted or staffed

The policy tries to solve too many problems with one complex framework instead of addressing the core tension: Engineering wants flexibility, Sales wants collaboration, and the company needs both to work together effectively.
