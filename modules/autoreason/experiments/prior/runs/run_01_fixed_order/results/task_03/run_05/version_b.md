# REVISED HYBRID WORK POLICY
## Practical Framework for Distributed Collaboration

**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Policy Owner:** CEO  
**Review Cycle:** Monthly for first 3 months, then quarterly

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a simplified hybrid framework that accommodates different department needs while maintaining operational feasibility. The approach focuses on essential collaboration moments rather than arbitrary presence requirements.

---

## 1. OFFICE PRESENCE REQUIREMENTS

### Core Framework: "Team Days + Individual Choice"

**Minimum Requirements:**
- **All US employees:** 1 designated team day per week + 1 flexible day per month
- **EU employees:** Monthly video team meetings (no travel requirements)

### 1.1 Department-Specific Requirements

**Engineering (20 people)**
- **Team day:** Wednesday (sprint planning, code reviews, technical discussions)
- **Flexible requirement:** 1 additional office day per month (any day, any week)
- **Tracking:** Simple monthly check-in with engineering manager
- **Override:** No additional requirements for project launches

*Fixes Problem 1: Eliminates unenforceable monthly minimums and day-banking complexity*

**Sales (15 people)**  
- **Team day:** Tuesday (pipeline reviews, deal strategy)
- **Flexible requirement:** 1 additional office day per month
- **Travel exception:** No office requirements during client travel weeks (defined as 3+ consecutive days away from home base)
- **Tracking:** Manager confirms travel schedule monthly

*Fixes Problem 1: Eliminates conflicting travel/office requirements and defines "travel week"*

**All Other Departments (15 people)**
- **Team day:** Thursday (department meetings, cross-functional work)
- **Flexible requirement:** 1 additional office day per month

### 1.2 Office Designation
- **Single office:** SF Bay Area location only
- **Remote employees:** No office requirements, participate in team days via video

*Fixes Problem 1: Eliminates multi-office complexity*

---

## 2. MEETING APPROACH

### 2.1 Meeting Defaults
- **Team day meetings:** In-person for office attendees, video for remote participants
- **All other meetings:** Video-first (all participants join via video regardless of location)
- **Core collaboration hours:** 10 AM - 2 PM Pacific for cross-timezone meetings

*Fixes Problem 2: Eliminates expensive hybrid A/V requirements and unrealistic timezone constraints*

### 2.2 Meeting Categories

**Weekly Team Meetings (Team Days)**
- Department-specific agenda
- In-person participants use existing conference room with laptop + external webcam
- Remote participants have equal participation via shared screen and video

**All Other Meetings**
- Default to video calls using individual laptops
- No special conference room requirements
- EU employees join during 10 AM - 2 PM Pacific window when possible

### 2.3 Basic Meeting Standards
- **Meeting agendas:** Shared 2 hours in advance via Slack
- **Action items:** Posted in relevant Slack channel within 24 hours
- **Important decisions:** Documented in existing project management tools

*Fixes Problem 2: Uses existing tools rather than requiring new systems*

---

## 3. EQUIPMENT APPROACH

### 3.1 Immediate Equipment (Existing Budget)
- **Remote employees only:** Laptop, external monitor, basic webcam, headphones
- **Budget:** $1,500 per remote employee (one-time)
- **Office employees:** Use existing office equipment + personal laptop

*Fixes Problem 3: Reduces cost from $175K to $45K maximum*

### 3.2 Home Office Support
- **Reimbursement:** Actual internet costs up to $100/month (with receipts)
- **No additional stipends or setup costs**

*Fixes Problem 3: Eliminates $90K annual stipend cost*

### 3.3 Office Equipment
- **Current state:** Use existing desks and conference rooms
- **Assigned seating:** Each person has designated desk (no hot-desking)
- **Basic upgrade:** One $200 external webcam per conference room

*Fixes Problem 3: Eliminates expensive hot-desking infrastructure*

---

## 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION

### 4.1 Core Principle: Maintain Current System
Performance measured using **existing review process** with location-neutral application.

### 4.2 Current Process Modifications
- **Quarterly reviews:** Include one question about remote/office work satisfaction
- **Manager training:** 2-hour session on managing hybrid teams (one-time)
- **Peer feedback:** Use existing informal feedback culture, no new systems

*Fixes Problem 4: Uses existing processes rather than implementing complex new systems*

### 4.3 Simple Monitoring
- **Monthly check:** Managers confirm each person met minimum office requirements
- **No additional metrics or tracking systems**

*Fixes Problem 4: Eliminates unmeasurable performance criteria*

---

## 5. COMMUNICATION COORDINATION

### 5.1 Timezone Approach
- **US operations:** Normal business hours (no changes)
- **EU employees (3 people):** Join critical meetings during 10 AM - 2 PM Pacific overlap
- **Async updates:** Use existing Slack for non-urgent communication

*Fixes Problem 2: Realistic timezone expectations*

### 5.2 EU Employee Integration
- **Monthly team video calls:** 1-hour department meetings during overlap hours
- **No travel requirements or budget**
- **Quarterly in-person option:** EU employees can expense one US visit per quarter if they choose (up to $2,000 per person), but not required

*Fixes Problem 6: Makes travel optional and reduces budget to $24K maximum*

---

## 6. IMPLEMENTATION

### 6.1 Rollout Timeline
- **Week 1:** Announce policy and team day schedules
- **Week 2:** Manager training session (2 hours)
- **Week 3:** Begin team day schedule
- **Month 1:** Order equipment for remote employees
- **Month 2:** Full implementation review

*Fixes Problem 8: Realistic timeline using existing resources*

### 6.2 Simple Monitoring
- **Monthly:** Department managers confirm attendance at team days
- **Quarterly:** CEO reviews policy effectiveness with department managers
- **No surveys, metrics tracking, or complex measurement systems**

*Fixes Problem 5: Eliminates non-existent governance systems*

### 6.3 Exception Process
- **Temporary changes:** Department managers can modify team day schedules with 1-week notice to affected employees
- **Individual needs:** Case-by-case discussion with direct manager
- **No formal escalation process required**

*Fixes Problem 5: Simple exception handling*
