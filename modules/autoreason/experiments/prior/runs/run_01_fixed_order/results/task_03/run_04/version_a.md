# HYBRID WORK POLICY
## Effective Date: [Date] | Version 1.0

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes a flexible hybrid framework that respects engineering's remote-first preference while addressing sales' collaboration needs. The policy emphasizes outcomes over location, provides clear structure for coordination, and ensures equitable treatment across all time zones.

---

## 1. REQUIRED IN-OFFICE PRESENCE

### 1.1 Department-Specific Requirements

**Engineering Team (20 people)**
- **Minimum requirement:** 1 day per month in office
- **Recommended:** 2-3 days per quarter for team building and architecture sessions
- **Flexibility:** Can work 100% remote with manager approval for specific projects or personal circumstances

**Sales Team (15 people)**
- **Minimum requirement:** 3 days per week in office (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday)
- **Flexibility:** 1 additional day per week at manager's discretion based on client needs
- **Remote allowance:** Up to 2 full remote weeks per quarter with advance approval

**Leadership/Other Functions (15 people)**
- **Minimum requirement:** 2 days per week in office
- **Core days:** Wednesday (all-hands alignment) + 1 flexible day
- **Department heads must be present during their team's required office days**

### 1.2 Core Office Hours
- **Primary office hours:** 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM local time
- **Overlap requirement:** All employees must be available (in-office or remote) during 12:00 PM - 3:00 PM EST for cross-team collaboration

### 1.3 Exceptions and Accommodations
- Medical accommodations reviewed case-by-case
- Temporary remote work (1-4 weeks) requires manager approval
- Extended remote work (1+ months) requires CEO approval
- New hires: First 30 days include minimum 2 office days per week regardless of department

---

## 2. MEETING NORMS AND COLLABORATION STANDARDS

### 2.1 Meeting Equity Standards
- **Default to hybrid:** All meetings accommodate both in-person and remote participants
- **Technology requirement:** Every conference room equipped with 360-degree camera, quality microphones, and large displays
- **No sidebar conversations** during hybrid meetings
- **Remote-first facilitation:** Meeting leaders must actively engage remote participants

### 2.2 Meeting Types and Protocols

**All-Hands Meetings**
- **Frequency:** Monthly, first Wednesday
- **Format:** Hybrid with CEO in office
- **Recording:** All meetings recorded and accessible within 24 hours
- **EU accommodation:** Alternate between 9:00 AM EST and 12:00 PM EST start times

**Department Meetings**
- **Engineering:** Bi-weekly, async-first with optional sync component
- **Sales:** Weekly, Tuesdays in office with dial-in option
- **Leadership:** Weekly, Wednesdays in office

**Cross-Functional Collaboration**
- **Project kickoffs:** In-person preferred, hybrid acceptable
- **Sprint planning:** Engineering's choice of format
- **Sales-Engineering handoffs:** Hybrid mandatory
- **Client meetings:** Sales team's discretion, Engineering dials in as needed

### 2.3 Communication Protocols
- **Urgent matters (< 4 hours):** Slack + phone call
- **Standard requests (same day):** Slack
- **Non-urgent updates:** Email or project management tools
- **Documentation requirement:** All decisions documented in shared systems within 24 hours

## 3. EQUIPMENT AND TECHNOLOGY POLICY

### 3.1 Standard Equipment Allocation

**All Employees Receive:**
- Laptop (MacBook Pro 14" or equivalent Windows machine)
- External monitor (27" minimum)
- Wireless keyboard and mouse
- Noise-canceling headphones
- Webcam (if laptop camera insufficient)
- Desk chair (up to $400 reimbursement)

**Additional Remote Worker Stipend:**
- **Monthly:** $75 internet reimbursement
- **One-time:** $500 home office setup allowance
- **Annual:** $200 co-working space allowance

### 3.2 Office Equipment Standards
- **Hot-desking system:** Reservable desks through office management platform
- **Dedicated spaces:** Sales team members can reserve permanent desks
- **Collaboration spaces:** 4 bookable conference rooms with hybrid technology
- **Phone booths:** 6 private spaces for confidential calls

### 3.3 Security and IT Support
- **VPN mandatory** for all remote work
- **Security software** auto-installed and monitored
- **IT support hours:** 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM EST, emergency on-call available
- **Equipment refresh cycle:** 3 years for laptops, 5 years for peripherals
- **Damage/loss policy:** Employee responsible for first $200, company covers remainder

## 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION APPROACH

### 4.1 Core Evaluation Principles
- **Outcome-based assessment:** Focus on results and deliverables, not location or hours
- **Equal treatment:** Remote and in-office work weighted equally in evaluations
- **Visibility bias prevention:** Managers trained to recognize and counteract in-person preference

### 4.2 Performance Metrics Framework

**Engineering Team:**
- Code quality and delivery metrics (70%)
- Collaboration and communication effectiveness (20%)
- Technical leadership and mentoring (10%)
- **Location neutrality:** No bonus points for office presence

**Sales Team:**
- Revenue targets and pipeline development (80%)
- Team collaboration and knowledge sharing (15%)
- Client relationship quality (5%)
- **Office presence impact:** Measured through team collaboration scores, not attendance

**All Teams:**
- Quarterly OKR achievement
- 360-degree feedback including remote collaboration skills
- Professional development goal completion

### 4.3 Evaluation Process
- **Frequency:** Quarterly check-ins, annual comprehensive reviews
- **Documentation requirement:** Managers must document specific examples of both remote and in-person contributions
- **Calibration sessions:** Leadership team reviews evaluations to ensure location bias doesn't influence ratings
- **Employee self-assessment:** Includes section on hybrid work effectiveness and preferences

### 4.4 Career Development
- **Promotion criteria:** Location-agnostic advancement opportunities
- **Leadership development:** Hybrid leadership training for all managers
- **Mentorship program:** Pairs remote and in-office employees across departments
- **Conference attendance:** Equal budget allocation regardless of work location preference

## 5. TIMEZONE COORDINATION STRATEGY

### 5.1 Timezone Coverage Plan

**Core Business Hours by Region:**
- **US East Coast:** 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM EST
- **US West Coast:** 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM PST  
- **EU Team (3 people):** 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM CET

**Daily Overlap Windows:**
- **US East/West overlap:** 12:00 PM - 2:00 PM EST (9:00 AM - 11:00 AM PST)
- **US/EU overlap:** 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM EST (3:00 PM - 5:00 PM CET)

### 5.2 EU Team Integration
- **Primary reporting:** EU team reports to US-based managers with weekly 1:1s during overlap hours
- **Meeting participation:** 50% of meetings scheduled during US/EU overlap, 50% recorded for async consumption
- **Project handoffs:** Structured documentation requirements for cross-timezone collaboration
- **EU representative:** One EU team member designated as regional coordinator for local concerns

### 5.3 Asynchronous Work Protocols
- **Daily standups:** Async updates in Slack by 10:00 AM local time
- **Code reviews:** 24-hour response time expectation
- **Decision documentation:** All decisions recorded with context for timezone-delayed team members
- **Project management:** All tasks and updates logged in shared systems with timezone-aware notifications

### 5.4 Special Accommodations
- **EU quarterly visits:** Company-sponsored trips to US office once per quarter
- **Rotating meeting times:** Monthly rotation of recurring meeting times to share inconvenient hours
- **EU-friendly events:** Quarterly virtual team building during EU-friendly hours
- **Holiday coordination:** Shared calendar of regional holidays with coverage planning
