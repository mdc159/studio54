## CRITICAL PROBLEMS WITH THIS HYBRID WORK POLICY

### 1. FUNDAMENTALLY BROKEN OFFICE REQUIREMENTS

**Engineering's "2 anchor days per month" is unenforceable**
- No mechanism to track or verify compliance with monthly minimums
- "Banking days" creates accounting nightmare - who tracks carried-over days across months?
- Vague "recommended" days during sprints means engineers will ignore them entirely
- Project leads requesting "additional office time" will create constant friction and negotiation

**Sales 3-day requirement conflicts with travel reality**
- "Travel weeks count as office time" makes the policy meaningless - sales teams travel 30-40% of the time
- Required Tuesday/Thursday creates scheduling conflicts with client meetings that can't be moved
- No definition of what constitutes "travel week" - one day trip? Multi-day conference?

### 2. MEETING FRAMEWORK IS OPERATIONALLY IMPOSSIBLE

**"All meetings hybrid-enabled by default" is a resource nightmare**
- Every conference room needs expensive A/V setup ($10K+ per room for quality hybrid)
- IT support burden will be massive - hybrid meetings fail constantly and need technical troubleshooting
- "All participants join via video even if in same office" will be ignored immediately - people won't video call the person sitting next to them

**Timezone constraints are unrealistic**
- "No meetings outside 9 AM - 3 PM Pacific" eliminates 6 hours of potential collaboration time
- EU employees "join 7 AM - 11 AM Pacific when critical" - who decides what's critical? EU staff will be excluded from important decisions made outside these windows

### 3. EQUIPMENT POLICY CREATES MASSIVE OPERATIONAL BURDEN

**$3,500 equipment budget per person = $175K upfront cost**
- No consideration of cash flow impact for 50-person company
- "Refresh every 3 years" creates ongoing $58K annual expense not budgeted
- EU shipping costs and local procurement creates complex procurement process requiring dedicated resources

**Hot-desking setup assumption is wrong**
- People will claim "permanent" desks within days, making hot-desking equipment unused
- Docking stations break constantly and create daily IT tickets
- No desk booking system mentioned in implementation but required for equipment policy to work

### 4. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION IS MEASUREMENT-IMPOSSIBLE

**"Outcome-based assessment" has no actual measurement system**
- OKRs aren't mentioned as existing at company - implementing OKR system is 6-month project by itself
- "Peer feedback on communication effectiveness" - no system, process, or criteria defined
- "Cross-location peer reviews" assumes people know each other's work across locations

**Department-specific metrics are fantasy**
- "Client relationship strength measured via surveys" - no survey system exists, clients won't respond
- "Technical mentorship and documentation" - no way to measure mentorship quality or documentation value
- Bias training "quarterly" for all managers = significant ongoing training cost and time

### 5. GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE DOESN'T EXIST

**"Department leads can request temporary modifications"**
- No department leads defined in org structure provided
- No process for evaluating requests or criteria for approval
- Creates immediate political battles over who gets exceptions

**Monitoring metrics require systems that don't exist**
- "Office utilization rates" - no badge system or tracking mentioned
- "Meeting effectiveness scores" - no survey system in place
- "Performance metric consistency" - no baseline performance metrics defined

### 6. FINANCIAL ASSUMPTIONS ARE UNSUPPORTED

**EU quarterly travel budget is $30K annually**
- $2,500 × 3 people × 4 quarters = $30K not budgeted anywhere
- Assumes EU employees want to travel quarterly (they may not)
- No consideration of visa requirements for frequent US travel

**Monthly home office stipend is $90K annually**
- $150 × 50 people × 12 months = $90K ongoing expense
- Plus $50K one-time setup costs ($1,000 × 50 people)
- No tax implications considered - stipends may be taxable income

### 7. CORE BUSINESS ASSUMPTIONS ARE WRONG

**"Sales needs collaboration" vs "3 days in office" mismatch**
- Sales collaboration happens with clients, not internal team
- Forcing internal office time may reduce client face time and hurt revenue
- Tuesday/Thursday requirements conflict with client availability patterns

**Engineering "remote-first preferences" ignored by equipment policy**
- Providing expensive office equipment suggests expectation of office use
- Home office stipend is lower priority than office setup, contradicting "remote-first"

### 8. IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE IS FANTASY

**"Month 1: Policy communication and manager training"**
- No training curriculum exists
- No communication plan beyond "announce in Slack"
- Managers need extensive training on hybrid management - not achievable in 30 days

**"Month 2: Equipment procurement and office setup"**
- $175K equipment purchase requires vendor negotiations, delivery coordination
- Office A/V setup requires space planning, installation, testing - 60+ days minimum
- No consideration of supply chain delays

The policy attempts to solve too many problems simultaneously without considering implementation complexity, existing systems, or resource requirements. Most provisions will be ignored or create operational chaos within weeks of implementation.
