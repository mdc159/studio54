# Go-to-Market Strategy: Kubernetes CLI Configuration Tool

## Executive Summary

With 5k GitHub stars and zero revenue, your Kubernetes CLI tool has proven product-market fit within the developer community. This strategy focuses on converting that organic adoption into sustainable revenue through a freemium SaaS model, targeting DevOps engineers at mid-market companies while maintaining the open-source core that drives growth.

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary: Mid-Market DevOps Teams (50-500 employees)
- **Profile**: Companies with 5-20 Kubernetes clusters, 3-15 DevOps engineers
- **Pain Points**: Configuration drift, compliance auditing, team collaboration on configs
- **Budget Authority**: DevOps leads/Engineering managers with $10K-50K annual tool budgets
- **Examples**: Series A/B SaaS companies, digital agencies, fintech startups

### Secondary: Platform Engineering Teams at Enterprise
- **Profile**: Large companies building internal developer platforms
- **Pain Points**: Standardization across teams, governance, security compliance
- **Budget Authority**: Platform/Infrastructure VPs with $100K+ budgets
- **Examples**: Fortune 1000 with mature DevOps practices

### Tertiary: Individual Contributors (Freemium Base)
- **Profile**: Senior DevOps engineers, Kubernetes consultants
- **Value**: Personal productivity, learning, proof-of-concept
- **Conversion Path**: Influence purchasing decisions at their companies

## Pricing Model

### Freemium SaaS Structure

**Free Tier (Open Source CLI + Basic Cloud)**
- Unlimited local usage of CLI tool
- Cloud dashboard for up to 3 clusters
- Basic configuration validation
- Community support via GitHub/Discord

**Professional ($49/user/month)**
- Unlimited clusters
- Team collaboration features (shared configs, comments)
- Advanced validation rules and policy enforcement
- Git integration with PR workflows
- Email support with 24-hour response SLA

**Enterprise ($149/user/month)**
- SSO/SAML integration
- Audit logs and compliance reporting
- Custom validation rules
- Priority support with dedicated Slack channel
- Professional services credits (implementation support)

### Rationale
- Freemium preserves open-source growth engine
- Per-user pricing scales with team size and value delivered
- Price points target mid-market sweet spot while leaving room for enterprise expansion

## What We Explicitly Won't Do (Yet)

### Geographic Expansion
- **Avoid**: International sales teams, localized marketing
- **Rationale**: Focus on English-speaking markets first; international expansion requires significant resources for compliance, support, and localization

### Adjacent Product Categories
- **Avoid**: General infrastructure-as-code, monitoring, or security tools
- **Rationale**: Stay focused on Kubernetes configuration management expertise; adjacent markets would dilute limited resources and confuse positioning

### Large Enterprise Direct Sales
- **Avoid**: Field sales team, enterprise account executives
- **Rationale**: 3-person team cannot support complex enterprise sales cycles; focus on product-led growth and inside sales for now

### Custom Professional Services
- **Avoid**: Implementation services, custom development
- **Rationale**: Services don't scale with small team; would distract from product development and create support burden

### Multiple Pricing Tiers
- **Avoid**: Complex pricing with many tiers or usage-based models
- **Rationale**: Simple pricing reduces buyer friction and sales complexity; can optimize later with more data

### Multi-Cloud Platform Support
- **Avoid**: Native integrations with AWS EKS, Azure AKS, GCP GKE dashboards
- **Rationale**: Focus on Kubernetes-agnostic value proposition first; cloud-specific features can come later
